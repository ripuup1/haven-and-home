You don't need to redecorate your entire living room to make it feel like spring. A few intentional swaps in textures, colors, and scents can shift the whole energy of a room in about an hour. Heavier throws get replaced with lighter ones. Dark pillow covers swap for softer tones. A winter candle gives way to something fresh and green.

The goal is under $50 total. Not $50 per item. Here are six swaps that make the biggest impact for the smallest investment.

## What Faux Greenery Looks Real?

**Faux eucalyptus stems ($24 for a set of 6) are the most realistic-looking artificial greenery. Place them in a ceramic vase on your coffee table or mantel for low-maintenance spring freshness that lasts all season.**

Real eucalyptus dries out and drops leaves within a couple of weeks. Good faux eucalyptus looks identical and lasts forever. These stems have realistic coloring with slight variations in the green tones, which is what separates good faux greenery from the obvious plastic kind. Arrange six stems in a vase on your coffee table, mantel, or bookshelf for instant spring energy. They look beautiful on [floating shelves](/blog/floating-shelves-hold-weight) paired with a candle and a small framed print. No water, no maintenance, no dead leaves on your floor.

## What Candle Scent Says Spring?

**A sage and citrus candle ($20) is the perfect spring transition scent. It's fresh and herbal without being overpowering. It bridges the gap between the warm winter scents you're retiring and the light summer scents that aren't quite right yet.**

Swapping your candle is the most underrated seasonal refresh. Your nose adapts to scents you've been burning for months, so you've probably stopped noticing your winter candle entirely. A spring candle should smell green and fresh, not floral or fruity. Sage and citrus hits that sweet spot. It's herbal enough to feel grounding but bright enough to feel new. ## Frequently Asked Questions

### How do you refresh a living room for spring on a budget?

The cheapest spring refresh is swapping pillow covers ($16 for 2), adding faux greenery ($24), and lighting a fresh candle ($20). That's $60 for three changes that affect how the room looks, smells, and feels. For a more complete refresh under $130, add a lightweight throw blanket, a ceramic vase, and sheer curtains.

### What is the best faux greenery that looks real?

Faux eucalyptus stems are the most realistic-looking artificial greenery. Look for stems with color variation in the leaves (not one uniform shade of green) and bendable stems that you can arrange naturally. A set of 6 stems ($24) fills a standard vase and lasts indefinitely.

### When should you swap decor from winter to spring?

Swap your decor when you start opening windows for fresh air, usually mid-March through April. Replace heavy throws with lightweight ones, swap dark pillow covers for lighter tones, and switch winter candle scents for something fresh and herbal. The transition should feel natural, not forced.
