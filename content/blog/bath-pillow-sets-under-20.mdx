The good news is you do not need to spend $40 to get a good one. If you have been putting off upgrading your bath routine, these are the ones worth trying.

## What Makes a Bath Pillow Actually Worth Using?

**The suction cups are everything.** A pillow that slides or peels off mid-soak defeats the whole purpose. Look for at least four suction cups, ideally positioned at the corners, and a design that works on both flat and curved tub walls.

Beyond that, you want quick-drying mesh fabric — anything that holds moisture is going to mildew faster than you expect. A hanging loop for drying between uses is a small detail that makes a real difference in longevity. These seven picks hit all the right notes at prices that make them very easy to justify.

## Quick Tips for Getting the Most from Your Bath Pillow

- Wet the suction cups before attaching to the tub wall — they grip significantly better when damp
- Rinse the pillow after each use and hang it to dry; do not leave it sitting in standing water
- If suction weakens over time, clean the cups with a little rubbing alcohol to restore grip
- Look for tubs that are clean and dry before attaching for maximum hold
- Check that the pillow positioning works before filling the tub fully — minor adjustments are easier before the water is running
