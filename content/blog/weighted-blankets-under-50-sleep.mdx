If you haven't tried a weighted blanket yet, I need you to understand something: the hype is real. I was skeptical for years. "A heavy blanket will help me sleep better? Sure." Translation: you fall asleep faster and stay asleep longer.

## How Heavy Should a Weighted Blanket Be?

**The general rule is 10% of your body weight, give or take a pound or two. A 15-Pound Cooling Weighted Blanket ($38, 4.6 stars, 24,300+ reviews) is the most popular weight because it works for adults between 130-170 pounds -- which covers a huge range of people. If you're between sizes, go lighter rather than heavier.**

Getting the weight right is the most important decision, and it's simpler than you think. Take your body weight and divide by 10. That's your target blanket weight. So a 150-pound person would use a 15-pound blanket. If you're between standard sizes (12, 15, 17, 20 lbs), round down -- a blanket that's slightly too light still feels wonderful, but one that's too heavy can feel claustrophobic.

## Quick Tips for Weighted Blanket Success

- **Start with 10% of your body weight** and adjust from there -- it's easier to add weight (by going up a size later) than to deal with a blanket that feels too heavy
- **Use your weighted blanket on top of your regular bedding**, not underneath -- it should be the last layer so the weight presses directly down on you
- **Give it 3-5 nights** before deciding if you like it -- your body needs time to adjust to the new sensation, and the first night might feel unfamiliar
- **Don't cover your entire bed** -- weighted blankets are meant to cover your body only, not hang over the edges like a comforter (that's why they're smaller than standard bedding)
- **Machine wash on gentle, cold water** and tumble dry on low -- or better yet, get one with a removable cover and just wash the cover
- **Keep it personal** -- weighted blankets are sized for one person, not couples, so each person should have their own in their preferred weight

A weighted blanket isn't going to cure insomnia overnight. But it consistently helps people fall asleep faster, wake up less during the night, and feel more rested in the morning. At under $50, it's one of the cheapest sleep upgrades you can make -- and unlike that $2,000 mattress, you can return it if it's not for you.
