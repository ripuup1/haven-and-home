## Do They Make Rotating Organizers for Brushes Specifically?

**Yes — a dedicated rotating brush holder keeps your brushes upright and separated, which both preserves the bristles and makes it faster to grab the right one. ## Quick Tips for Organizing a Tiny Vanity

- **Group by use, not by type.** Keep your daily routine products on the top tier and less-used items on lower shelves so your most-reached-for products are always within easy reach.
- **Decant products you use daily.** Transferring cotton swabs and bobby pins into small clear cups that sit alongside your rotating organizer creates a cohesive, uncluttered look.
- **Use a lazy Susan for overflow.** If your rotating organizer fills up, a larger lazy Susan in a cabinet below your vanity can hold backup products, keeping your counter clear.
- **Measure before you buy.** Most rotating organizers have a base footprint of 6 to 8 in. Check your vanity space first — many tiny vanities can only comfortably fit one unit without feeling cramped.
- **Clean the base regularly.** Makeup dust and product residue collect on rotating bases over time, which eventually makes the spin feel sticky. A quick wipe with a damp cloth monthly keeps it spinning smoothly.
