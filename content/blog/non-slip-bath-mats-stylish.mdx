The bath mat is the first thing you step on when you get out of the shower. It should feel good underfoot, keep you from slipping, dry quickly so it doesn't smell like a wet dog, and not look like something from a hospital supply catalog. The problem is that most "non-slip" mats are fine on the safety side but terrible on everything else — they bunch up, develop that rubber-smell, and turn gray after five washes.

These six bath mats are the ones that consistently solve all of those problems at once. All of them are under $35, and all of them have thousands of reviews from people who cared enough to report back.

## Quick Tips for Choosing and Caring for Bath Mats

- Wash fabric bath mats every 1 to 2 weeks to prevent mildew — most are machine washable, just check the label first.
- Air dry or tumble on low rather than high heat, which degrades foam and rubber backing faster.
- For stone and teak mats, a weekly rinse and monthly sun-dry is all the maintenance they need.
- If your mat slides on polished tile, add a rug grip pad underneath — it works better than the mat's own backing on super-smooth surfaces.
