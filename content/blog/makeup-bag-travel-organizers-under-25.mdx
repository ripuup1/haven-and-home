If you've ever stood over a hotel bathroom counter staring at a pile of products that tumbled out of your makeup bag, you already understand why a well-organized travel cosmetic case is worth every dollar. The right bag keeps your brushes from getting crushed, your liquids from leaking on everything, and your eyeshadow from shattering in transit.

The good news is you don't need to spend $80 on a Béis case to solve this problem. There are travel makeup organizers under $25 that work just as well for most people — compartments in the right places, waterproof lining, sturdy zippers. I've sorted through hundreds of reviews to find the ones that actually live up to their photos.

## What Actually Matters in a Travel Makeup Bag

**The zipper quality is everything.** Cheap bags fail at the zipper, usually within three or four trips. Look for reviews that specifically mention the zipper holding up over time, and avoid bags where people mention broken zippers in the first month.

Beyond that, think about how you actually pack: Do you need brush holders? A dedicated pocket for liquids? A mirror inside? Compartments that open flat so you can see everything at once? The "best" bag is the one that matches your specific routine — there's no universal answer.

## Quick Tips for Packing a Travel Makeup Bag

- Put liquid products (foundation, toner, serum) in a dedicated zip-lock or TSA bag even if they're in a cosmetics bag — airline pressure can cause leaks
- Roll brushes handle-first in a cloth or wrap them in a face cloth to protect bristles
- Leave your "nice" palettes home; travel with pressed versions or decant into a smaller palette
- Check the bag's interior measurements before buying — "large" means different things to different brands
