If you have a small living room, you already know the struggle: a big square coffee table takes over the whole space, makes it hard to walk around, and just feels clunky no matter what you put on it. Round coffee tables are genuinely the better solution — no sharp corners to bang your shins on, they flow better in tight layouts, and there's something about a circle that makes a room feel more polished and intentional.

The good news is that you don't have to spend $300 to get a table that looks like it came from a boutique. These eight picks are all under $150, most are under $100, and they cover the full range of styles — minimalist black, warm walnut, white marble-look, and rustic farmhouse. Any of them will anchor your living room without eating it alive.

## What to Look for in a Round Coffee Table for Small Spaces

**Aim for a diameter between 24 and 36 inches — anything larger will crowd a small sofa arrangement.**

For a standard small sectional or loveseat, a 24-inch table leaves breathing room while still giving you enough surface for drinks and a candle or two. If your seating is a full three-seat sofa, you can go up to 32 inches comfortably. Also think about leg style: thin metal legs keep a table feeling light and airy, while chunky wood bases can make a small room feel heavier.

## Quick Tips for Styling a Round Coffee Table

- Use a tray on top to corral smaller items (candle, remote, coaster set) and make the table look intentional
- Layer heights: pair a low bowl or dish with a slightly taller object like a stack of books
- Round tables look best with at least 12 to 18 inches of clearance between the edge and your sofa — any closer and it starts to feel cramped
- If your rug is rectangular, a round table over it creates a nice tension that actually works beautifully
