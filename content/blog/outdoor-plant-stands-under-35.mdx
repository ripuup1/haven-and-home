## Quick Tips for Porch Plant Display

- **Think in odd numbers.** Groups of three or five plants look more intentional and visually balanced than even numbers. Fill your plant stand with groups of three at each tier.
- **Vary heights, not just plants.** Use the tiers purposefully: trailing plants at the top, medium bushy plants in the middle, low groundcover types at the bottom.
- **Add a statement plant beside the stand.** One large floor plant next to the stand — a bird of paradise, a tall ornamental grass — gives the whole arrangement an anchor.
- **Use terracotta for outdoor style.** Terracotta pots on a metal plant stand is a classic combination that always looks right. The warm orange of terracotta contrasts beautifully with dark metal finishes.
- **Rotate your plants weekly.** Even on a covered porch, plants on stands can develop uneven growth toward the light. A weekly quarter-turn keeps them growing evenly and looking full.
