The problem is that real olive trees are finicky — they need full sun, consistent watering, and the right temperature range. Most of them slowly die indoors and the guilt is real.

Enter the faux olive tree. ## Quick Tips for Making Faux Olive Trees Look Real

- Always reshape branches after unpacking. This is not optional — a faux tree straight from the box looks flat and fake. Spend 15 minutes spreading every branch outward.
- Replace the basic pot it comes with. A wicker basket, a terracotta pot, or a linen-wrapped planter elevates the whole look immediately.
- Add a small amount of real soil or a layer of moss on top of the pot to complete the illusion. Fake bark chips also work well.
- Dust the leaves occasionally with a damp cloth. Dusty faux leaves are the biggest giveaway that a plant is not real.
- Position faux trees near windows for the best light, even though they do not need it. Natural light makes the silk leaves look more alive and adds shadows that improve the realistic effect.
