## What Is the Best Countertop Mug Tree?

**A good countertop mug tree is a small piece of kitchen decor that happens to be incredibly functional.** The classic design has a central pole with branches that hold mugs by their handles. ## Can You Store Mugs Under the Cabinet?

**Under-cabinet mug hooks are one of the most space-efficient solutions available.** They mount underneath your upper cabinets using screws or adhesive, and mugs hang from their handles. The result is that you have freed up an entire cabinet shelf while keeping your mugs in easy reach.

The wire rack versions are the most practical because they hold multiple mugs in a row, are more stable than individual hooks, and can often be extended by adding sections. They work best for mugs with standard D-shaped handles, which is most mugs. The install takes about five minutes and does not require any particular handyman skill.

## Quick Tips for Organizing Your Mug Collection

- **Edit before you organize.** The best mug organization starts with a realistic count. If you have not used a mug in a year, it is time to let it go.
- **Group by size.** Keeping espresso cups, standard mugs, and large mugs in separate areas makes grabbing the right one faster and prevents awkward stacking.
- **Under-cabinet hooks are invisible from eye level.** If you hate countertop clutter but also hate digging through cabinets, under-cabinet hooks are the solution you have been looking for.
- **A carousel pays off at two-plus people.** In a household where multiple people make coffee at different times, a spinning carousel with each person's mugs in their section is more practical than it sounds.
- **Handle-out storage prevents chips.** Storing mugs handle-out rather than stacked rim-to-rim eliminates the most common cause of chipped mugs.

Your morning ritual should not start with an avalanche. One of these organizers will make the cabinet or countertop feel manageable, and you will notice the difference every single day.
