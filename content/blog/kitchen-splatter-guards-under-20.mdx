## Quick Tips for Getting the Most Out of Your Splatter Guard

- **Match the size to your pan.** A screen that's the same diameter as your pan's opening works best — bigger is slightly okay, smaller leaves gaps.
- **Use it for stovetop popcorn.** A splatter guard over a pot with a bit of oil and kernels catches any flying pieces while letting steam escape — makes a huge difference.
- **Store it vertically.** Most slide neatly between sheet pans or in a slim cabinet gap upright. Much easier to grab than hunting through a drawer.
- **Don't skip it for pasta sauce.** Tomato-based sauces splatter aggressively and leave stains. A mesh screen over a simmering pot saves a lot of stovetop scrubbing.
- **Check for fine mesh vs. coarse.** Cheap screens have large holes that let small grease droplets through. Look for "fine mesh" in the description — it's worth the extra dollar or two.
