Gallery walls used to mean framed prints, matted photos, and a lot of hammer holes. But over the last few years, something much warmer and more interesting has taken over: woven wall basket collections. They bring texture, dimension, and a handcrafted feel that no printed canvas can replicate — and the best part is they work in almost any style of living room, from modern farmhouse to eclectic boho to clean Scandinavian.

The key is grouping. A single basket on a wall can look like an afterthought, but a thoughtful cluster of three, five, or seven baskets at different sizes creates something that genuinely reads as intentional art. The trick is mixing sizes, textures, and shapes while keeping the tones within a cohesive palette — natural wheat, warm brown, sandy beige, and the occasional dark wicker accent.

Below are six of the best sets and individual options on Amazon for building your own woven wall art arrangement. Each one is made from natural or natural-look materials and comes ready to hang.

## Why Woven Baskets Beat Framed Art on a Boho Wall

**Woven wall baskets add something framed art simply cannot: tactile texture and three-dimensional depth.** When light hits a wicker or rattan basket, it creates subtle shadows that shift throughout the day, making the wall feel alive in a way that flat art never does.

They are also incredibly forgiving to hang. Because each basket is typically a standalone piece (even in sets), you can adjust the spacing and arrangement after hanging without it looking wrong. Try doing that with a gallery wall of frames.

## Building Your Arrangement: The Rule of Odd Numbers

**When hanging multiple baskets, always work with odd numbers — three, five, or seven.** An arrangement of four or six tends to feel stiff and symmetrical in a way that looks more like a grid than art. Odd numbers feel more organic and visually dynamic.

Start with your largest basket as the anchor at the center-top of the arrangement, then work outward and downward with progressively smaller pieces. Leave about four to eight inches of wall space between each basket — enough to breathe but close enough to read as a cohesive grouping.

## Quick Tips for Styling Woven Wall Baskets

- Use a paper template (trace each basket on paper and tape it to the wall) before driving a single nail — this saves a lot of patching
- Mix at least two different weave textures in your arrangement for visual interest
- Add a small pothos or trailing plant in one of the larger baskets for a living-wall moment
- Baskets in odd multiples of three read best from a seated position, so hang your anchor basket slightly lower than you think — eye level from a sofa, not eye level standing
