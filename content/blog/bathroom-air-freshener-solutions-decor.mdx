## Quick Tips for Getting the Most From Bathroom Air Fresheners

- **Rotate your reed diffuser sticks weekly.** Flipping the reeds exposes a fresh dry end to the oil, which restores fragrance intensity. This simple step doubles the effective lifespan of most diffusers.
- **Layer scents intentionally.** A subtle sachet in the cabinet plus a reed diffuser on the shelf creates depth. Two competing strong fragrances cancel each other out. Keep one gentle, one more present.
- **Place diffusers near the door, not the window.** Air movement carries fragrance into the room. Near a window or vent, the scent exhausts quickly. Near the door, it greets everyone who enters.
- **Clean spray nozzles monthly.** Automatic dispensers and aerosol cans can clog at the nozzle. A quick wipe with a damp cloth keeps the spray pattern even and prevents dribbling.
- **Refresh sachets with essential oil.** When a sachet fades but you like the bag design, add three to four drops of lavender or eucalyptus essential oil directly to the fabric. It reactivates immediately and lasts another few weeks.

A bathroom that smells good registers as cleaner and more welcoming, regardless of the actual cleanliness. These five solutions handle the fragrance side of that equation while contributing to the look of the room rather than working against it.
