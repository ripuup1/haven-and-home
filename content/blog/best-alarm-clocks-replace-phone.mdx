Your phone is the worst alarm clock you can use. Not because it doesn't wake you up, but because it keeps you scrolling before bed and first thing in the morning. Moving your phone off the nightstand and replacing it with a dedicated alarm clock is one of the simplest sleep improvements you can make. You fall asleep faster without the temptation to check one more thing, and you wake up without immediately diving into emails and notifications.

The trick is finding an alarm clock that makes you genuinely willing to leave your phone in another room. Here are the best options for a screen-free bedroom.

## What Is the Best Alarm Clock to Replace Your Phone?

**The best alarm clock to replace your phone is a sunrise alarm clock ($35). It wakes you gradually with simulated sunlight over 30 minutes, which is less jarring than a sudden alarm sound. Most sunrise clocks also include nature sounds and a bedside lamp function, so they replace multiple nightstand items at once.**

A sunrise alarm is especially valuable in winter or if your bedroom is dark in the morning. The gradual light mimics a natural sunrise and triggers your body's wake-up hormones before the alarm sound even starts. Many people find they wake up naturally from the light alone, which means no more jolting awake to a blaring alarm.

## How to Transition to a Phone-Free Nightstand

The transition is easier than you think. Start by buying your alarm clock and using it alongside your phone for one week. Once you trust it to wake you up, move your phone to a drawer or another room. Charge it in the kitchen overnight. Most people adjust within three nights and report better sleep almost immediately.

If you need your phone for emergencies, keep it in the room but in a drawer or face-down across the room. The goal isn't to be unreachable. It's to remove the temptation of a glowing screen within arm's reach during the hours you should be sleeping. For [cozy bedroom lighting](/blog/cozy-bedroom-lighting-under-30) that helps wind down, swap your phone scrolling for a warm bedside lamp.
