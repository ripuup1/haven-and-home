## Quick Tips for Using Garden Plant Markers Effectively

- Use a paint pen rather than a standard permanent marker for outdoor markers — it is more UV-resistant and will stay legible through an entire growing season without fading
- Include both the plant name and the date planted on each marker — you will be glad to have the date when you are trying to remember how long something has been growing
- For seed trays, also note the variety — not just Tomato but Cherry Tomato San Marzano — because the details matter at transplant time
- Group markers by plant family in a raised bed rather than mixing randomly — it makes rotation planning much simpler in subsequent seasons
- Keep a small container of spare markers near your potting bench so you never go searching for one when you need to label a new planting on the spot
