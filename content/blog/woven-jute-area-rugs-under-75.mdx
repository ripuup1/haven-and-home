## Natural vs. Braided vs. Woven: What's the Difference?

**All three are made from jute fiber — the weave style is what changes the look.**

- **Natural woven** (flat weave) has a grid-like texture, shows the individual fibers, looks most organic
- **Braided** has that classic rope-braid look — more casual, great for farmhouse vibes
- **Chevron or herringbone woven** adds a pattern while keeping the natural fiber feel — slightly more polished

For most living rooms, flat woven or herringbone is the most versatile. Braided works great in entryways, dining rooms, and spaces where you want something more casual.

## Quick Tips for Jute Rugs

- Always use a rug pad — jute doesn't grip hardwood well on its own, and a pad extends its life significantly
- Vacuum with a brushless attachment; beater bars can damage the fibers
- Jute absorbs moisture, so avoid humid rooms like bathrooms
- Spot clean with a barely-damp cloth and mild soap; never soak it
