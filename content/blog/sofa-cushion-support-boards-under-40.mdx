There is a particular kind of frustration that comes from settling into your living room couch — the one you spent real money on — and feeling yourself sink into a pit where the cushion used to be. Saggy sofas are not just uncomfortable; they make the whole room feel tired and neglected.

Replacing a sofa can cost anywhere from $500 to several thousand dollars. But here is the thing most people do not know: if the frame of your couch is still in good shape, the sagging is usually a cushion and spring support problem that you can fix for under $40.

A couch cushion support board slides under the cushions and creates a firm, level base that distributes your weight evenly again. These are not a permanent replacement for truly worn-out foam, but for a couch that has lost its springiness before its time, they can genuinely feel like a new piece of furniture.

## Quick Tips for Using a Couch Cushion Support Board

- **Measure your sofa before ordering.** The distance from the back of the seat to the front edge, and the total width across all cushions, determines which size fits. Most three-seat sofas are around 70 in wide and 21 to 22 in deep, but check yours.
- **Remove the cushions first.** Before sliding the board in, take the cushions completely off so you can see exactly where to position the board. It should sit on top of the frame and springs, not wedged in at an angle.
- **The board goes under the cushions, not the entire sofa.** It sits on the sofa frame and the cushions sit on top of the board. You should not feel the board through the cushion — if you do, the cushion itself may be too worn and needs replacement foam as well.
- **Pair with new foam if the cushion filling is also worn.** A support board addresses the sinking-spring problem. If the cushion foam itself has compressed flat, adding a 2 to 3 in foam topper on top of the board will restore both the support and the comfort.
- **Check for sagging springs before buying.** Press down on the sofa frame area under the cushions. If it feels completely hollow and the couch frame is cracked or broken, a support board will not help — that requires a structural repair.
