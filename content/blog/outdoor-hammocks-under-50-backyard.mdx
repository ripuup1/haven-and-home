The good news is that you really don't need to spend a lot. Outdoor hammocks have gotten remarkably affordable while the quality has kept pace, and there are genuinely excellent options under $50 that will give you years of backyard enjoyment. Whether you want a classic rope hammock strung between two trees, a portable set with its own stand, or a cozy hammock chair to hang from a porch beam, the picks below deliver.

These are all rated highly, hold two adults comfortably in the case of the doubles, and are designed specifically for outdoor use. You can have one set up and swaying in your backyard within an afternoon.

**A hammock that comes with its own stand is the most practical choice if you don't have perfectly spaced trees — or if you want flexibility in where you set it up.**

Freestanding hammock sets have gotten much better in recent years. The steel stands are powder-coated for rust resistance, the hammocks themselves are typically made of soft cotton or polyester blends, and the whole thing assembles and disassembles in under 20 minutes.

## Cotton Rope Hammocks: The Classic Experience

**If you have two trees or anchor points at the right distance, a classic cotton rope hammock is the most authentic, breathable, and beautiful option available.**

There's a reason the woven rope hammock has been around for centuries — it works beautifully. The open weave allows airflow, which makes it significantly cooler than a fabric hammock on a warm afternoon. Cotton softens beautifully with each use, and a quality rope hammock properly stored can last a decade or more.

## Quick Tips for Setting Up Your Backyard Hammock

- **Check your tree distance first.** Most double hammocks need anchor points 10 to 15 feet apart. Measure before ordering to make sure you're buying the right length.
- **Invest in good tree straps.** Wide tree straps (1.5 in. or wider) protect bark and are far more adjustable than rope or chain. Many hammocks include them, but if yours doesn't, a $10-$15 set is worth it.
- **Hang it low.** The classic mistake is hanging a hammock too high. Aim to have the lowest point of the sag about 18 in. off the ground — it's safer and easier to get in and out.
- **Bring it in during storms.** Even weather-resistant hammocks last much longer when they're stored or covered during extended rain or wind.
- **Add a pillow.** A basic outdoor throw pillow makes any hammock 10x more comfortable for long afternoon sessions.
