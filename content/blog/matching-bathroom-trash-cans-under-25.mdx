Here's a bathroom styling truth nobody talks about: the trash can is the first thing guests notice. It sits right there on the floor, collecting everything, and most of us are still using whatever plastic bin came with the apartment or was grabbed on a Target run years ago. If you've invested in a pretty soap dispenser, a coordinated towel set, or a stylish vanity tray, an ugly trash can undoes all of it.

The good news is you don't have to spend much to fix this. There are genuinely attractive bathroom waste bins that cost under $25 — ones with lids that actually close, materials that don't look cheap, and shapes that tuck neatly next to a toilet or under a vanity. Whether your bathroom leans modern, farmhouse, or somewhere in between, you'll find a match here.

## What Makes a Bathroom Trash Can Worth Buying?

**You want the right size (small, not large), a lid you'll actually use, and a material that handles humidity without rusting or warping.**

The bathroom is not the kitchen — you don't need a big bin. Something in the 1 to 3 gallon range is perfect, small enough to tuck discreetly but big enough to skip daily emptying. A lid matters more in the bathroom than anywhere else in the house for obvious reasons. And if you're buying metal (which looks more polished), make sure it specifies rust-resistant or stainless steel, because humidity will test a cheap finish fast.

## Quick Tips for Choosing a Bathroom Trash Can

- Match your trash can finish to your existing hardware — black bin with matte black faucet, bronze with brushed gold or oil-rubbed fixtures
- A step pedal is worth it for the main bathroom; a simple open-top bin works fine for a guest bath that sees less traffic
- If you have a small powder room, look for a D-shaped or slim rectangular bin that hugs the wall without eating up floor space
- Removable inner liners make cleaning 100 times easier — worth prioritizing over a pretty exterior if you can only pick one feature
