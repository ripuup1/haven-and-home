## Quick Tips for Outdoor Platter Styling

- **Match platter shapes to what you're serving.** Oval platters work for charcuterie and meats; rectangular trays work for rows of appetizers; round platters work for salads and composed displays.
- **Melamine mimics ceramic better when food is on it.** A plain melamine platter can look plastic when empty but genuinely convincing when filled with colorful food.
- **Use one large platter and several small ones** rather than lining everything up on the same size. The variation in scale makes the table look more intentional.
- **Label what's in the dip.** A small folded card next to the guacamole, the hummus, and the salsa keeps guests from needing to ask and makes the table look more like an event.
- **Refrigerate cold items in the platters before serving.** Melamine is fine in the refrigerator, and starting with a cold surface helps keep food at the right temperature longer outdoors.
