The fastest way to make a bed look styled rather than "just made" is to add two oversized Euro shams — and almost nobody does it. A Euro sham is the 26x26 inch square pillow you see propped at the back of the bed in every hotel and magazine photo. It's the difference between a dorm-room bed and a bed that looks like it came out of a design blog.

## How to Actually Style Them

Two Euro shams go at the back of the bed, propped against the headboard. In front of those, two standard or king pillows (the ones you actually sleep on). In front of those, a smaller decorative lumbar or two square throw pillows. That's the five-pillow setup every styled bed uses and it takes 30 seconds to make the bed look intentional.

Never use four Euro shams unless you have a king bed. On a queen, two is exactly right. On a full, you can get away with two Euros laid on their side, overlapping slightly.

## Quick Tips

- Always buy inserts that are 1 inch larger than your sham cover. A 27-inch insert in a 26-inch sham looks full; a 25-inch insert looks deflated.
- Wash Euro shams inside out to protect the fabric and any ruffle or tassel detail.
- A quick karate chop in the center of the pillow gives it that styled "hotel fold" look. Instagram uses this trick. You can too.
- Skip decorative Euro shams with bold patterns until you have neutral ones down. Solid Euros + patterned standard pillows is the classic combo.
- Don't buy Euro shams that come with inserts. The included inserts are always too small. Buy cover and insert separately.

The oversized sham look is the designer trick that nobody told you about. Under $75 total for the two shams plus inserts is genuinely the biggest visual upgrade you can make to a bed without buying new bedding.
