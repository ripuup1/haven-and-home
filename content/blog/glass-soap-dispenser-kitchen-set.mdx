## How to Make Your Soap Dispensers Look Styled

The soap dispenser set is just the starting point. Place them on a small tray or [countertop organizer](/blog/rental-kitchen-look-expensive) with a small potted herb or a folded dish towel. This groups everything together and makes the whole area feel curated rather than cluttered. Use matching waterproof labels with a clean font, and refill both dispensers at the same time so the levels always look balanced.

For the best look, choose soap colors intentionally. A clear or lightly tinted hand soap paired with a colored dish soap creates a nice contrast in matching dispensers. Avoid dispensers with printed designs or logos. The whole point is a clean, unified look that elevates your countertop soap pump setup without overthinking it.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is the best soap dispenser for a kitchen sink?

The best kitchen soap dispenser is an amber glass set of 2 with matte black pumps ($18). Amber glass hides soap levels, the matte finish resists fingerprints, and a set of 2 gives you a matching pair for dish soap and hand soap. Look for 16 oz capacity or larger to reduce refill frequency.

### How do you keep glass soap dispensers from looking messy?

Place your glass soap dispensers on a small tray to catch drips and keep the area contained. Refill both dispensers at the same time so the soap levels match. Choose soap colors that complement each other, and use waterproof labels with a clean font to identify each one.

### Are glass or plastic soap dispensers better for the kitchen?

Glass soap dispensers are better for the kitchen because they're heavier (so they don't tip over), they don't absorb odors, and they look significantly more polished than plastic. Glass is also easier to clean and won't yellow or cloud over time like plastic dispensers tend to do.
