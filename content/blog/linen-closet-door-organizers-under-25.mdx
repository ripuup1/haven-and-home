Meanwhile, the inside of the closet is probably packed tight with towels, sheets, and toiletries competing for the same three shelves. Adding an over-door organizer turns that wasted door surface into extra storage for all the small items that don't deserve shelf space but still need a home.

They range from pocket-style fabric organizers to wire racks and mesh systems, so there's an option for every closet and every type of item you need to store. None of them require tools or drilling, which means they work in rentals and you can move them if you change your mind.

## Quick Tips

- Check your door thickness before ordering. Most over-door organizers fit standard doors (1.375 to 1.75 inches thick), but some won't work on thicker or thinner doors.
- Place heavier items in the lower pockets or shelves to keep the weight distribution balanced and prevent the organizer from swinging.
- Label shelves or pocket rows by category (first aid, hair care, cleaning) so every family member knows where things go and where to put them back.
