The problem is most vanities are either ugly, overpriced, or both.

Under $100 used to mean a wobbly table with a mirror that barely stayed attached. That's changed. You can get a real vanity desk with built-in lighting, multiple drawers, and a sturdy flip mirror for under $80 if you know where to look. These seven picks actually hold up.

## What Makes a Vanity Desk Worth Buying?

**The three things that separate a good vanity from a frustrating one: stable mirror attachment, real drawer capacity, and a seat height that works with the desk height.**

Most vanities list the desk height but not the leg clearance, which is what determines whether a standard stool or chair actually fits underneath. You want at least 26 inches of clearance from floor to desk bottom for a typical vanity stool. If it's less than that, your knees will hit the drawer bottom and you'll be hunched.

The mirror should tilt and lock, not just balance. And if you're getting an LED-lit mirror, check the reviews specifically for whether the lighting stays bright after 6 months — cheap LED strips dim fast.

## Quick Tips for Bedroom Vanities

- Check the stool or chair seat height against the desk leg clearance before ordering
- Use a small tray or acrylic organizer on the desk surface to keep daily products from spreading everywhere
- If your bedroom gets morning light, position the vanity to face the window for the best natural light
- LED vanity mirrors with dimmer controls are worth the small extra cost — you'll use the dimmer setting at night
- Trifold mirrors show the back of your head, which is genuinely useful for hair styling, not just eye makeup
