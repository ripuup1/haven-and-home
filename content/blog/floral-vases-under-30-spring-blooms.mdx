There is something about fresh flowers in a vase that makes any room feel more intentional and alive. It doesn't matter if you spent twelve dollars on grocery store tulips or cut something from your own garden — the right vase transforms a handful of stems into a proper moment of beauty. And the right vase under $30 is absolutely within reach.

Spring is the best time to build out your vase collection because you're going to be using them constantly. Farmers markets start popping up, peonies and ranunculus hit their peak, and even a five-dollar bunch of grocery store daffodils deserves a good home. From clear glass cylinders to earthy ceramic bud vases, these nine picks cover every style and every stem length you're likely to encounter between now and summer.

## What Vase Shape Works for Which Flowers?

**Tall cylinder vases work beautifully for long-stemmed flowers like tulips, sunflowers, and roses — they provide support so stems don't flop.** Wide-mouth vases suit full, round blooms like peonies and hydrangeas that need room to open up. Small bud vases are perfect for single stems, wildflowers, or that lone sprig of fresh herbs from your garden that you can't bear to chop.

For mixed arrangements, a tapered vase with a narrower neck does the work of holding stems in place so you don't need floral foam. Ceramic vases add warmth and texture — they're great for a dresser or bookshelf moment. Glass lets you see the stems, which adds its own kind of visual interest. The honest answer is: you want a few of each.

## Quick Tips

- Change the water every two days and trim stems at a diagonal for longer-lasting flowers
- A drop of bleach and a teaspoon of sugar in the water can extend the life of cut flowers
- Group odd numbers of vases together on shelves or consoles for a more natural, organic look
- Mix heights and shapes for visual interest — all matching vases can feel a little flat
- Dried flowers and pampas grass in ceramic vases need zero maintenance and look great year-round
