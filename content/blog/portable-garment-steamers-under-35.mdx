Ironing is one of those chores that nobody has time for anymore. Setting up the board, waiting for it to heat up, figuring out which setting works for which fabric, accidentally creating a new crease while trying to fix the old one — it is a whole production for something that should take 30 seconds. A good steamer heats up in under a minute, works on everything from silk to denim, and lets you de-wrinkle clothes while they are still on the hanger. No board, no guessing about temperature settings, no scorched shirts. The best ones are small enough to throw in a suitcase for travel.

Every option on this list is under $35, heats up fast, and gets consistently positive reviews from people who actually use them daily. We paid close attention to steam output, heat-up time, water tank capacity, and whether they spit or drip — because a steamer that leaves water spots defeats the purpose.

## Quick Tips for Getting the Best Results From Your Steamer

- Always use distilled water to prevent mineral buildup that clogs the steam vents over time
- Steam in downward strokes and let gravity help pull wrinkles out — do not press the steamer against the fabric
- For stubborn creases, hold the steamer close and slow down, giving the steam time to penetrate the fibers
- Empty the water tank after each use to prevent standing water from causing scale or mildew
- Start with the lowest setting on delicate fabrics and work up — you can always add more steam but you cannot un-scorch silk
