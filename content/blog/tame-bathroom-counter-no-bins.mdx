If your bathroom counter has slowly become a graveyard of half-empty bottles, free toothbrushes from the dentist, and three different hand soaps, the fix isn't another plastic bin under the sink. Bins hide the problem; they don't fix it. The real move is making the things you actually use every day look like they belong on the counter, and making the things you don't use go somewhere else entirely.

Here are the five bathroom counter problems every small bathroom has, with one specific product that solves each one. None of them are bins.

## What to Skip

- **Plastic bins on the counter.** They make the clutter look more organized but they're still clutter.
- **Drawer dividers if your drawer isn't half empty.** You can't divide a drawer that's already overstuffed; you have to throw out half first.
- **A second hand soap dispenser** "for the kids." One soap, one bathroom. Fewer things equals less to manage.
- **Multiple toothbrush holders.** Get one with four slots if there are four people. Don't get four single ones.

The whole strategy here is replacement, not addition. Every product on this list replaces something already on the counter, which is what actually makes the surface feel less cluttered. You're not adding storage. You're making what's already out look intentional.
