## What to Look for in a Blanket Ladder

**The most important factors are height (look for 5 feet or taller), the number of rungs (5 is ideal), and whether the wood finish complements your existing furniture.**

A ladder that is too short looks awkward and holds fewer blankets. Five or six feet is the sweet spot — tall enough to make a visual statement but not so tall it overwhelms the room. Five rungs give you room to drape blankets at different levels, which creates that effortlessly layered look you see in interior design photos. Pay attention to finish: natural bamboo and light wood work in bohemian and Scandinavian rooms, torched or dark brown finishes work in farmhouse and traditional spaces, and white painted ladders are endlessly versatile.

Leaning designs (no wall anchor required) are more flexible — you can move them around seasonally or try different corners until you find the right spot.

## Quick Tips for Styling a Blanket Ladder

- Vary the texture and weight of what you drape — mix a chunky knit with a lighter linen throw for visual interest
- Leave a little space between each drape rather than packing them tight; it looks more intentional
- Place the ladder in a corner to keep it from looking like it is floating awkwardly in the middle of the wall
- In summer, use the ladder to hold light cotton or gauze throws; in winter, bring out the chunky knits and velvet for a cozy vibe
