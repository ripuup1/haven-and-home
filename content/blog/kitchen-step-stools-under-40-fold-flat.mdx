## Quick Tips for Kitchen Step Stools

- **9 inches is the kitchen standard.** Most kitchen top shelves are accessible from a 9-inch boost. Higher stools are useful for garage or basement storage but can be awkward in a kitchen space.
- **Check the weight capacity honestly.** Most plastic folding stools are rated at 300 pounds. If you are close to that limit, look for stools with higher ratings or metal construction.
- **Non-slip surface and non-slip feet both matter.** The top keeps you from slipping when you step on it. The rubber feet keep the stool from sliding on your kitchen floor. Make sure a stool has both.
- **Measure where you plan to store it.** A folded step stool is compact but not flat — it still has about 1 to 2 inches of depth. Measure the gap behind your door or between appliances before buying.
- **Test stability by shifting your weight side to side.** A wobbly step stool is dangerous. The best way to evaluate this is in-person, but reading reviews specifically mentioning stability is the next best thing.

A step stool is the solution to the "I have not used those bowls in six months" problem. Once you can actually reach things, you use them.
