If you have a craft room, a dedicated corner of the kitchen, or just a drawer that's become a black hole of scissors, tape, and rubber bands, a craft caddy is the upgrade you didn't know you needed. The difference between a craft space you want to use and one you avoid is almost entirely organizational. When everything has a place and you can see it and grab it, you actually create things. When it's buried in a box, you don't.

A caddy — specifically one with a handle and multiple compartments — is the most practical organizational tool for craft supplies because it's portable. You can take it to the table when you're working, bring it to another room, and put it away when you're done, all without ever losing track of where anything is. They're also genuinely useful for keeping kids' art supplies organized and accessible without spreading crayons across every flat surface in the house.

Under $25 covers everything from simple single-unit caddies to multi-pack classroom sets that work just as well at home. Here's what to look for and what to buy.

## What Should I Look for in a Craft Caddy?

**Look for a caddy with a center handle, multiple compartments of different sizes, a sturdy base that won't tip when full, and material that wipes clean easily.**

The handle is non-negotiable — it's what makes the caddy portable and keeps you from carrying armloads of supplies from room to room. Multiple compartments in different sizes let you separate markers from scissors from tape without everything jumbling together. Plastic wipes clean more easily than fabric for craft supplies, which are inherently messy.

## Is a Fabric Craft Caddy Better Than Plastic?

**Fabric caddies are lighter and softer to carry but harder to clean — for craft supplies that include paint, glue, and markers, plastic is significantly more practical.**

Fabric caddies work well for clean items like yarn, knitting needles, or paper. For supplies that drip, leak, or leave residue — glue guns, paint brushes, markers without caps — plastic is far easier to clean. A damp cloth handles most messes on plastic; fabric requires actual washing and may stain permanently from certain supplies.

## Quick Tips for Organizing Craft Supplies

- **Sort by category first** before buying caddies — knowing what you have tells you how many compartments and how many units you actually need.
- **Go plastic for messy supplies** — paint, glue, and markers belong in wipeable plastic caddies, not fabric.
- **Label each caddy** if you're using multiple units — a piece of washi tape with a label written in marker is fast and effective.
- **Keep most-used supplies at the front** of the caddy so you're not digging every time.
- **Buy an extra caddy** for a donation or gift bag — having extras makes art supply gifting to nieces, nephews, or friends very easy.
