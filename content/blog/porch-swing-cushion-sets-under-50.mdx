A porch swing without a cushion is just a wooden bench that swings — functional, sure, but not exactly the cozy retreat you pictured when you installed it. The right cushion set transforms the whole experience: suddenly you're out there every evening with a glass of wine, watching the neighborhood wind down. The wrong one leaves you adjusting your position every five minutes and retreating inside after 20.

What makes a porch swing cushion worth buying? First, thickness. A 4-to-5-inch cushion makes a genuine difference in long-term comfort — anything thinner starts to feel like sitting on a padded board after an hour. Second, weather resistance. Your cushion will live outside, get rained on, and sit in the sun for months. Waterproof fabric and UV-resistant coating aren't luxury features; they're maintenance requirements. Third, tie attachments. Without ties, your cushion will migrate to one end of the swing within a week.

Every pick below comes in under $50, hits at least 4 inches of thickness, and is rated waterproof. The only decision is which color goes with your porch.

## Which Porch Swing Cushion Fits Best?

**Measure your swing seat length before buying — most swing cushions come in 55-inch or 60-inch widths for a 2-3 seat swing. Most porch swing cushions are designed for swings in the 48-to-60-inch range.**

A cushion that's too short looks sloppy and slides around; one that's too long overhangs and flops. Pull out a tape measure before you order. Also note whether you want just a seat cushion or a set with a backrest — a backrest adds a few dollars but makes a significant comfort difference for extended sitting.

## Quick Tips for Porch Swing Cushions

- Bring cushions inside or store in a deck box during extended rain or storms, even if they're waterproof — prolong their life significantly
- Spot clean with mild dish soap and a soft brush; avoid machine washing unless the label specifically says safe
- In winter, store cushions in a breathable storage bag rather than a sealed plastic bin to prevent mildew
- Anti-slip mats placed under your cushion will prevent sliding better than ties alone on very smooth swing seats
- For a cohesive look, match your swing cushion color to one element on your porch — a door color, planter, or outdoor rug
