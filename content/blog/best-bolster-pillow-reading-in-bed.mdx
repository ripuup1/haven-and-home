## Quick Tips for Reading Pillow Setup

A few things that took me embarrassingly long to figure out.

Position the reading pillow with the arms slightly behind your shoulders, not flush with them. The arms are meant to support your elbows, and if the pillow is too far forward your elbows dangle awkwardly.

Use a regular pillow under your lower back as extra lumbar support if the reading pillow alone leaves your lower back unsupported. Two pillows working together is the actual correct setup.

Don't read with the pillow directly against a hard headboard. The foam collapses against the headboard and you lose support. A thin throw pillow or rolled blanket between the reading pillow and the headboard gives the foam room to do its job.

If you share the bed, leave the reading pillow at the foot of the bed during the day instead of trying to fit it in with your regular pillows. It's easier to grab when you want it and doesn't clutter the bed.

The best reading pillow is the one you actually pull out and use. If your current pillow setup is keeping you from reading in bed, any of these will fix that problem for under $35.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is the best reading pillow for bed under $35?

The best reading pillow under $35 is the Milliard Reading Pillow at $29. It has shredded memory foam fill, built-in arms for elbow support, a removable washable cover, and a side pocket for a phone or remote. The 20 x 17-inch size works for most adults in queen and king beds.

### Are bolster pillows or reading pillows better for neck pain?

Bolster pillows are better for neck pain because the cylindrical shape fills the natural curve at the base of your neck, which flat or wedge pillows can't do. The Brentwood Home Bolster at $34 is specifically designed for this. For best results, combine a bolster (for neck) with a reading pillow (for back).

### How do husband pillows compare to regular reading pillows?

Husband pillows are larger (around 24 inches tall) and have wingback-style arms that wrap around your shoulders, providing more upper back and head support than standard reading pillows. They work best for long reading sessions or people with back problems. The tradeoff is storage — they take up significant space on the bed when not in use.

### Is a bed wedge pillow good for reading?

Yes, a 30-degree bed wedge pillow supports your entire back at once rather than relying on you to sit upright against a vertical back. Wedge pillows are especially good for side readers and for people with acid reflux who also want to read in bed. The Kolbs Wedge at $33 is a reliable multi-purpose pick.

### How do I wash a reading pillow?

Most quality reading pillows have removable covers that machine wash on cold with a mild detergent. Tumble dry on low or air dry to keep the fabric soft. The foam insert should not be washed directly — spot clean only with a damp cloth if needed. Always check the specific product's care tag before washing.
