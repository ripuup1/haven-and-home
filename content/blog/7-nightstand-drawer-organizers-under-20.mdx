What actually lives in a nightstand drawer? For most people: a tangle of charging cables, three lip balms, a book light that has been dead for months, random receipts, an expired cough drop, and at least one item that cannot be identified in daylight. The drawer functions as a holding zone for everything that gets set on the nightstand and eventually swept in.

The fix is not buying a bigger nightstand. It is adding structure to the drawer you already have. The right organizer splits the space into defined zones so each category has a home — and the chaos stops regenerating every week. Best of all, none of the options below cost more than $20. Most cost less.

Whether your drawer is wide and shallow, narrow and deep, or some awkward in-between dimension, there is an organizer on this list that fits. Here are seven of the best.

## Quick Tips

- Measure your drawer depth before ordering a set — "standard" varies widely between nightstand models.
- Pull everything out before you install an organizer. The act of emptying the drawer usually reveals things you did not know were in there.
- Clear organizers photograph well if you ever style your bedroom for guests or social media, but velvet and bamboo options look more intentional in open-drawer situations.
- Assign one section specifically for items you reach for in the dark — sleep mask, earplugs, hand cream — so you can find them without turning on a light.
- Expandable organizers are worth the extra few dollars if you move frequently or own multiple nightstands.

A nightstand drawer that works well makes the whole room feel more under control. None of these fixes costs more than a takeout order — but the improvement to the nightly routine is immediate.
