Real greenery garlands are gorgeous for about four days, and then they dry out, shed leaves on your mantel, and become a crunchy fire hazard you feel guilty about throwing away. Faux garlands skip all of that — the good ones look convincingly real from normal viewing distance, hold their shape for years, and never need water, sunlight, or guilt when they inevitably get dusty.

The key to faux greenery that doesn't scream "fake" is all in the details: realistic color variation in the leaves, visible veining, flexible stems you can shape and arrange, and a mix of leaf sizes that mimics actual plant growth. Cheap faux garlands use uniform plastic leaves in one shade of green — they look fake from across the room. The garlands on this list use better materials, better color variation, and better construction, and they're all under $25.

Whether you're draping a mantel, winding through open shelving, framing a doorway, or creating a tablescape centerpiece, here are the most convincing options at prices that won't make you second-guess the purchase.

## Quick Tips for Styling Faux Greenery Garlands

- Bend and separate the leaves after unpacking — faux garlands arrive compressed from shipping, and spending 5 minutes fluffing makes a dramatic difference in realism.
- Mix garland types together for the most realistic effect: layer a eucalyptus garland with a willow vine, or tuck ivy into a boxwood garland for varied texture.
- Dust faux garlands monthly with a dry microfiber cloth or a quick blast of compressed air to keep them looking fresh and prevent that dusty-plastic look.
