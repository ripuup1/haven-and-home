## Do You Really Need an Expensive TV Stand?

Here's the thing. You can absolutely get a great-looking TV stand without spending $500 or more. The under-$200 range has exploded with options that look way more expensive than they are. Solid construction, real wood finishes, and thoughtful details like cable management are all standard now, even at budget price points.

The key is knowing what style works for your space and what features actually matter. Let's break it down.

## How to Choose the Right TV Stand

Before you start shopping, measure two things. First, your TV size. Your stand should be at least a few inches wider than your TV on each side for visual balance. A 55-inch TV, for example, looks best on a stand that's at least 60 inches wide.

Second, measure the wall space where the stand will go. Don't forget to account for any outlets, vents, or baseboards. And think about what you need to store. Streaming devices, gaming consoles, remotes, and cables all need a home.

## What to Look for in a Budget TV Stand

Weight capacity matters more than you think. Make sure the stand supports your TV's weight, not just its size. Most product listings include a maximum weight, so check it against your TV's specs.

Cable management is another big one. Look for stands with holes in the back panel or built-in cord channels. Nothing ruins the look of a nice console like a tangle of visible cables.

And pay attention to the assembly reviews. Some budget stands are easy to put together with clear instructions, while others turn into a frustrating afternoon project. The reviews will tell you which is which.

## How to Style Your TV Stand

Your TV stand doesn't have to be just about the TV. Use the open shelves and surfaces to display a mix of functional and decorative items. A small plant (real or [faux](/blog/faux-plants-that-look-real)), a stack of books, and a decorative box for remote storage go a long way.

Keep the area around the TV relatively minimal. Too many items competing for attention around the screen makes the whole wall feel cluttered. A few well-chosen pieces are always better than a dozen random objects.

## FAQ

### What size TV stand do I need for a 55-inch TV?

A 55-inch TV measures about 48 inches wide, so you'll want a stand that's at least 54 to 60 inches to give it breathing room on each side. Going wider is always fine and actually looks better proportionally.

### Are floating TV stands hard to install?

Most floating TV stands require basic wall mounting with a drill. The key is finding studs in your wall for secure support. If you're comfortable hanging a heavy shelf, you can handle a floating TV stand. Budget about 45 minutes to an hour for installation.

### Is it better to mount a TV or use a stand?

It depends on your space. Wall mounting saves floor space and looks sleek, but you lose the storage a TV stand provides. If you have gaming consoles, a sound bar, or other media devices, a stand with shelving is usually more practical. Many people do both by mounting the TV above a low console for the best of both worlds.
