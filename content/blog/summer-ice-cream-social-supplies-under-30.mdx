Want to throw the most talked-about summer party on your block without spending hours in the kitchen? Two words: ice cream social. It's the easiest possible entertaining format — no cooking, no timing everything at once, no stress. You set up a station, you pull out the toppings, and your guests do the rest. The whole thing runs itself.

The catch is that "ice cream social" done right looks different from "ice cream straight from the carton." You want the right bowls, the right tools, and a few conversation-starting touches that make it feel like an event. Every item on this list is under $30, and most of them can be used again for every summer party you host from here on out.

## Quick Tips

- Set up the ice cream bar 15 minutes before guests arrive so the scoops are ready and toppings are open — cold scoops of ice cream that sit out waiting get icy
- Pre-scoop ice cream into the bowls and put them back in the freezer for 10-15 minutes before serving if you're hosting a large group so you're not scooping for everyone at once
- Offer 3 ice cream flavors max — choice paralysis is real, and vanilla, chocolate, and one wild card covers everyone
- The waffle cone maker's batter makes about 8-10 cones per batch — make one test batch the day before so you know how yours comes out
- Rent a cooler or use an insulated bag under the table to keep backup ice cream cold during the party without making constant trips inside
