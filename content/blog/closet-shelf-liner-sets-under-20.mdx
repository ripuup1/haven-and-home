## Should You Use Adhesive or Non-Adhesive Liners?

**Non-adhesive is almost always the better choice for closets.** Adhesive liners seem more secure, but they create problems: they leave residue when you try to remove them, they can peel up over time in humid spaces, and replacing them requires a lot of scrubbing. Non-adhesive liners grip through texture and weight, reposition easily if you cut them wrong, and come out cleanly when you want to swap them out.

The exception is if your shelves have a very slippery surface like high-gloss laminate or if you're lining kitchen shelves where liquids could slide under the liner. For standard closet use, non-adhesive every time.

## Quick Tips for Lining Shelves Like a Pro

- **Measure first, cut generously** — it's much easier to trim a liner that's slightly too long than to discover yours is an inch short.
- **Line the shelf before putting anything on it** — obvious but easy to skip; once your items are back in place you won't want to move them again.
- **Use double-sided tape at the front edge** to keep the liner from creeping forward when you slide items out.
- **Replace liners every year or two** — they collect dust and lose grip over time; replacing them is a 10-minute refresh that makes your whole closet feel new again.
- **Stack two thin liners** in high-wear areas (like under shoe storage) for extra cushioning and protection.
