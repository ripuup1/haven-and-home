I used to wake up at 2am and blindly knock my phone off the nightstand at least twice a week. My water glass lived in constant danger. My chapstick disappeared into the mattress gap every other night. I tried a fancier nightstand. I tried a charging station. I tried telling myself I'd be more intentional about where I put things before bed. None of it worked.

Then I bought a $22 bedside caddy that hangs over the mattress edge, and all of those problems disappeared. I'm not exaggerating when I say it's the highest impact-to-dollar ratio of any home product I've owned. I've since recommended it to my roommate, my mom, two friends who mentioned their nightstand was cluttered, and one coworker who made the mistake of complaining about it near me. All of them have it now. Here's the full breakdown of the category — including the ones worth upgrading to if $22 isn't your style.

## Quick Tips

- The tuck-in panel needs to go under the mattress at least 8–10 inches to stay stable — if yours is slipping, it's not far enough under.
- Choose a caddy with at least one closed pocket for chapstick and small items — open pockets let things slide out when you reach in at night.
- If you share a bed and want a caddy on each side, buy two — the mattress caddy is cheap enough that symmetry is worth it.
- Felt and canvas versions are machine washable on gentle cycle. Do it once a month — they collect a surprising amount of dust.
- If you go the valet tray route, also put a charging pad on the nightstand and treat the tray as the phone-free zone. Better sleep, no excuses.

The $22 hanging caddy is still the one I'd recommend first to almost anyone. But whichever version you pick, the upgrade is the same: the stuff that used to be in the gap between your mattress and the floor is now actually accessible. That alone is worth it.
