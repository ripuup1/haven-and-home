Bathroom counters are always smaller than they look in photos. By the time you have a soap dispenser, a moisturizer, your toothbrush holder, and a candle that makes you feel like a person, you are out of room. A shelf riser changes everything — it lifts some items up a tier so the counter underneath opens up for the things you actually use every morning.

The best bathroom shelf risers are not just shelves. They are risers specifically designed for counter depth — usually 10 to 14 inches deep — so they do not overhang the sink or fall off the back edge. They should also be stable enough that they do not wobble when you grab something from the top tier in a half-awake morning rush.

What makes a riser actually useful: two tiers (so you gain a full extra shelf), either removable legs so you can adjust for different cabinet heights, or a design narrow enough to fit in the corner next to the faucet. All five picks below hit one or more of those marks and stay under $25.

## Quick Tips for Using Bathroom Shelf Risers

- Put your most-used morning items on the lower tier so you grab them without lifting anything
- Taller bottles (serums, hair oil) go on the bottom; shorter jars (moisturizer, lip balm) go on top
- A shelf riser inside the cabinet under the sink doubles your storage down there too
- If the riser slides on your counter, add a small strip of non-slip drawer liner underneath
- Measure counter depth before buying — standard bathroom vanities are 21 to 22 inches deep, and a 12-inch-deep riser leaves plenty of front space
