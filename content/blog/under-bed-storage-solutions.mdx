The space under your bed is the most underused storage in any bedroom. It's hidden, easily accessible, and large enough to hold an entire season of clothes, extra bedding, or shoes. The problem is that most storage containers are too tall to slide under a standard bed frame, so everything ends up jammed in and impossible to pull out.

The fix is measuring the clearance under your bed first, then choosing containers designed for that height. Most bed frames have 6-8 inches of clearance. Here are the best under-bed storage solutions that actually fit and slide in and out smoothly.

## Do Vacuum Storage Bags Save Space Under the Bed?

Vacuum storage bags compress bulky items like comforters, pillows, and winter coats to a fraction of their original size. A queen-size comforter that normally fills an entire bin compresses down to about 2 inches thick. This means you can store three times as much in the same under-bed space.

These large vacuum bags come with a hand pump (no vacuum needed), which makes them easy to seal anywhere. Once compressed, slide them directly under the bed or place them inside a flat under-bed bin for extra protection. They're the best solution for [small bedroom storage](/blog/small-bedroom-ideas) where every cubic inch matters.

## What If My Bed Is Too Low to the Ground?

If your bed frame sits less than 6 inches off the floor, bed risers are the solution. A set of 4 risers lifts your bed by 3, 5, or 8 inches, creating storage space where there was none. Heavy-duty risers support up to 1,200 lbs per riser, so they're safe for any mattress and frame combination.

Choose stackable risers so you can adjust the height. Start with 3 inches of lift, and if you want more room, add another set on top for 6 inches total. Just make sure you can still get in and out of bed comfortably after adding height.

## How to Measure for Under-Bed Storage

Before buying anything, measure the clearance between the floor and the bottom of your bed frame at the lowest point (usually the center where the slats sag slightly). Subtract one inch from that measurement to account for carpet or the thickness of the bin sliding in and out. If your measurement is 7 inches, buy containers that are 6 inches tall or shorter.

Also measure the width and depth available, accounting for any center support legs or side rails that narrow the usable space. Most queen beds offer about 50 x 60 inches of usable under-bed area, minus the center support.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is the best under-bed storage for small bedrooms?

Vacuum storage bags (6-pack, $18) are the best option for small bedrooms because they compress bulky items to one-third their size. Pair them with low-profile zippered bins (set of 2, $22) for organized, dust-free storage. For even more space, add bed risers ($15) to create extra clearance under the bed.

### How tall should under-bed storage bins be?

Under-bed storage bins should be at least 1 inch shorter than your bed's clearance height. Most standard bed frames have 6-8 inches of clearance, so bins that are 5-6 inches tall work for most beds. Always measure your specific bed before buying, checking at the lowest point where slats may sag.

### Can you use under-bed storage on carpet?

Yes, but choose bins with smooth bottoms or rolling drawers with casters designed for carpet. Fabric bins with reinforced handles are easiest to slide on carpet. Avoid bins with lip edges that catch on carpet fibers. Rolling drawers with large-wheel casters work on both carpet and hardwood.
