If you've ever had three friends over and one of them ended up sitting on the rug awkwardly, you already know the apartment seating problem. Most apartment living rooms can fit a couch, maybe a single accent chair, and a coffee table. That's it. The minute you have more than three or four people in the room, somebody is on the floor.

Floor pillows fix this without adding furniture you don't have space for. The good ones double as decor when nobody's over, store flat under the bed when you need them gone, and cost a fraction of what an extra chair would. After buying way too many of these, here's what I've learned actually works.

## What to Look For in a Floor Pillow

Most floor pillows on Amazon look nicer in photos than they feel in person. A few specs to check before you click buy:

- **Filling weight at least 4 pounds.** Anything under that flattens the second you sit on it.
- **Removable cover with a zipper.** You will spill on it. Trust me.
- **A handle or carry strap.** Sounds minor, but moving a 24-inch floor pillow without a handle is genuinely annoying.
- **Stiffer foam or buckwheat fill** for sitting upright, **shredded foam or down alternative** for lounging.
- **Solid color, neutral palette.** Patterns date faster and clash with whatever sofa you swap in next year.

If a floor pillow nails three of those five, it's probably worth buying. Here are the ones I'd actually keep.

## How to Choose

Pick by your most common use case. If you mainly host adults, get one square Moroccan-style pillow plus one bolster — both can store flat behind your sofa. If you have kids, get a set of two round cushions plus one tufted plush pillow. If your space is really tight, the bolster does triple duty better than any other option here.

Don't buy more than three. Floor pillows pile up, and the goal is to add seating without adding clutter. Three good ones will handle every situation a small apartment ever throws at you.
