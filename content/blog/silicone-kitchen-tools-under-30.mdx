## Quick Tips for Switching to Silicone Kitchen Tools

- Hand wash your silicone tools after cooking with strong-smelling foods like garlic or onion — even though they are dishwasher safe, odors can linger if you skip an initial rinse.
- Store silicone utensils in a utensil holder or hung from a hook rather than stuffed in a drawer. The flexible heads can get bent out of shape if heavy items press on them.
- Match your silicone tool color to your kitchen palette. Most brands offer the same products in neutral (black, gray, beige) and bold (red, teal, colorful sets) colorways.
- Replace tools gradually if budget is a concern. Start with a spatula set and one pot holder set — those two things cover 80% of daily cooking needs.
- Check that any silicone tool is labeled BPA-free and food-grade. Cheap imports sometimes skip this certification, so stick with products that explicitly state it.
