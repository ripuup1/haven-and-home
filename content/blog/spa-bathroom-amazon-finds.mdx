I used to think a spa-like bathroom required a full gut renovation, a freestanding tub, and one of those rainfall showers you see in luxury hotel ads. Then I spent about $120 on Amazon over a long weekend and genuinely changed how my bathroom feels. Not how it *looks* in photos (though that improved too), but how it feels at 9 PM when the kids are finally asleep and I'm trying to decompress.

The secret isn't any single product. It's layering a few small, affordable touches that engage your senses. Something that smells good, something that feels soft, something that sets a mood. Here are the six things that made the biggest difference in my bathroom, and I think they'll do the same for yours.

## Bamboo Bath Caddy Tray

If you take baths even occasionally, a bath caddy changes the whole experience. This one extends to fit most standard tubs (it adjusts from about 29 to 43 inches) and has a slot for your phone or tablet, a wine glass holder, and a little book rest. The bamboo looks clean and warm against white tile.

One thing to know: you'll want to wipe it down after each use and let it dry upright. Bamboo can get water spots if you leave it sitting in moisture. But honestly, that takes ten seconds, and the aesthetic payoff is worth it.

## Turkish Cotton Bath Towels

I'll be honest, I resisted spending $40+ on towels for years. Then I finally tried a set of Turkish cotton towels. The difference is real. They're thinner than those fluffy towels from Target, which actually means they dry faster and don't get that musty smell. They also get softer with every wash instead of stiffer.

The only downside is they don't feel as plush right out of the package. Give them three or four washes and you'll understand the hype.
