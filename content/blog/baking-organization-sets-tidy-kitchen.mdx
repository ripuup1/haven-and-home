## How Do You Store Baking Ingredients Neatly?

**The Simply Gourmet Stainless Steel Measuring Set ($15 for 12 pieces) pairs perfectly with a set of [airtight canisters](/blog/aesthetic-kitchen-canisters). But for baking specifically, ingredient containers with flat sides store more efficiently in a cabinet than round ones.**

The key to baking ingredient organization isn't just having containers — it's having the right shape. Square or rectangular containers with flat sides pack together in a cabinet without wasted space between them. Round containers look prettier on a countertop but waste about 30% of shelf space in a cabinet. For baking staples (flour, sugar, baking powder, cocoa), you want airtight containers with wide openings so you can scoop with a measuring cup inside. Label everything with a [label maker](/blog/label-maker-home-organization) so there's no guessing which white powder is flour and which is powdered sugar. A dedicated baking shelf in your pantry or cabinet keeps everything together.

## Quick Tips for Baking Organization

- **Store baking sheets vertically, never stacked flat** — vertical storage lets you grab one without disturbing the rest
- **Keep your most-used items at the front** of the cabinet and seasonal items (holiday cookie cutters, specialty pans) in the back
- **Dedicate one cabinet or shelf** to baking supplies exclusively — mixing baking items with everyday cooking gear creates chaos
- **Nest mixing bowls** inside each other and store them with your baking supplies, not with serving dishes
- **Hang measuring cups** inside a cabinet door with a command hook to keep them accessible and off the shelf
