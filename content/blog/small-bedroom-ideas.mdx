Living in a small bedroom teaches you things. Like how to get dressed in a 2-foot gap between the bed and the wall. Or how to use your nightstand as a desk, vanity, and dining table simultaneously. Small bedrooms aren't charming. They're a spatial puzzle that requires actual strategy to make livable.

The standard advice is always "get a smaller bed!" which is unhelpful if you're an adult who doesn't want to sleep in a twin. The real solution is thinking vertically, using the back of doors, the space under the bed, and the walls. Here's what actually works when your bedroom square footage is in the single digits.
