## What Size Cabinet Shelf Riser Do I Need?

**Measure the inside of your cabinet first -- width, depth, and the height between shelves. Most standard kitchen cabinets are 12-13 inches deep and 10-12 inches between shelves. An expandable riser ($18, 4.5 stars, 11,300+ reviews) adjusts from 15 to 22 inches wide, so it fits everything from narrow spice cabinets to full-width upper cabinets.**

The biggest mistake people make with shelf risers is buying them without measuring first. Grab a tape measure, open your cabinet, and check three things: the width of the shelf, the depth from front to back, and the height between shelves. That last measurement is crucial -- your riser needs to leave enough clearance above AND below for what you're storing.

## Quick Tips for Maximizing Cabinet Storage

- **Measure before you buy** -- check width, depth, and height between shelves inside your cabinets so nothing is too tight or too loose
- **Store heavy items on lower shelves** with heavy-duty risers, and lighter items (mugs, glasses) on upper shelves with standard risers
- **Use the riser's top surface for daily items** and the space underneath for things you use less frequently
- **Group similar items together** when using risers -- all plates on one level, all bowls on another -- so muscle memory kicks in
- **Add shelf liner under your risers** to prevent sliding and protect cabinet surfaces from scratches
- **Declutter before you organize** -- a shelf riser won't help if you're trying to cram in 40 mugs when you only use 8

The beautiful thing about shelf risers is that they work instantly. No assembly instructions to decipher, no anchors to drill, no trips to the hardware store. Open cabinet, place riser, add stuff. Five minutes and you've basically doubled your storage capacity.
