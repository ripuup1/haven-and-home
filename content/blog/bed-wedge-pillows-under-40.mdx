If you've ever woken up at 2am with that burning feeling in your chest, you already know about acid reflux. What you might not know is that a wedge pillow — not an extra pillow, but an actual inclined wedge — can significantly reduce nighttime symptoms by keeping stomach acid from traveling upward while you sleep. The science is simple: elevation works.

But wedge pillows aren't just for acid reflux. They're also genuinely useful for reducing snoring, relieving back pressure during pregnancy, improving circulation for people who sit up to read, and supporting the upper body after chest or shoulder surgery. The trick is getting the right height and firmness for your specific situation.

## What Height Wedge Pillow Do You Need?

**For acid reflux and GERD, doctors typically recommend 6 to 8 inches of elevation — that's enough to keep stomach acid down without putting strain on your lower back. For snoring, even 4 to 6 inches helps. For sitting up to read, 10 to 12 inches is more comfortable.**

The other factor is width. Standard bed wedges are 24 to 26 inches wide, which works for most single sleepers. But if you move around at night or sleep with a partner who sometimes needs it, look for a wider option (30 inches+). Most of the options below come in multiple widths, so check the listing for the dimensions that fit your bed.

## Quick Tips for Bed Wedge Pillows

- **Pair with a regular pillow on top:** most people sleep better when they place a normal pillow on top of the wedge for neck support rather than resting their head directly on the foam.
- **For acid reflux:** the incline works best when you sleep consistently on your left side, which keeps the stomach positioned below the esophagus. Right side sleeping can actually worsen symptoms even with elevation.
- **Break-in period:** foam wedge pillows can feel firmer than expected for the first week or two. Give it 10 to 14 nights before deciding it's not working — your body adapts.
- **Cover care:** unzip and wash the cover weekly, especially if you're a warm sleeper. Foam wedges can accumulate moisture, which affects longevity.
- **FSA/HSA eligible:** if you have a Flexible Spending Account, a doctor's note for acid reflux or sleep apnea can make most wedge pillows FSA-eligible. Check with your plan.
