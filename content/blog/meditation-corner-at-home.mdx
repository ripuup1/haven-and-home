You don't need a spare room or a yoga studio membership to meditate. You need a corner. Literally any corner. A spot by a window, the end of a hallway, a quiet section of your bedroom. The trick is making it feel intentional enough that when you sit down there, your brain gets the signal that it's time to be still. Here's everything you need to set one up, all from Amazon, all under $35 a piece.

The cushion matters more than you think. Sitting on the floor without one gets uncomfortable fast, and discomfort is the fastest way to bail on a meditation practice. A zafu cushion lifts your hips above your knees, which takes the pressure off your lower back and lets you sit with a straight spine without straining. This one is filled with buckwheat hulls, so it molds to your shape and doesn't flatten out over time. It's firm but comfortable. The only adjustment period is getting used to sitting on something that isn't a couch.

A lightweight throw blanket draped over your shoulders during meditation keeps you warm without weighing you down. Your body temperature drops when you're sitting still, and getting cold is another quick way to cut a session short. This one is soft enough to feel like a comfort object but light enough that it doesn't feel heavy or restrictive. Keep it folded on your cushion when you're not using it. It doubles as a visual marker for your space.
