The average person uses about 17 different bathroom products between waking up and walking out the door. Toothbrush, toothpaste, mouthwash, face wash, moisturizer, eye cream, sunscreen, deodorant, hairbrush, dry shampoo, makeup remover, mascara, foundation, eye liner, blush, lip balm, contact lenses. That's a stat I genuinely think about every time I walk into someone's bathroom and the counter looks naked. Where does it all go?

The answer, in well-organized bathrooms, is "into one of these seven things." All under $20, all available on Amazon, and all designed to hide the daily mess in a way that takes you 30 seconds to put back at night. Skip down to the Quick Tips at the end if you just want my one big rule about bathroom storage.

## Quick Tips for Bathroom Organization

A few things I've learned from years of trying to keep a small bathroom looking tidy:

- **Pick a finish and stick to it.** All gold-and-clear, or all bamboo, or all matte black. Mixing three or four finishes turns the whole counter into visual noise.
- **The empty-shelf trick.** Always leave one shelf or one corner empty on purpose. It signals "this space is intentional" and not "this is everything I own."
- **Decant the everyday stuff.** Mouthwash, hand soap, lotion. Generic drugstore bottles ruin the whole effect. Glass dispensers run $15 for a set of two and they last forever.
- **The 30-second reset.** Every night before bed, put everything back into its tray, jar, or drawer. If it takes longer than 30 seconds, your system isn't working and you need fewer products on the counter.
- **Toss empties weekly.** That half-empty bottle of conditioner you've been meaning to use up has been there for nine months. It's not coming back. Throw it out.
