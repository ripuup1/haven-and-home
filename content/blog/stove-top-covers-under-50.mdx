Small kitchens have a counter space problem that no amount of reorganizing can fix. You can declutter your countertops, add a cart, mount shelves on the wall — and you'll still run out of room when you're prepping a real meal. But there's a full 30 by 20-inch surface sitting right in the middle of your kitchen that's only being used a few hours a day: your stove top.

Stove top covers — sometimes called noodle boards — turn that dead space into usable counter area when you're not cooking. Set it over the burners, and suddenly you have room for a cutting board, a stand mixer, or just a clean surface to stage ingredients before dinner. Pull it off when you're ready to cook, and the stove works exactly as before.

Every option here is under $50, sized for standard ranges, and designed to handle daily use.

## Are Stove Top Covers Safe to Use?

**Yes, with one important rule: never place a cover on a stove that's still hot or has a burner turned on.** The covers themselves are made from heat-resistant materials — bamboo, hardwood, or composite board — but they're not designed to withstand direct burner heat. Always let your stove cool completely before placing the cover, and always remove it before turning on any burner.

For gas stoves, make sure the cover sits flat over the grates without wobbling. For electric and glass-top ranges, flat covers work best since there are no grates to deal with. Some covers come with rubber feet on the bottom to prevent scratching on glass cooktops — if yours doesn't, add adhesive felt pads yourself.

## Bamboo Noodle Board Stove Cover: Best Overall Pick

**The bamboo noodle board is the most popular stove top cover for a reason — it's attractive, functional, and gives you that farmhouse-kitchen Pinterest look while adding genuinely useful counter space.**

Bamboo is naturally moisture-resistant and antimicrobial, which makes it ideal for a surface that'll be near food prep. Most bamboo noodle boards come with handles cut into the sides for easy lifting and legs or rails on the bottom to keep the board stable over burner grates. The standard size is about 30 by 22 inches, which covers a four-burner range with a little overhang on each side.

At around $35, it's a solid investment that doubles as a serving board when you're entertaining. The bamboo surface is smooth enough for rolling dough (that's where the name "noodle board" comes from) and sturdy enough to hold a KitchenAid mixer without flexing.

## Quick Tips

- Always let your stove cool completely before placing any cover — even heat-resistant materials aren't designed for direct burner contact
- Add adhesive felt or rubber pads to the bottom if your cover doesn't come with them, especially on glass cooktops
- Oil bamboo covers every few months with food-safe mineral oil to prevent drying and cracking
- Store your cover vertically (leaning against the wall or beside the fridge) to save space when you're cooking
- Measure your stove top before ordering — standard ranges are 30 in. wide, but some are 36 in.
