A fruit basket sounds like the most boring kitchen purchase imaginable until you realize it's sitting on your counter every single day, visible from every angle. The right one adds warmth and texture to your kitchen. The wrong one looks like it belongs in a college dorm. The difference between a $15 wire bowl and a $35 tiered stand is more about your counter space and style than quality — both work, they just solve different problems.

Here are eight fruit baskets that actually look intentional on a kitchen counter, from simple wire bowls to statement-making tiered stands and boho hanging baskets. Every option here pulls double duty as decor.

## Quick Tips for Displaying Fruit

- **Wash fruit before displaying** only if you'll eat it within a day or two — moisture speeds up spoiling for most produce
- **Keep bananas separate** from other fruit; they release ethylene gas that ripens everything around them faster
- **Rotate your display** by putting newer fruit on the bottom and older fruit on top where you'll grab it first
- **Skip the fridge** for tomatoes, avocados, bananas, peaches, and citrus — they taste better and last well at room temperature
- **Mix in a faux lemon or artichoke** for visual interest when your real fruit supply is running low
