If you've ever read the back of a cooking spray can and wondered what half those ingredients are, you're not alone. Traditional aerosol cooking sprays contain propellants, emulsifiers, and anti-foaming agents that have no business being in your food. The fix is simple: a refillable oil sprayer bottle that lets you use the oil you actually want to cook with.

These sprayers work with olive oil, avocado oil, coconut oil, and basically anything liquid at room temperature. You fill them up, pump or press, and get a fine, even mist across your pan, air fryer basket, or salad. Most pay for themselves within a month since you're buying oil in bulk instead of $6 spray cans. Here are six options under $20 that actually deliver a consistent spray without clogging.

## Quick Tips for Oil Sprayer Bottles

- Clean the nozzle weekly with warm water to prevent olive oil buildup and clogging
- Don't fill sprayers with extra virgin olive oil if you're cooking above 375 degrees — use avocado or refined olive oil instead
- Let oil come to room temperature before spraying for the most consistent mist
- Keep a separate sprayer for vinegar-based dressings to avoid flavor mixing
