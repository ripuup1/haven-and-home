## What Makes a Ceramic Baking Dish Worth Buying?

**The key markers of quality ceramic bakeware are consistent glaze coverage, thick walls that heat evenly, and handles with enough grip to be safely pulled from a 425-degree oven.** Secondary factors include whether it can go directly from the fridge to the oven (thermal shock resistance), and whether it is actually dishwasher safe without the glaze crazing over time.

The dishes below were selected because they hit all of these marks. A beautiful dish that chips after three uses is a bad investment at any price. Look for porcelain and stoneware construction rather than thin earthenware, and always check whether the handles are part of the molded piece or added attachments — molded handles are structurally stronger.

## Quick Tips for Ceramic Bakeware

- Allow ceramic dishes to reach room temperature before placing cold food directly from the fridge into a preheated oven — or start in a cold oven and heat together to avoid thermal shock
- Never put ceramic bakeware on a direct stovetop burner, even gas — these are oven-only pieces
- To remove baked-on residue, soak in warm soapy water for 20 minutes rather than scrubbing aggressively, which can dull the glaze
- Ceramic retains heat longer than metal, so let your dish rest 5 minutes before cutting into it — it continues cooking after leaving the oven
