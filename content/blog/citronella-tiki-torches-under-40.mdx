The warm flicker of the flame, the citrus-tinged smoke drifting lazily across the patio — it transforms an ordinary Tuesday night into something that actually feels like a gathering. And if those torches happen to be keeping mosquitoes at bay while they do it, even better.

The market for tiki torches ranges from cheap bamboo stakes that fall apart in one season to over-engineered metal designs with more parts than they need. This list cuts through the clutter to find the torches that look great, burn reliably, and won't cost you more than $40 — for the whole set. ## Bamboo vs. Metal vs. Glass Torches — Which Is Right for You?

**Bamboo torches are the classic choice: natural-looking, lightweight, inexpensive, and easy to replace.** They work beautifully in relaxed, tropical-inspired outdoor spaces and come in packs of four or more for very little money. The downside is durability — bamboo weathers faster than metal and can crack or warp if left outside year-round.

Metal torches are more durable and often have a more polished, permanent feel. They hold up better through multiple seasons and usually have adjustable stakes for firmer ground placement. Glass torches are the most decorative option — they look stunning in evening light but require a bit more care. For most backyards, a set of four to six bamboo or metal torches positioned around a seating area strikes the perfect balance of ambiance and function.

## Quick Tips

- Position torches 6 to 8 feet apart for the best mosquito-repelling coverage around a seating area
- Fill canisters no more than three-quarters full to allow room for expansion on hot days
- Always extinguish with the snuffer cap — blowing them out spreads hot wax or fuel
- Keep citronella torch fuel in a sealed container away from direct sunlight
- Bring glass torches inside during heavy storms to protect them from cracking
