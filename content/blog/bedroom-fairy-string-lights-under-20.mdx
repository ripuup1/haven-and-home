## What's the Difference Between Fairy Lights and String Lights?

**"Fairy lights" typically refers to lights on a thin copper or silver wire, which makes them flexible and virtually invisible when draped.** Regular string lights use a thicker wire or plastic cord, which is more visible and structured. For bedroom use, copper wire fairy lights are almost always the better choice — they disappear into whatever you're decorating and the individual LED bulbs look like tiny floating stars.

USB-powered is the way to go for bedrooms since you can plug them into a phone charger or laptop port without needing an outlet in just the right spot. Battery-operated works too but you'll be changing batteries every few weeks with regular use.

## Quick Tips for Bedroom Fairy Lights

- Use removable hooks (Command strips) to hang copper wire lights along a wall without putting holes in your drywall
- For a canopy effect, attach four hooks to the ceiling above your bed and let the lights drape down and outward
- Warm white (2700-3000K) is best for bedrooms — cool white or daylight looks harsh at night
- Keep lights on a timer (most remotes have one) so they turn off automatically after 2-4 hours — you won't have to think about it
- Layer two or three different types for the richest look: a copper wire base layer, a curtain panel behind the bed, and globe lights along a shelf
