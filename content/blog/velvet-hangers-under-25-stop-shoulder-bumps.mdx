Skip the cheap plastic hangers — they're the reason your sweaters all have weird points sticking out of the shoulders. The plastic hook on a standard hanger is too narrow to support knit fabric, so the sweater slowly molds itself into a permanent peak right where the hanger's shoulder ends. Velvet hangers fix this in two ways: the wider, flatter shoulder distributes weight, and the velvet flocking grips the fabric so it doesn't slide and stretch.

The good news is decent velvet hangers are dirt cheap. Most of these sets work out to under 50 cents per hanger, and they make your closet look better in a way that's almost embarrassingly easy. Here are the eight sets I'd actually buy.

## Quick Tips

A few things I've learned the hard way after using velvet hangers for years:

- **Don't mix hanger colors.** The whole point is visual consistency. Pick one color for your main closet and stick to it.
- **Replace plastic dry cleaner hangers immediately.** They are the worst offenders for shoulder dimples.
- **Skip wire hangers entirely.** Even when they're free, they're worth less than free.
- **For knits, button or zip the garment before hanging** so the velvet has more surface area to grip.
- **Vacuum the velvet flocking once a year** with a soft brush attachment. Lint builds up and reduces grip.

The whole closet conversion takes about 20 minutes per 50 hangers, including donating the old ones. Total cost for a complete adult closet: usually under $60. You'll see the difference the first time you go to grab a sweater and it isn't lying on the floor.
