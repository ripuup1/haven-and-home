## Quick Tips for Using Acrylic Drawer Organizers

- Before buying, measure your drawer inside width, length, and depth. Most acrylic organizers are 2 to 3 inches deep — check that your drawer is deep enough for the organizer and still closes properly.
- Use a microfiber cloth slightly dampened with water to clean acrylic. Avoid harsh cleaners, which cloud and scratch the surface over time.
- Arrange organizers so the most-used items are in the front section and less-used items go to the back. A simple rule that saves time every day.
- Add a thin liner of contact paper under acrylic organizers in kitchen drawers to prevent the drawer bottom from getting scratched.
- When filling a new organizer, do a trial edit first: empty the drawer completely, sort items into categories, then only put back what you actually use.
