Medicine cabinets are the junk drawers of the bathroom. You open them looking for ibuprofen and end up staring at expired cough syrup from 2021, three half-used tubes of Neosporin, and a contact lens case from a prescription you no longer have. The good news: because medicine cabinets are small, organizing one takes about 15 minutes and costs under $30 in supplies.

The trick is using the right-sized organizers. Most medicine cabinets have narrow shelves (3 to 5 inches deep), so standard bins don't fit. Everything here is specifically sized for that tight space. Start by emptying the whole cabinet, tossing anything expired, and then follow along with these products.

## Quick Tips for Medicine Cabinet Organization

- **Check expiration dates first** — toss anything expired before you start organizing (you'll be surprised how much you can throw away)
- **Group by purpose**: daily meds together, first aid together, pain relief together
- **Keep daily items at eye level** and rarely-used items on the top shelf
- **Store prescriptions in their original bottles** for safety, but consolidate OTC meds if you have duplicates
- **Do a 5-minute check every 3 months** to toss expired items and put misplaced items back
