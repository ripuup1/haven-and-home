You know the drill. You need a battery for the remote, the smoke detector, a kid's toy — whatever it is. A battery organizer case solves this problem completely. Everything sorted by size, visible at a glance, and — with the models that include a built-in tester — you can instantly know which batteries still have juice before you grab them.

These cases are almost embarrassingly affordable. All five picks below are under $20, and every one of them is worth every dollar.

## Why You Actually Need a Battery Organizer

**The short answer: you're wasting money without one.** Batteries left loose in a drawer drain faster, get confused with dead ones, and end up in the trash when they could have had more life. A proper case protects them, keeps sizes separate, and gives you a way to test them before you waste time swapping in dead batteries.

The best cases also include a removable battery tester — usually a small digital device that clips into the case — so you can check any battery in seconds. This alone will save you from countless frustrating moments of finding out your smoke detector battery is dead at 2 AM.

## Quick Tips for Battery Organization

- **Test before storing.** When you get fresh batteries, mark the purchase date lightly with a permanent marker on the package. When you open the package, test any loose batteries before adding them to your organizer to confirm they are fresh.
- **Separate used from new.** Keep a small separate section (even just a labeled bag) for batteries that are partially used but still have life. Use those first.
- **Keep your organizer somewhere visible.** In a drawer you rarely open, you'll forget it exists. On a shelf in the garage, laundry room, or home office where it's easy to grab — that's where it actually gets used.
- **Check smoke detector batteries twice a year.** Daylight saving time changes are an easy reminder. Test them with your battery tester and replace anything below 80% capacity.
- **Dispose of dead batteries properly.** Many hardware stores and electronics retailers accept old batteries for recycling. Keep a small container near your battery organizer for dead batteries so they don't get mixed back in.
