Most Mother's Day gifts do one of two things: they get used once out of obligation, or they sit on a shelf looking decorative while serving no actual function. Bath bombs are gone in a week. Candle sets get burned once and then feel too nice to use. Chocolates last three days.

A good jewelry dish — or a set of them — is different. It earns a spot on the vanity or dresser because it solves a problem that comes up every single day: where to put the ring when you wash your hands, the earrings when you get home, the bracelet you keep forgetting to put back in the jewelry box. Once a dish like that is on the counter, it's there for years.

This is not a sentimental gift. It is a genuinely useful one, which is more respectful, not less.
