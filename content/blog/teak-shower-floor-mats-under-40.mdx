Standard bath mats are the worst thing in your bathroom. They trap moisture, develop that musty smell within a week, and look dingy no matter how often you wash them. I replaced mine with a teak shower mat and I have not looked back once. No mildew smell, no throwing it in the wash every three days, no pink ring from sitting wet for too long.

Teak wood is naturally water-resistant and contains oils that repel moisture, which is why it's been used in boat decks and outdoor furniture for centuries. In the shower, this means the slats dry out quickly between uses and don't create the damp environment that mold needs to grow. You do need to oil it occasionally if you want it to last for years, but the maintenance is genuinely minimal compared to fabric mats.

The best part is that a good teak mat turns your shower into something that feels like a spa. Here are seven worth buying, all under $40.

## Quick Tips for Teak Mat Maintenance

- **Let it dry between uses** — Stand it upright or lean it against the wall once a week so both sides air out completely.
- **Oil it every 6-12 months** — Teak oil or tung oil applied with a rag keeps the wood from drying out and graying.
- **Clean with a soft brush** — A gentle scrub with mild soap and water removes any soap scum without damaging the wood.
- **Don't leave it sitting in pooled water** — Teak handles moisture beautifully but standing water for days on end will eventually cause warping.
- **Let it lighten naturally** — Raw teak turns a silver-gray over time if left untreated, which is also beautiful. Oiling maintains the warm brown color.
