There's nothing more annoying than opening a kitchen cabinet and watching your shelf liner bunch up, slide forward, or roll into a tube every time you pull out a pot. You bought it to protect your shelves and keep things tidy, and instead it's created a new problem. The issue is usually one of two things: the liner is too thin to stay flat, or the grip material on the bottom is decorative rather than functional.

After comparing six popular shelf liners on actual kitchen shelves (painted wood, wire, and laminate), I can tell you that material thickness and grip pattern matter more than brand name. The best ones use a textured grip on both sides, so the liner sticks to the shelf and your dishes stick to the liner. Here are the six that actually earned their spot in your cabinets.

## Quick Tips

- Measure your shelves before ordering. Most liners come in 12 inch widths, but standard kitchen shelves are often 11 or 14 inches. You'll need to trim to fit.
- For the best grip, wipe shelves with rubbing alcohol before laying liner. Dust and grease underneath are the main reason liners slide.
- Replace shelf liners every 1-2 years. The grip material breaks down over time, especially in humid environments near the dishwasher or under the sink.
- If a liner keeps curling at the edges, use a hair dryer on low heat for 30 seconds to soften it and press it flat.
