## Quick Tips for Buying Measuring Sets

- **Engraved beats printed.** If a set is under $10, the markings are almost certainly painted or printed on. Engraved markings last the life of the tool.
- **Stainless for dry, glass for liquid.** Stainless cups are great for dry ingredients; a liquid measuring cup with a pour spout is a separate tool entirely. A good set covers both.
- **Check if the sizes actually match the recipe you use most.** Standard baking recipes need 1/4, 1/3, 1/2, and 1 cup. If you frequently cook international recipes, look for sets that include metric measurements too.
- **The ring or holder matters.** Sets that come apart and scatter in a drawer become a daily annoyance. A stacking ring or handle loop keeps the set together.
- **Spoon depth is different from cup size.** Long, shallow spoons are better for reaching into spice jars. Deep, short spoons are easier for liquids. Most affordable sets land somewhere in the middle.

A good measuring set costs less than a latte and lasts a decade. It is one of those quiet kitchen upgrades that makes everyday cooking slightly more pleasant without making a fuss about it.
