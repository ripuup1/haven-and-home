If you are a hot sleeper, you know the routine — kicking off blankets at 2 AM, flipping the pillow to the cool side, waking up stuck to your sheets. A new mattress with cooling technology can run $800 to $2,000. A cooling gel mattress pad does essentially the same job for under $50 and goes right on top of whatever mattress you already have.

These pads work by either infusing gel into memory foam (which absorbs and redistributes body heat) or using breathable fabrics like bamboo that wick moisture away from your skin. The best ones combine both approaches. The difference on the first night is noticeable — you sleep cooler, you stay asleep longer, and you wake up without that overheated, groggy feeling.

All six of these are queen-size prices under $50, with most available in other sizes too.

## Quick Tips

- Let a new memory foam topper off-gas for 24-48 hours before sleeping on it — the initial smell is normal and harmless but not pleasant
- Pair any cooling pad with breathable sheets (cotton percale or bamboo) for the best results — flannel sheets will cancel out the cooling effect
- Wash cooling pads on a gentle cold cycle every 1-2 months to maintain the moisture-wicking properties
- If you share a bed, consider a split topper (two twins on a king frame) so each person can choose their own thickness and firmness
