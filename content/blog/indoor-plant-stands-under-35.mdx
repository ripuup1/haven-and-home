That same pothos elevated 18 inches on a mid-century wood stand looks like a design decision. The elevation also actually helps your plants, since most houseplants prefer good airflow and indirect light, and getting them off the floor helps both.

The problem is that plant stands are shockingly expensive at most home stores. We're talking $80 to $150 for basic wood pedestals at places like West Elm or CB2. ## Quick Tips for Styling Plant Stands

- **Vary the heights.** Mix tall and short stands to create visual movement. A room full of plants at the same height looks like a store shelf.
- **Terracotta and wood go together naturally.** If you're using wooden stands, terracotta pots in earthy tones almost always look better than white plastic.
- **Group in odd numbers.** Three or five plants together look more designed than two or four. The "rule of threes" is a design cliche because it works.
- **Trail downward.** Stands look best when the plant actually spills slightly below the shelf edge — it gives the whole display more life.
- **Consider saucers.** Especially for wood stands, always use drainage saucers. Water damage to wood stands is the number one reason they need early replacement.
