Light leaking into your bedroom is one of the most underrated sleep killers. Even small amounts of ambient light — from streetlamps, early sunrise, a neighbor's porch light — suppress melatonin production and fragment your sleep cycles. If you have ever wondered why you sleep so much better in a hotel room, blackout window coverings are usually the answer.

Blackout roller shades are the cleanest, most modern solution. They roll up neatly when you want light, pull down for total darkness, and look intentional rather than makeshift. The best part? The no-drill options install with adhesive brackets or tension mounts, so renters and commitment-phobes can both relax.

These six picks are all under $40, fit standard window sizes, and actually block light effectively — not the "blackout" label that still lets in a glow around the edges.

## Quick Tips

- Measure your window width at three points (top, middle, bottom) and use the narrowest measurement for inside-mount shades
- For maximum blackout, choose a shade 3-4 inches wider than your window and mount it outside the frame to cover light gaps
- Cordless shades are safer for homes with children and pets — no dangling cords to worry about
- Layer a sheer curtain over your roller shade for daytime privacy without total darkness
