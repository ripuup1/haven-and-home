The only thing standing between you and that scenario is usually logistics: where does the wine glass go? Where do you put your phone without risking a catastrophic drop? And how is a book supposed to stay dry across from running water?

A bamboo bath tray caddy solves all of it. These simple wooden trays rest across the width of your bathtub and hold everything you need for a real spa night at home. They run $20 to $35, come with built-in slots for glasses, books, phones, and candles, and many are expandable to fit most tub widths. We found six of the best under $35 so you can spend your money on the bath products instead.

## What Makes a Good Bamboo Bath Tray?

**The most important feature is fit — a bath tray that does not span your tub securely is a hazard, not a luxury.** Most quality bamboo trays are expandable, stretching from about 27 inches to 32 or 34 inches to fit standard tubs. Measure your tub's interior width before ordering if you are unsure.

Beyond fit, look for trays with built-in wine glass holders (a slot that cradles the stem), a book or tablet stand, and waterproof bamboo treatment that will hold up to humidity over time. Natural bamboo is naturally moisture-resistant, but cheaper trays may use untreated wood that warps.

## Expandable Caddies for Deeper Soaks

**If your bathtub is deeper or wider than standard, or if you just want extra stability, look for caddies with extended side arms that grip the tub walls rather than simply resting across.**

The difference between a caddy that rests flat versus one with side arms is how secure it feels when you reach for something. A flat tray can slide; arms that grip the sides stay put. Both styles work, but the arm design is more stable.

## Quick Tips for Bamboo Bath Tray Care

- Dry your tray after each use and let it air completely before storing — bamboo held in standing water will eventually warp even with a good sealer
- Apply a light coat of food-safe mineral oil once a year to keep the bamboo moisturized and resistant to cracking
- Do not submerge your tray or put it in the dishwasher; a damp cloth is all you need to clean it
- Most expandable trays store compactly folded in a cabinet or closet — hang it on a hook for easy access between bath nights
