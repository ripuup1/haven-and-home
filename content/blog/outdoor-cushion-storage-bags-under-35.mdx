Every spring it happens: you drag your patio cushions out of wherever you shoved them last fall, and — yep — there's the musty smell, maybe a mildew spot, and that depressing puff of dusty air. If you're storing cushions in plastic grocery bags, old pillowcases, or just loosely stacked in the garage, you're setting yourself up for disappointment every single season.

A proper outdoor cushion storage bag is waterproof, breathable enough to prevent mildew, and built to handle the weight and bulk of patio furniture cushions. The good news: you don't have to spend a fortune. These picks are all under $35 and will protect your cushion investment for years to come. Think of it as cheap insurance against having to replace your patio set cushions every other year.

## What Makes a Good Outdoor Cushion Storage Bag?

**The material matters most — look for 420D or higher Oxford fabric, heavy-duty polyester, or 600D canvas.** These fabrics are waterproof or water-resistant and hold up to UV exposure, mold, and the general abuse of outdoor storage. Avoid flimsy bags made of thin plastic or light nylon — they'll tear within a season.

Also check the zipper: a sturdy double-pull zipper with a weather-resistant closure will keep moisture out better than a simple single pull. Handles are a nice bonus since cushions are heavy and awkward to lug around. Finally, think about size — most standard patio chair cushions fit in a 40-inch to 50-inch bag, but if you have a chaise lounge or sectional cushions, you'll want something 60 inches or longer.

## Quick Tips

- Brush off any dirt or debris from cushions before storing — debris traps moisture
- Let cushions dry completely in the sun for a few hours before bagging to prevent mildew
- Store bags upright or flat in a cool, dry spot — a garage shelf beats a damp basement
- Label your bag with a tag or marker so you know which cushions are inside
- Measure your largest cushion before buying a bag — when in doubt, size up

A good storage bag is the difference between patio cushions that last a decade and ones that need replacing every few years. Found a size or style that works for your setup? Pin this for later so you can find it when spring cleanup rolls around!
