The back of every door in your home is unused real estate. Think about it: your pantry door, bathroom door, closet doors, bedroom door. You are using space that literally did not exist before. Here are seven over-the-door organizers that solve real storage problems in every room of your home.

## Quick Tips for Over-the-Door Organizers

- **Check your door thickness before buying.** Most over-the-door organizers fit standard 1.5 to 1.75-inch doors, but hollow-core doors, French doors, and some older doors are different thicknesses.
- **Add adhesive felt pads** to the back of any organizer where it touches the door. This prevents scratches and reduces the banging noise when the door opens and closes.
- **Do not overload.** Over-the-door organizers have weight limits. Exceeding them can bend the hooks, damage the door, or cause the whole thing to fall off. Distribute weight evenly across shelves.
- **Use S-hooks to add hanging storage** to wire rack-style organizers. Small S-hooks on the bottom shelf give you extra hanging space for measuring cups, pot holders, or small bags.
- **Consider what is behind the door.** Make sure the organizer does not interfere with the door closing fully. Test it before loading it up.

The backs of your doors are the most underutilized storage space in your home. One over-the-door organizer on your pantry, bathroom, and bedroom closet doors adds the equivalent of a small bookshelf worth of storage without taking up a single square inch of floor space.
