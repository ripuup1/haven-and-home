A good candle does more for a room than most people give it credit for. It's not just about the smell. It's the warm glow, the way it makes you slow down for a second, the fact that lighting one at 6 PM on a Tuesday somehow makes your apartment feel like a place you actually want to be. I've gone through more candles than I'd like to admit, and the difference between a $6 Amazon candle and a $6 gas station candle is enormous. Here are the ones actually worth buying.

If you've never tried a soy candle set, this is a great entry point. You get six different scents in one box, which means you can rotate based on mood or room. The lavender is genuinely calming (not that fake purple cleaner smell), and the vanilla bourbon is warm without being cloying. They burn clean and last about 20 hours each. The only downside is the tins are small, so they won't fill a large living room with scent. Best for bedrooms, bathrooms, and smaller spaces.

These are dripless and burn evenly, which is surprisingly hard to find in pillar candles. I keep a set of three on my dining table year-round. They add warmth to a room without competing with whatever you're cooking for dinner.

If you love candles but hate the soot, a candle warmer lamp might be your thing. It melts the wax using a halogen bulb from above, releasing the scent without ever lighting a wick. No smoke, no soot, and your candles last significantly longer because the wax melts evenly instead of burning away. It looks surprisingly chic too, almost like a little desk lamp. The downside is you lose that flickering flame ambiance. But for scent alone, this method is actually better than burning.

The best candle advice I can give is to have different types for different purposes. Scented soy for the bedroom. A crackling woodwick for the living room. Unscented pillars for the dining table. It's a small thing that makes your whole home feel more intentional. And at these prices, there's no reason not to have a few going at once.
