Switching from bottled water to a filter pitcher is one of those changes that pays for itself almost immediately. A single Brita pitcher replaces roughly 300 standard water bottles over the life of one filter. That's real money back in your pocket and a lot less plastic in the landfill.

But not all filter pitchers are created equal. Some just improve taste by reducing chlorine. Others actually remove lead, mercury, microplastics, and PFAS chemicals. The difference matters, especially if your municipal water supply has known contaminant issues. Here are five pitchers under $35 that range from basic taste improvement to serious filtration, so you can pick the level of protection that makes sense for your water.

## What's the Best Overall Water Filter Pitcher?

**The Brita Everyday Elite 10-Cup Pitcher ($30) is the best all-around option. It removes 99% of lead, reduces chlorine taste and odor, and the 10-cup capacity means you're not constantly refilling it for a family of two to four.**

Brita earned its reputation for a reason. The Elite filter (formerly Longlast) lasts 6 months or 120 gallons, which is three times longer than their standard filter. That longer lifespan brings the cost per gallon down significantly. The pitcher itself has a comfortable handle, a flip-top lid for easy refilling, and an electronic filter indicator.

## Quick Tips for Water Filter Pitchers

- Always soak new filters for 15 minutes and run two full pitchers through before drinking
- Set a phone reminder to replace filters on schedule — an exhausted filter can actually add contaminants back
- Keep your pitcher in the fridge even if you prefer room temperature water (cold slows bacterial growth)
- Check your local water quality report at ewg.org/tapwater to know which contaminants to prioritize
