There's nothing that makes a living room look more finished than a TV mounted on the wall with zero visible cords. It's one of those upgrades that seems like it should cost a lot, but honestly, the hardware to do it well is shockingly affordable. The hard part is knowing which pieces you actually need — and which ones are overpriced for no reason.

Here's the thing: a wall mount itself is only part of the equation. The real magic happens when you pair it with a cord management raceway that runs along the baseboard or up the wall. Together, these two things transform a tangle of cables into a completely clean installation. And you can pull it off for well under $80 total — often closer to $40-50 if you're strategic.

## What Full Motion TV Mount Actually Lets You Do

**A full-motion (also called articulating) TV mount is worth every penny over a flat mount because it lets you tilt, swivel, and pull the screen away from the wall to reach cables or adjust the angle from different seats.**

For most living rooms, a good full-motion mount in the $25-35 range handles TVs from 26 in. up to 60 in. and holds anywhere from 60 to 100 lbs. Always check the VESA pattern on the back of your TV before ordering — it's the screw hole spacing — but most mounts accommodate multiple VESA sizes.

## Quick Tips for a Clean TV Wall Mount Install

- **Stud-find first:** Always mount your TV bracket into wall studs, not just drywall anchors — a 55 in. TV is too heavy to risk it.
- **Buy extra cord cover:** Cord raceways are cheap, so buy more than you think you need. It's better to have extra than to run short 3 feet from the outlet.
- **Paint the raceway before installing:** If you're painting the cord cover to match your wall, it's much easier to paint it flat on a table than after it's adhered to the wall.
- **Plan the cable route before drilling:** Trace the path from the TV to the outlet and measure twice before you commit to mounting holes.
- **Use a magnetic stud finder:** Spring-loaded stud finders are notoriously unreliable — a magnetic one (under $10) is far more accurate.
