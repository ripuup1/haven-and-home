Spring closet cleanouts get abandoned for one reason: you pull everything out, get overwhelmed, and shove it all back in a slightly different arrangement. The right tools make the difference between a project that sticks and one that resets itself by July.

These eight picks under $25 are the ones that do the actual work — compressing bulky off-season items, dividing shelves so sweaters stay stacked, and giving storage bags a label system so you can actually find things in October.

## What Should You Actually Buy for a Closet Cleanout?

**Start with vacuum storage bags for anything you're storing seasonally.** After that, add shelf dividers to keep stacks from toppling, and fabric bins for items that need a home on the shelf. The cedar blocks are a bonus — they protect stored wool and cashmere so you're not dealing with moth damage next fall.

One rule worth following: only bring in new organizational products after you've removed anything you don't want. Organizing clutter is just rearranging the problem.

## Quick Tips for a Spring Closet Cleanout

- Work in 45-minute blocks — full-day projects collapse under their own weight
- Put on a podcast or playlist before you start; the right audio makes sorting tolerable
- Create three sections immediately: keep, donate, and seasonal storage
- Label every storage bag before you seal it — not after, when you're tired
- Take a photo of each stuffed storage bag so you can remember what's inside without reopening it
- Get vacuum bags out before fall, not when you're looking for the sweaters you put away
