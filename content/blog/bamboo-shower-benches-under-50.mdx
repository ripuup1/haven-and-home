## What Makes Bamboo a Good Choice for a Shower Bench?

**Bamboo is one of the most moisture-resistant natural materials available, making it significantly better for shower environments than regular wood, which warps and rots with persistent moisture exposure.** It is also harder than most hardwoods, lightweight, and grows quickly — so it is both durable and sustainable.

The key quality markers to look for are: a waterproof sealant finish (most bamboo shower pieces have this, but verify), non-slip feet to prevent movement on wet tile, and solid joinery at the legs and shelf supports. Avoid any bamboo furniture with thin slats that look like they would snap under weight. A good bamboo bench should easily hold 200 to 300 pounds and feel solid when you press down on it with both hands.

## Quick Tips for Bamboo Shower Benches

- Apply a fresh coat of food-safe mineral oil or bamboo conditioning oil every 6 months to extend the life of your bench and prevent cracking
- Keep the bench slightly away from the direct spray zone if possible — natural wood does better with steam than with continuous direct water contact
- Place non-slip bath mat underneath the bench feet if you have very smooth tile — even benches with rubber feet can shift on a wet, perfectly smooth floor
- Bamboo benches make excellent plant stands for moisture-loving plants like pothos or ferns near a bathroom window — the spa aesthetic extends beyond the shower
