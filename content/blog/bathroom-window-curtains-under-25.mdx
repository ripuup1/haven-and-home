You want privacy in your bathroom without making it feel like a cave. That's the challenge with bathroom windows — you need something that blocks the view from outside but still lets in some natural light. And you definitely don't want to spend a lot on something that's going to get splashed with water and steam every single day.

The good news is there are actually great options under $25 that look way more expensive than they are. Whether your window is over the tub, next to the sink, or a small frosted window near the toilet, one of these will work. Here's what I've found that actually holds up.

## What Makes a Good Bathroom Curtain?

**The key things to look for: waterproof or moisture-resistant fabric, easy-care machine washing, and the right length for your window.**

Most bathroom windows are smaller than living room windows, so you'll often want a cafe-style panel (around 24–36 inches) or a tier curtain rather than a full-length drape. Look for polyester or a poly-blend fabric — these resist humidity and dry fast. And check the rod pocket size before you order, because a lot of bathroom windows use thin tension rods.

Here are seven options I'd actually put in my own bathroom.

## Quick Tips for Bathroom Window Curtains

- Measure your window before ordering — bathroom windows are often non-standard sizes
- A tension rod inside the window frame gives a cleaner, more built-in look than a bracket rod
- Wash curtains every 4–6 weeks to prevent mildew smell — polyester dries fast in the dryer
- For windows facing a neighboring house, combine a sheer curtain with frosted window film for layered privacy
- White and cream stay fresh-looking longest; dark colors show soap scum more
