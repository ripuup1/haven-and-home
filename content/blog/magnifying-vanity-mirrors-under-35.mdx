## What Magnification Do You Actually Need?

**For everyday makeup application — foundation, blush, mascara — 5x is a sweet spot. For brow shaping, tweezing, or detailed liner work, 10x is transformative. Most people end up wanting 10x.**

One thing to keep in mind: the higher the magnification, the smaller the field of view. A 10x mirror shows you a very small area at once, so you'll move it around more. That's a feature, not a bug — it forces you to slow down and pay attention to each section of your face. If you want something you can use at arm's length for a full-face check, look for dual-sided mirrors that offer 1x on one side and 5x or 10x on the other.

## Quick Tips for Magnifying Vanity Mirrors

- **Distance matters:** most 10x mirrors are designed to be used 8 to 12 inches from your face. Back up slightly if everything looks blurry.
- **Natural light first:** if you have a window in your bathroom, position your mirror to use it. Even the best LEDs can't beat actual sunlight for foundation matching.
- **Suction cups need clean glass:** if your mirror or tile has any hairspray or product residue on it, the suction cup won't hold. Wipe it down with rubbing alcohol first.
- **Don't apply all makeup at 10x:** use the magnified side for brows, liner, and detail work only. Switch to 1x for blush and bronzer placement, or you'll over-apply.
- **For skincare too:** a 10x mirror is a game changer for seeing exactly where you missed sunscreen, or confirming that retinol is actually absorbing instead of sitting on top.
