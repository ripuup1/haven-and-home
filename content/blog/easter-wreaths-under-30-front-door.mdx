There's something genuinely delightful about walking up to a front door that's been dressed up for Easter. A pretty wreath signals that spring has officially arrived at your home — it's welcoming, cheerful, and sets the whole seasonal tone before anyone even knocks. The best part? You absolutely do not need to spend a fortune to make it happen.

Easter wreaths have come a long way from plastic eggs on a wire frame. The options on the market now feature lush hydrangeas, elegant bunny silhouettes, woven farmhouse styles, and even faith-inspired designs with wooden crosses. Whether your front door aesthetic leans cottage-cozy or modern-minimalist, there's a spring wreath here for you — and every single one is under $30.

These picks are ideal for anyone who wants to refresh their entryway for Easter and Passover season without investing in something they'll store for 11 months of the year. Hang it the first week of April, enjoy it through Mother's Day, then tuck it away until next year.

## Quick Tips for Displaying Your Easter Wreath

- **Use a wreath hanger, not a nail.** An over-the-door wreath hanger (usually $5-$10) avoids putting holes in your door and makes it easy to swap the wreath each season.
- **Protect it from rain.** Most artificial wreaths can handle light moisture but look their best under a covered porch or with a storm door between them and the elements.
- **Mix it with a door mat.** A spring-themed doormat paired with a coordinating wreath doubles the seasonal impact without much extra effort.
- **Hang indoors too.** Above a fireplace mantel, in a window, or on an interior door — Easter wreaths aren't just for the front door.
- **Store properly.** A large zip-lock bag or wreath storage box keeps it dust-free and maintains the shape for next year.

Spring only comes once a year, and your front door is the first thing every visitor sees. A little floral cheer goes a long way.
