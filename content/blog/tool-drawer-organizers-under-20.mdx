## What Makes a Great Tool Drawer Organizer?

**The best tool drawer trays are modular, non-slip, and deep enough to actually hold your tools upright without tipping.**

A flat tray with shallow compartments sounds good in theory, but screwdrivers and pliers just slide around and tip over. Look for organizers that come in multiple sizes so you can customize the layout to your specific drawer dimensions. Foam-lined or rubberized bottoms are a huge bonus — they keep the trays from shifting even when you yank a drawer open fast.

## Quick Tips for Organizing Your Tool Drawer

- **Sort before you organize.** Before placing trays, pull everything out and toss what you don't need. Organizers can't save you from expired junk.
- **Group by task, not type.** Instead of all screwdrivers together, try keeping a small multi-tool drawer for the jobs you do most often — one screwdriver, a utility knife, measuring tape, and a few often-used bits.
- **Label the trays** if the drawer is shared. A quick piece of masking tape with a label saves everyone the "where does this go?" conversation.
- **Measure before you buy.** Check the interior dimensions of your drawer and compare them to the total footprint of the tray set before ordering.
- **Line the drawer first.** A thin shelf liner underneath the trays adds extra grip and makes the whole setup feel more polished.

The junk drawer doesn't have to stay a junk drawer. An afternoon, a $15-$20 investment, and a little sorting is all it takes to turn chaos into a workspace you'll actually enjoy using.
