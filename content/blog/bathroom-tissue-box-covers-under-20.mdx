The cardboard tissue box on your bathroom counter is doing absolutely nothing for the aesthetic of the space. And yet it's also a fixture — you need tissues, so there it sits. A tissue box cover is one of the fastest and cheapest ways to make a bathroom feel pulled together, because it replaces the one thing that consistently breaks the visual harmony of an otherwise nice counter.

Water hyacinth, rattan, woven paper rope, and linen are all having a moment in bathroom decor right now — and most of the best options are well under $20. Here's what's worth buying.

## Why Tissue Box Covers Make a Bigger Difference Than They Should

**A decorative tissue box cover does one specific thing really well: it makes your bathroom counter look intentional instead of accidental.** When everything on the counter is chosen — soap dispenser, candle, small plant, tissue cover — the space reads as designed. When the standard cardboard box is sitting out, it creates visual noise that undermines even nice towels and fixtures.**

The best covers fit standard cube tissue boxes (the kind most popular brands use, approximately 5x5x5 in.), have an open or easy-access bottom, and stay put without falling over when you pull a tissue. That last point is more important than it sounds — a cover that tips every time you grab a tissue gets replaced quickly.

## Quick Tips for Choosing the Right Tissue Box Cover

- Measure your tissue box before buying — standard cube boxes are about 5x5x5 in., but some boutique brands run slightly larger or smaller
- Open-bottom designs are more practical than hinged-lid styles for everyday use — you can reload in seconds
- If your bathroom has a very light palette, natural rattan or whitewash adds warmth without color-matching stress
- Woven covers hide dust better than smooth surfaces and only need a quick shake or light brush to clean
- Matching your tissue cover to one other textural element in the bathroom — a woven basket, a jute bath mat — makes the room feel deliberately styled
