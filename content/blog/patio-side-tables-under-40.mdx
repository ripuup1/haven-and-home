## Quick Tips for Patio Side Tables

- **Buy one more than you think you need.** You'll use them all. Extra side tables end up holding candles, plants, throw pillows, and all the outdoor accessories that otherwise pile up on chairs and the floor.
- **Use a small tray on top.** A 10-inch round tray corrals the items on a side table surface, keeps things from blowing off in wind, and makes the setup look intentional.
- **A side table at arm height is more useful than one at floor height.** When choosing, hold your arm out as if you're reaching for a drink seated in your outdoor chair. That height (usually 18 to 22 inches) is the sweet spot.
- **If you're using it as a plant stand, add a saucer underneath the pot.** Water draining from a pot onto a metal table will eventually cause rust, even on powder-coated surfaces.
- **Round tables waste less space on a crowded patio than rectangular ones.** When square footage is limited, round is almost always the right choice for side tables.
