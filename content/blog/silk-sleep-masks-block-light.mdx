Most sleep masks fail at the one thing they are supposed to do: block light. You put one on, settle into your pillow, and immediately notice light leaking in around your nose, under the edges, or through the thin fabric itself. A silk sleep mask solves two problems at once — the natural silk fiber blocks light better than synthetic materials, and the smooth surface is genuinely beneficial for the delicate skin around your eyes.

The difference between a good silk mask and a cheap one comes down to three things: the momme weight of the silk (higher is thicker and more light-blocking), the contour around the nose bridge, and the elastic tension (too tight gives you a headache, too loose lets light in). Here are six masks that get all three right.

## Quick Tips

- Hand-wash silk masks in cool water with gentle detergent to maintain softness
- If you wear the mask every night, have two so you can rotate while one dries
- Look for momme weight of 19 or higher for the best light-blocking and durability
- Adjust the strap so the mask is snug around your nose bridge — that is where most light leaks in
