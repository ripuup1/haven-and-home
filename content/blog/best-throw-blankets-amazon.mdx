I currently own nine throw blankets. My husband thinks this is excessive. I think he's wrong. Because here's what he doesn't understand: you don't just pick one throw and call it done. Different seasons need different weights, different textures, different vibes entirely. The chunky knit that's perfect draped over the couch in November would be suffocating in July. The lightweight muslin you love in summer looks flimsy and sad in January. Throws are seasonal, and once you accept that, everything clicks.

Beyond warmth, a throw is one of the easiest ways to style a couch. Folded in thirds and draped over one arm. Casually rumpled in a corner. Layered over a solid-colored pillow. It adds dimension, texture, and that lived-in feeling that makes a living room actually inviting instead of just clean. So yes, you need more than one. Here are five that I've personally tested across multiple seasons, and they're all on Amazon right now.

## The Seasonal Throw Rotation

Here's how I think about it:

- **Spring/Summer**: The muslin cotton blanket takes center stage. Light, breezy, effortless.
- **Fall**: Swap to the chunky knit or the waffle weave. Add the warmth and texture back in.
- **Winter**: Layer the sherpa throw on top of the waffle weave for maximum coziness. Keep the weighted throw nearby for evenings.
- **Year-round**: The waffle weave cotton lives on the couch permanently. It just works.

Total for all five throws is about $135, which sounds like a lot until you realize that's less than one designer throw from West Elm. And you'll actually use all of them.

My honest recommendation if you're only buying one? Start with the waffle weave cotton. It's the most versatile, it looks great in every room, and it'll get you through most of the year. Then add the chunky knit for winter and the muslin for summer when you're ready to expand.
