## Are Magnetic Knife Strips Worth the Wall Space?

**A Stainless Steel Magnetic Knife Strip ($15, 4.5 stars, 14,200+ reviews) frees up your entire counter where a block used to sit. It holds knives securely with strong neodymium magnets, keeps blades visible for quick grabbing, and looks incredibly sleek mounted above your prep area.**

If you're short on counter space -- or you just like the look of a clean, uncluttered counter -- a magnetic strip is the way to go. They mount on the wall (usually above the counter or next to the stove), and your knives hang there like a professional kitchen display.

The magnet strength matters here. Cheap ones use weak ceramic magnets that can barely hold a butter knife. You want neodymium magnets -- they're strong enough that even a heavy chef's knife stays firmly in place. A 16-inch strip typically holds 6-8 knives comfortably with enough spacing that you're not knocking handles together.

One thing to know: you'll need to install it into a stud or use heavy-duty anchors. Don't try to hang this on drywall alone with the included hardware unless you want your knives clattering to the floor at 2 AM and scaring the entire household.

## Quick Tips for Knife Storage

- **Never put good knives in the dishwasher.** The heat and jostling dulls blades fast. Hand wash, dry immediately, and store properly.
- **Magnetic strips should be mounted at least 18 inches above the counter** so you don't accidentally bump knife handles while prepping food.
- **If you choose a block, clean the slots** by turning it upside down and shaking out crumbs every month or so. A pipe cleaner works great for deep cleaning.
- **For drawer organizers, line the bottom with a shelf liner** so the organizer doesn't slide around every time you open the drawer.
- **Store knives blade-down in blocks** to protect the edge. Sliding a sharp blade against wood every time you pull it out is slowly dulling it.
