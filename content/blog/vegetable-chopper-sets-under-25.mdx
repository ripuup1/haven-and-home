## Quick Tips for Getting the Most From a Vegetable Chopper

- Cut vegetables into pieces that fit the chopper opening before pressing — most choppers work best with vegetables cut to roughly the size of the chute, not pushed in whole
- Chill onions in the freezer for 10 minutes before chopping to reduce the volatile compounds that cause eye irritation, regardless of whether you are using a chopper or knife
- Rinse blades immediately after use rather than letting food dry on them — dried vegetable residue is harder to remove and dulls blades faster
- Use the collection container as your mise en place bowl rather than transferring the chopped vegetables to a separate bowl — one less dish to wash
- Store extra blades in a labeled bag in the box or a small drawer pouch so they stay organized and do not create a loose-blade hazard in a utensil drawer
