There are few small annoyances more reliably irritating than sitting down to watch TV and spending two minutes looking for the remote. It's under a cushion. It's on the coffee table buried under a magazine. It slid between the armrest and the wall. And then there's the second remote, the third remote, the gaming controller, and someone's phone charger in the mix.

A couch caddy is the genuinely unglamorous solution that actually works. You clip or drape it over the arm of your sofa or recliner, and suddenly everything has a spot. The best ones hold multiple remotes, your phone, and maybe a magazine or two — all without looking terrible or getting in the way. And most are under $15.

## Do Couch Arm Caddies Actually Stay Put?

**The best armrest organizers use a non-slip rubberized lining on the contact surface, which grips upholstered fabric and leather reasonably well on most standard sofa arm widths.**

The honest answer is that no couch caddy will stay perfectly put if you're constantly shoving things in and pulling them out with force. But for a recliner or sofa arm that doesn't get a lot of jostling, a non-slip lining is usually enough. Wide armrests can cause issues with some designs — check product dimensions before buying.

The caddies with the most consistent reviews have pockets in different sizes: at least one large enough for a tablet or magazine, and smaller side pockets for remotes and phones. A few also include a detachable tray for snacks, which is either very practical or a slippery slope depending on your household.

## Quick Tips

- Measure your sofa armrest width before buying — most caddies fit standard 6 to 8 in. wide arms, but wide recliners or curved arms may not work with all designs.
- Go for a caddy with at least one pocket wide enough for a TV Guide or magazine — that's usually the largest item you'll store.
- If you have a leather sofa, look specifically for caddies with soft non-slip backing to avoid scuffing.
- Treat the caddy as a max of 3 to 4 items, not a dumping ground — it stays useful when it's not overstuffed.
