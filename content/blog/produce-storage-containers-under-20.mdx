You know the cycle: buy a beautiful bunch of strawberries, put them in the original plastic clamshell, and find them mushy and moldy three days later. Buy a bag of spinach and forget about it until it becomes a swamp in the back of the crisper drawer. The problem is not the produce — it is the storage. Most refrigerators are not actually designed to keep delicate fruits and vegetables in their ideal environment, and the original packaging makes things worse by trapping moisture against the food.

Produce storage containers with built-in drain trays solve this. They lift the food above any accumulated moisture, let air circulate, and create just enough humidity to slow down the wilting process without creating the wet, anaerobic environment that breeds mold. The ones below are all under $20 — most under $15 — and they genuinely extend the life of produce by several days.

## Quick Tips for Extending Produce Life

- **Wash produce just before storing, not before eating.** The exception is leafy greens, which benefit from being washed, spun dry, and stored with a paper towel — the towel absorbs moisture that would otherwise accelerate rot.
- **Keep ethylene-producing fruits separate.** Apples, bananas, avocados, and tomatoes release ethylene gas that speeds up ripening in nearby produce. Store them away from things like strawberries and leafy greens.
- **Set your refrigerator to 35-38 degrees Fahrenheit.** Many home refrigerators run slightly warm, which significantly shortens produce life. A cheap refrigerator thermometer is worth the $8 investment.
- **Don't overcrowd the containers.** Air needs to circulate around the produce for the drain tray system to work properly. Overfilling defeats the purpose.
- **Line the drain tray with a paper towel for extra-delicate items.** For particularly fragile berries, adding a paper towel on top of the colander creates a softer resting surface that prevents bruising.
