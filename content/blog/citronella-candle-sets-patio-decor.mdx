## Do Citronella Candles Actually Work?

**Yes — citronella candles do repel mosquitoes, but placement matters more than most people realize. The effective zone of a single candle is roughly 3 feet in calm conditions. Using two or three candles spaced around a seating area creates overlapping zones that cover the full space. In windy conditions, citronella dissipates faster, so placing candles in a more sheltered spot or using a windscreen improves effectiveness.**

The concentration of citronella oil varies significantly between products. Candles that list citronella as the primary scent note rather than a secondary one tend to be more effective. Soy-based candles burn cleaner and diffuse the scent more evenly than paraffin candles, which is why soy options appear frequently in this list. Burn time is also worth checking — a 6-ounce candle with a 12-hour burn time is very different from a 14-ounce candle with a 40-hour burn time.

## Quick Tips for Citronella Candles on the Patio

- Use two or three candles spaced around your seating area rather than one in the center for better coverage
- Trim wicks to 1/4 inch before each burn to prevent uneven burning and soot buildup
- A candle snuffer is worth having — blowing out a citronella candle in a jar creates a wax spray inside the jar that shortens its life
- Set candles on a tray or flat stone rather than directly on a wood table — the heat from a long burn can leave marks
- Pair citronella candles with a citronella plant or two in pots nearby for layered repellent coverage
