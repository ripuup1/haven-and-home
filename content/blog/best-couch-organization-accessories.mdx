You spend half your movie night reaching between cushions for the remote, and the other half balancing a drink on the arm of the sofa. These sofa accessories are specifically designed for living room life. They hold what you actually use on the couch without adding clutter or looking out of place. Here are the best options for keeping your couch zone organized.

## What Is the Best Couch Organizer for Remotes?

**The best couch organizer for remotes is an armrest caddy ($14) that drapes over the arm of your sofa. It has pockets for remotes, your phone, glasses, and a book or magazine. The weighted design stays in place without sliding off, and the felt lining protects the arm of your couch from scratches.**

An armrest caddy is the simplest solution because it requires zero installation and works on any couch. Just drape it over the arm and fill the pockets. Most caddies have 5-6 pockets in different sizes, so everything gets its own spot. When guests come over, you can pull it off and tuck it away.

## Is a C-Shaped Side Table Better Than an End Table?

A C-shaped side table slides under the couch, putting a sturdy surface right next to you without taking up floor space. The narrow base fits under most sofas, and the tabletop sits at the perfect height for grabbing a drink or setting down your phone. For [small living rooms](/blog/small-living-room-feel-bigger), this is a better option than a bulky end table.

These tables are also great for working from the couch. Slide a laptop onto the surface and you have an instant couch desk. When you're done, slide it back under and the floor space opens up again.

## How to Keep Your Couch Area Organized

The rule for couch organization is the same as any other surface: everything needs a designated home. Remotes go in the caddy or holder. Drinks go on the tray. Blankets get folded over the back or tucked in a basket nearby. When everything has a spot, tidying up takes thirty seconds between TV episodes.

Keep a small basket or bin next to the couch for blankets and throw pillows that migrate off the sofa. This prevents the "pile of pillows on the floor" look that makes any living room feel messy.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is the best way to organize remote controls on a couch?

The best way is an armrest caddy ($14) that drapes over the sofa arm with pockets for remotes, phone, and glasses. For a coffee table or end table solution, a faux leather remote holder with 5 slots ($12) keeps all remotes upright and visible in one spot.

### How do you keep drinks stable on a couch arm?

Use a bamboo couch cup holder tray ($22) with an anti-slip silicone base that grips round and flat armrests. The weighted design and grip bottom prevent sliding, and the cutout cup holders keep drinks secure. It's much more stable than balancing a mug directly on the armrest.

### What is a C-shaped side table?

A C-shaped side table ($35) has a base that slides under your couch so the tabletop hovers right next to you at armrest height. It provides a sturdy surface for drinks, snacks, and laptops without taking up floor space like a traditional end table. Most have wheels for easy repositioning.
