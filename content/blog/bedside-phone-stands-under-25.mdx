## Quick Tips for a Better Nightstand Charging Setup

- **Choose the phone stand before the charging cable, not after.** Different stands route cables differently. Some have a slot at the back, some at the bottom. Match the cable routing to where your outlet is relative to your nightstand to avoid a stretched or bent cable.
- **Use a short charging cable (1 to 2 feet).** Standard charging cables are 6 feet long, which creates a snake of excess cord on the nightstand and floor. A short cable keeps everything tidy and sits cleanly inside the cable routing slots on most stands.
- **Keep one slot for tomorrow, one for tonight.** A two-slot stand with an accessory tray naturally organizes the nightstand routine: phone in its slot charging, watch charging separately, tomorrow's essentials in the tray. Everything has a place so nothing piles up.
- **Wipe wood stands monthly.** Wood surfaces in bedrooms accumulate a fine layer of face oil, hand lotion, and dust. A quick wipe with a slightly damp cloth keeps them looking fresh. Avoid saturating the wood — just a damp cloth, then dry.
- **Position the stand on the far corner of the nightstand.** Keeping the phone at arm's length rather than directly next to where you sleep creates a small physical barrier to late-night scrolling. It sounds minor but the slight friction of reaching across makes a difference in sleep habits over time.

A nightstand that looks organized and calm is one of those environmental cues that supports better sleep. The phone stand is the small piece that makes the whole nightstand work as a system rather than just a surface.
