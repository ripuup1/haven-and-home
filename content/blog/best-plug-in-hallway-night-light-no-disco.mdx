Search "night light" on Amazon and you'll get bombarded with star projectors, color-changing bedroom mood lamps, and Bluetooth-controlled monstrosities marketed as nursery decor. None of them are what you actually need at 3 a.m. when you're trying to walk to the bathroom without stepping on a Lego.

A hallway night light has exactly one job. Light the floor. Don't blind the person walking past it. Turn on automatically. That's it. Here's how to find a good one — and the six picks that actually deliver.

## What to Look For in a Hallway Night Light

Most of the differences between hallway night lights come down to four features. Get these right and the rest is just aesthetics.

- **Motion sensor or dusk-to-dawn**: Motion-activated lights save energy and are kinder to sleep, but dusk-to-dawn lights are better for older family members who don't move quickly enough to trigger sensors. Decide which problem you're solving.
- **Warm vs cool color temperature**: Anything over 3000K (cool white) will wreck your night vision and signal "wake up" to your brain. Look for 2700K or warm amber. Some lights have an actual amber-only mode for the bathroom trip.
- **Plug position**: Standard outlets are 12-18 inches off the floor. A light that aims down at the floor works better than one that throws light upward into your eyes. Check the design before ordering.
- **Brightness levels**: Adjustable brightness matters more than maximum brightness. The light you want when you're awake is not the light you want when you're sleepwalking. Two or three levels is enough.

## How to Choose

Pick based on what you're actually trying to fix:

- **Old house, dark hallway, multiple family members**: Get the four-pack budget dusk-to-dawn lights. One in every hallway. Done in five minutes.
- **Light sleeper, single hallway**: Motion-sensing only — the constant glow of dusk-to-dawn will leak under doorways and disrupt sleep.
- **Long open hallway**: Directional optics, two lights minimum, spaced 8-10 feet apart.
- **Kids' rooms**: Motion-sensor with amber mode. Skip blue-tinted lights entirely.
- **Closets, stairs, weird spots**: The rechargeable stick-on. It goes places plug-ins can't.

The mistake people make is overbuying — picking a $40 smart light when a $13 four-pack would have actually solved the problem. Hallway lighting is plumbing. Boring is good. Found something you love? Pin this for later so you don't lose it!
