## Quick Tips for Getting the Most Out of Spring Candles

- **Burn new candles for at least 2 hours the first time.** This lets the wax melt all the way to the edges, which prevents tunneling and ensures you get the full burn time.
- **Trim the wick to about a quarter inch before each use.** Long wicks create soot and shorten burn time.
- **Match candle size to room size.** A 3-oz candle in a large open living room won't scent the space effectively. Save small candles for bathrooms and use 10+ oz candles for main living areas.
- **Layer citrus scents thoughtfully.** Lemon reads as energizing (good for kitchens, home offices), while grapefruit leans more spa-like (better for bathrooms), and lime with coconut feels more playful (good for a sunroom or patio area).
- **Store unused candles with their lids on** in a cool, dark place. Heat and light fade both the scent and the color of the wax.
