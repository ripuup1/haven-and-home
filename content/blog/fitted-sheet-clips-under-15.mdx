Fitted sheet clips are little elastic straps with clips on each end that attach to two corners of the sheet and run underneath the mattress, creating constant tension. They take about two minutes to install, and you'll wonder how you ever slept without them. The best part? Every option on this list is under $15.

Whether your sheets are too big for your mattress, you sleep hot and move around, or you've got a thick memory foam topper that makes tucking impossible, the right sheet clips will solve it.

## Why Do Fitted Sheets Come Off So Easily?

**The main culprits are mattress depth mismatches, low-thread-count elastic that loosens over time, and active sleepers who literally kick the sheet off.** Most modern mattresses run 10 to 14 inches deep, but many fitted sheets are only designed for 8 to 10 inches — so the elastic simply doesn't have enough grip. Sheet clips add mechanical tension that keeps the corners locked down no matter how much you toss and turn.

The style matters too. Corner-only clips (one clip per corner) work for most people. Criss-cross straps that run diagonally across the mattress provide more even tension. And if you have a particularly slippery mattress or topper combination, a set of 8 clips instead of 4 gives you extra insurance.

## Quick Tips for Using Sheet Clips

- **Install them diagonally**, not straight across the corner — the diagonal angle creates more even tension and keeps the clip from riding up.
- **Start with 4 clips** on the corners. Only upgrade to 8 if corners still pop after a few nights.
- **Adjust the length** so there's consistent tension but the clip isn't so tight it strains the sheet fabric.
- **Use on sofa covers too** — the same diagonal technique works perfectly on slipping couch cushion covers.
- **Replace elastic if it goes limp** — sheet clips are cheap enough that a fresh set every year or two makes sense if you notice the elastic losing its snap.
