Dry air in the bedroom is one of those problems that affects everything — you wake up with a scratchy throat, your skin feels tight, your lips are cracked, and you never quite feel rested. A good humidifier fixes all of that, but the wrong one creates new problems: loud fan noise that wakes you up, a water tank you have to refill every four hours, or a mist so heavy it leaves water spots on your nightstand.

The humidifiers below are specifically selected for bedroom use, which means quiet operation is the top priority, followed by tank size (you want it to last all night) and ease of cleaning. All are under $45 and available on Amazon.

## Quick Tips

- Clean your humidifier weekly with white vinegar to prevent mold and mineral buildup
- Use distilled water if you have hard water to avoid white mineral dust on your furniture
- Point the mist nozzle away from your bed, not directly at your face
- A humidity level of 40-50% is ideal for sleep — too high promotes mold growth
