Every living room needs a focal point, and a fireplace is one of the most natural options. But if your home doesn't have one (hello, apartments, newer builds, and ranch-style houses), you're not out of luck. Faux fireplace mantels give you the visual anchor of a real fireplace without tearing into walls, running gas lines, or dealing with chimney maintenance. They're decorative surrounds that lean against or mount to a wall and instantly make a room feel more put-together.

I've found six options under $150 that range from full freestanding surrounds to floating mantel shelves you can mount above an electric insert or even just a collection of candles. Some are under $50, which is less than a nice throw blanket. Whether you're going for farmhouse charm or sleek modern lines, there's a faux mantel here that'll transform your room.

## What's the Best Freestanding Faux Fireplace Surround?

**The White Freestanding Fireplace Surround ($129, 4.4 stars, 2,100+ reviews) is a full mantel-and-pillar frame that leans against the wall and creates an instant fireplace alcove. No tools, no mounting, no damage to your walls.**

This is the most dramatic option on the list. You get the full mantel shelf on top, two side pillars, and an open center where you can place candles, an electric insert, stacked birch logs, or whatever else suits your aesthetic. The whole thing is about 42 inches wide and 42 inches tall, which is realistic fireplace proportions. It's made from MDF with a white finish, so it won't fool anyone into thinking it's marble, but from across the room it creates exactly the cozy focal point you're after.

Assembly takes about 45 minutes, and then you just lean it against the wall. Some people secure it with a furniture strap for stability, which I'd recommend if you have kids or pets. At $129, it's the priciest option here but also the most transformative. This is the one that makes guests say "wait, you don't have a real fireplace?"

## Quick Tips

- Fill the firebox opening with pillar candles at varying heights for the most realistic flame-free look. Group 3-5 candles in cream or white.
- Stack birch logs in the opening for a year-round rustic look that requires zero maintenance.
- Use your faux mantel as seasonal decor central. Swap garland, pumpkins, flowers, and stockings throughout the year to keep the space feeling fresh.
