Here's the most overlooked real estate in any kitchen, pantry, or closet: the space underneath your shelves. That 4-to-6 inch gap between the bottom of a shelf and the items below it? That's usable storage you're probably walking past every day. Under-shelf baskets and hanging bins clip onto or slide under existing shelves and instantly give you a new storage layer without drilling a single hole.

This is one of those organizing moves that's hard to believe until you try it. You get a full extra row of storage in a kitchen cabinet, pantry, or closet without buying new furniture or rearranging everything. The items that work best in under-shelf storage: things you need visible but don't want taking up prime shelf real estate — foil and wrap rolls, spice packets, small sauce bottles, cleaning supplies, folded dish towels.

Here are the eight under-shelf organizers that actually work — plus some standard clear bins that maximize what's already on your shelves.

## Quick Tips for Under-Shelf and Clear Bin Organizing

- **Measure your shelf thickness before buying.** Most under-shelf baskets fit shelves up to 3/4 inch or 1 inch thick. Thicker shelves or ones with rounded edges may not work — check the product specs.
- **Use under-shelf baskets for daily-grab items.** The most convenient items should be at the easiest reach — and for daily-use items, under-shelf baskets provide that grab-and-go access.
- **Pair under-shelf baskets with labeled bins above.** A labeled bin on the shelf plus a basket underneath creates a two-tier system: primary storage above, overflow or frequent-access items below.
- **Don't overload the baskets.** Under-shelf baskets work best when they hold light, small items — not heavy cans or appliances. Keep them under a pound or two of contents.
- **Use the same brand or system throughout one zone.** Mixing different basket heights and sizes in one cabinet creates visual chaos. One consistent system looks organized even when it's full.
