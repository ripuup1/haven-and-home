## Quick Tips for Teacher Appreciation Gifts

- **Check with the room parent first** — coordinate so five families don't show up with the same candle.
- **Personalized items need lead time** — order at least a week before Teacher Appreciation Week, not the day before.
- **Useful beats cute** — things that get used every day outperform decorative items that sit on a shelf.
- **Group gifts work** — if multiple families pool $10-15 each, you can get something higher quality, like a nice restaurant gift card plus one of these practical items.
- **Card beats no card** — whatever you give, a handwritten note from the student is what teachers actually remember.

Teacher Appreciation Week only comes once a year. These six picks cost less than a restaurant lunch and will get used for the rest of the school year. That's a better ROI than another mug. Pin this for later so you don't lose it!
