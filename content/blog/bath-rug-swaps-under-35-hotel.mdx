Here's a stat that'll make you flip yours over and check the tag: the average bath rug is designed to last about four years before the backing breaks down and the pile flattens. The average person keeps theirs for closer to eight. Most bathrooms I walk into have a rug that is technically still functional but objectively sad, and the owner has been looking at it so long they stopped noticing.

Swapping a bath rug is the single cheapest high-impact bathroom upgrade you can make. A new $25 rug changes the way the whole room reads more than a $200 shower curtain ever will, because your eyes naturally drop to the floor when you walk into a small space. Here are five under-$35 picks that do the hotel-bathroom thing without spending hotel-bathroom money.

## Quick Tips

- Wash a new bath rug by itself the first time. The first wash sheds lint that will stick to everything else.
- Size matters. A 17 x 24 in. rug looks lost in front of a full-size tub. Upgrade to 20 x 32 in. for a better look.
- If your rug keeps sliding, the backing is dead. Throw it out and replace it. Rug grippers on bathroom rugs rarely work long-term.
- Hang bath rugs to dry instead of tumble-drying. The backing lasts 2-3x longer.
- Replace bath rugs every 3-4 years with daily use. After that the pile flattens and they stop absorbing water.

The cheapest and highest-impact thing you can do to your bathroom this month is replace the rug. Pick one that matches your lifestyle (plush for comfort, stone for low-maintenance, chenille for style) and budget a new one into the rotation every few years. Your feet, guests, and future photos will thank you.
