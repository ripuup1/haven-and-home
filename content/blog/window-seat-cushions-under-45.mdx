Most come in multiple sizes to fit bay windows, reading nooks, and built-in benches. Let's find the one that transforms your window into a destination.

## Quick Tips for Window Seat Cushion Success

- **Measure your window seat before ordering**, including depth. Most people measure the length and forget the depth — standard cushions are 14-16 in. deep, but many bay windows are 18-20 in. or more.
- **Consider your sun exposure.** South and west-facing windows get intense afternoon sun that will fade fabric faster. Look for cushions with fade-resistant covers, or plan to use a throw over it during peak sun hours.
- **Add a lumbar pillow.** Even a 4 in. thick bench cushion doesn't provide back support for extended sitting. A small lumbar pillow transforms your window seat from a perch into a proper reading spot.
- **Use Command strips to anchor your cushion** if you're finding even non-slip backings aren't enough. One strip on each corner prevents sliding entirely without damaging your window ledge.
- **Coordinate with your curtains**, not your wall color. Window seat cushions read as part of your window treatment, so matching or coordinating with your curtain fabric creates a more cohesive look than matching the wall or floor.

The right window seat cushion turns an underused architectural feature into one of your most-loved spots in the house. Whether you're looking for plush velvet, practical waterproof, or a custom size that finally fits, one of these options will get you there for well under $45.
