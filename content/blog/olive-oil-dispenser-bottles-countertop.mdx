## What About a Ceramic Olive Oil Dispenser?

Ceramic oil dispensers are the best option for protecting oil from light because they block it completely. They also add a warm, handmade feel to your countertop that glass can't replicate. This ceramic oil bottle has a narrow pour spout built into the design, so you don't need a separate spout attachment. It looks beautiful sitting next to your [styled open shelving](/blog/style-open-kitchen-shelving).

The one downside is you can't see how much oil is left. If that bothers you, stick with tinted glass. But if you prefer the look and don't mind lifting the bottle to gauge the weight, ceramic is the most stylish option.

## Do Weighted Pour Spouts Really Prevent Drips?

Yes. Weighted pour spouts use gravity to snap a small metal flap shut as soon as you tilt the bottle back upright. The weight at the end of the spout is what makes this work. Without it, regular pour spouts leave a drip trail every time. If your current oil dispenser leaves a ring on the counter, switching to weighted spouts solves it immediately.

These pour spouts fit most standard bottle openings and work great if you want to keep your oil in a decorative bottle you already own. They're also a good option if you want to convert a wine bottle or any narrow-necked bottle into a cooking oil dispenser.

## How to Style Oil Dispensers on Your Counter

Place your oil dispenser near the stove on a small tray or cutting board with your salt cellar and pepper grinder. This creates a "cooking station" that looks like a styled vignette instead of a random collection of bottles. If you have [pretty kitchen towels](/blog/kitchen-towels-linens-look-expensive) nearby, even better.

Keep only the oils you use daily on the counter. Store backup bottles in the pantry. The goal is a clean, functional counter with just what you need within arm's reach.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is the best container for olive oil on the counter?

The best container for olive oil on the counter is a dark-tinted glass cruet with a weighted auto-flip pour spout ($14). The dark glass protects oil from light degradation, and the weighted spout prevents drips. For complete light protection, choose a ceramic olive oil dispenser ($18) instead.

### Does olive oil go bad in a glass dispenser?

Olive oil can degrade in clear glass if exposed to direct sunlight. Use a dark-tinted or ceramic dispenser, keep it away from direct sunlight, and refill every two weeks from a larger stored bottle. This keeps the oil on your counter fresh and flavorful.

### Should I get an oil and vinegar set or just an oil dispenser?

Get the set if you use both regularly for cooking or salads. A matching oil and vinegar dispenser set with a rack ($22) looks more intentional on the counter than two mismatched bottles. If you only use olive oil, a single dispenser is all you need.
