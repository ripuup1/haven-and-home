The good news is you do not need to spend much to solve this problem well. The stands below are all under $25, and several come with tablet compatibility built in — which means they work just as well for a cooking video as they do for a printed recipe card. Here are the ones actually worth clearing counter space for.

## Quick Tips for Getting the Most from a Cookbook Stand

- **Measure your counter depth first.** Most stands work best with at least 6-8 in. of clear counter space between the stand and the wall. If you are working in a tight galley kitchen, a compact folding stand might serve you better than a fixed organizer.
- **Position it at arm's length from the stovetop.** Close enough to read easily, far enough that steam and splatters do not reach it. About 18-24 in. from the burners is the sweet spot for most ranges.
- **Use page weights or binder clips for thick books.** Even the best groove will not hold a heavy book open to page 300 without a little help. Two small metal binder clips on either side of the open pages work perfectly.
- **Clean it before you store it.** Even a light coating of cooking oil mist can build up over time. A weekly wipe-down keeps wood from discoloring and acrylic from clouding.
- **Consider height.** If you have ever crouched down to read a recipe at counter level, a stand that props the book at a slight upward angle changes everything.
