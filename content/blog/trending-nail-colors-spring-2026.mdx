# 15 Trending Nail Colors for Spring 2026

![Manicured nails in trendy colors](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1519014816548-bf5fe059798b?w=1200&q=80)

Every spring, the same safe pinks and corals cycle through. This year is different. The colors dominating Pinterest saves and Instagram nail accounts lean earthier, moodier, and more interesting than the usual pastel lineup. I've been testing these shades on myself and friends for the past month, and a few surprised me with how universally flattering they are.

The biggest shift for spring 2026 is that "clean girl" nails are evolving. Instead of barely-there nudes, the trend is moving toward soft color with personality. Think muted sage, dusty lavender, warm terracotta. Colors that still look polished at the office but have enough character to stand out in a photo.

## Lavender Isn't Going Anywhere

Lavender has been trending for two years straight, and spring 2026 keeps the momentum going. The version that's saving the most right now is a slightly greyed-out, dusty lavender rather than a bright purple. Think dried lavender flowers, not Easter eggs. This shade works across all skin tones, which is rare for purple family colors. Deeper skin tones look incredible in it. Fair skin gets a nice contrast without it looking jarring.

## Terracotta Is the New Nude

Here's the color I didn't expect to love. Terracotta sits somewhere between a warm nude, a burnt orange, and a brown. It sounds weird on paper. On nails, it looks like you spent serious money on a custom shade. This color especially suits warm and medium skin tones, giving a rich, earthy vibe that pairs with literally everything in your spring wardrobe. On fair skin, go lighter, more of a peachy terracotta to keep it wearable.

## Warm Nudes Still Win

Nudes will always trend, but the nudes for spring 2026 are warmer. Less grey, less cool, more caramel and honey. The goal is matching or slightly deepening your natural nail color for that "I just have perfect nails naturally" illusion. Finding your exact nude is personal, though. The rule of thumb: your ideal nude should be one or two shades darker than your inner wrist.

## Other Shades to Watch

Beyond the big five, keep an eye on butter yellow (subtle, not neon), chocolate brown (the fall holdover that still works), sky blue (classic spring energy), cherry mocha (a brownish red that photographs beautifully), and glazed donut chrome (the Hailey Bieber effect continues). The theme across all these trends is the same: muted, wearable, grown-up color. The days of neon accent nails and complicated nail art as the default are fading. Spring 2026 is about finding one perfect shade and wearing it well.

If you're picking just one color to try from this list, go with sage green. It's the shade that surprises everyone, and it'll still look fresh through summer.
