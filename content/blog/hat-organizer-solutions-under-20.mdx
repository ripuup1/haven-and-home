If you have ever reached into a closet shelf and pulled out a baseball cap that has been sitting crushed under three other hats for six months, you know exactly why hat organization matters. Caps are surprisingly hard to store well — they have a brim that needs to be kept in shape, a dome that collapses if stacked, and they tend to multiply faster than you can find space for them.

The good news is that there are several genuinely clever hat organization systems under $20, and the one that works best for you depends almost entirely on where your hats are going to live. Hanging closet organizers work well if you have vertical closet space. Wall racks are great for display-worthy hats. Over-door solutions maximize door space you are probably not using. And clear boxes protect prized caps from dust and crushing. Here are seven options that work.

## Which Hat Storage Style Is Right for You?

**Think about how many hats you have, whether you want them on display or hidden away, and whether you own the space (drilling is fine) or rent (no-damage options only).**

A collection of 5-10 caps fits neatly in a hanging closet organizer or a wall-mounted rack. More than 15-20 hats calls for a more systematic solution — either a large-capacity hanging organizer or dedicated storage boxes. If your hats are all baseball caps, the compartment-style hanging organizers are usually the most efficient use of space.

## Quick Tips for Hat Storage

- Store caps with the brim facing forward and slightly upward to maintain brim shape over time
- If a brim has been bent out of shape, stuff the hat with tissue paper and let it sit in a warm spot for a few hours to reform
- Hanging organizers keep caps more accessible than boxes for everyday-wear hats
- Collectible or less-frequently-worn caps benefit from box storage to protect them from dust
- Take stock of your collection twice a year — if you have not worn a hat in a year, consider donating it
