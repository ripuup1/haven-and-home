## Quick Tips for Getting the Most Out of a Hair Catcher

- **Clean it after every shower.** Yes, every shower. It takes five seconds and means the hair never builds up into a gross mat. If you let it go for a week, cleaning becomes unpleasant enough that you avoid it.
- **Match the catcher to your drain style.** Round drains need round catchers; square drains do better with square or wide-rim options. Measure your drain opening before buying if you are unsure.
- **Hair catchers work with conditioner.** A lot of people skip them when using conditioner or deep treatments, worrying the product will clog underneath. In reality, the catcher still does its job — just rinse thoroughly when you clean it.
- **Keep a spare.** These are inexpensive enough that having a backup makes sense. When one cracks or loses its grip, you can swap it immediately without waiting for shipping.
- **If you have long hair, empty it more often.** Long hair fills up a catcher faster than short hair. For households with long hair, a quick post-shower check is worth building into the routine.
