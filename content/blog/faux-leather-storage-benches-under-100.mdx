A storage bench is one of those pieces of furniture that quietly does about four jobs at once. It gives you a seat, it hides your clutter (blankets, out-of-season pillows, shoes, whatever needs to disappear), and it looks intentional — like you planned to have it there. Faux leather in particular is the smart choice if you have kids or pets because it wipes clean in seconds and holds up far better than fabric over time.

These six picks are all under $100 and cover the most common use cases: end-of-bed, entryway, and living room ottoman. Most hold 300 to 500 pounds, come with safety hinges so the lid doesn't slam on fingers, and are simple enough to assemble in 20 minutes. All come in both black and brown, so there's a version for whatever your room is doing.

## How to Choose the Right Size Storage Bench

**For an end-of-bed bench, aim for a length that's about 60 to 80 percent of your mattress width — this gives you balance without the bench looking stubby or oversized.**

For a queen bed (60 inches wide), that means a bench between 36 and 48 inches long is ideal. For a king, you can go up to 55 inches. If it's going in an entryway, measure your wall first — you want at least 12 inches of clearance on each side so the bench doesn't block traffic flow. For a living room ottoman, match it to your sofa's depth for a proportional look.

## Quick Tips for Styling a Storage Bench

- Add a folded throw blanket or two on top for a layered, cozy look that also makes the storage purpose feel intentional
- In an entryway, pair the bench with a small tray on top for keys and mail to keep the surface organized
- A bench at the foot of the bed works hardest when it's the same length as the mattress footboard — anything shorter looks like it wandered in by accident
- Choose black if your space is modern or contemporary; choose brown if it's farmhouse, transitional, or warm-toned
