## Quick Tips for Laundry Sorting Carts

- **Label the bags from day one.** Whether you use the included labels or a piece of tape with a marker, clear labels prevent your sorting system from devolving into "everything goes in whichever bag is closest."
- **Wash the bags monthly.** Even though they're holding dirty laundry, the bags themselves need cleaning to prevent odor and bacteria buildup. Most are machine washable.
- **Lock the wheels when loading.** Nothing is more annoying than a cart rolling away while you're trying to toss clothes into it. Use those locking casters.
- **Position the cart where laundry happens.** If clothes come off in the bedroom, put the cart in the bedroom — not the laundry room. Sort at the source, transport when full.
- **Don't overload sections.** It's tempting to keep stuffing clothes in until the bag can't hold any more. But overloaded bags are heavy, hard to remove, and strain the frame.

A good sorting cart turns laundry from a dreaded marathon into a quick, organized task. Pre-sorting saves 15-20 minutes on every wash day, which adds up to hours over a month. Pin this for later so you don't lose it!
