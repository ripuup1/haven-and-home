## Wall Mount vs. Freestanding: Which Is Better?

**Wall-mounted racks are almost always the better choice** — especially in garages and sheds where floor space is already tight. They keep tools off the floor entirely, eliminate the tripping hazard of handles propped against walls, and make tools immediately visible and accessible. The only reason to go freestanding is if you're renting and can't put holes in walls, or if you need something portable between locations.

Most wall-mount racks use standard wood screws into wall studs, or heavy-duty drywall anchors if you're mounting between studs. A stud finder and a level are all you need. Most installs take under 20 minutes.

## Quick Tips for Organizing Garden Tools

- Group tools by task: keep weeding tools together, planting tools together, and pruning tools together — it speeds up every trip to the garage
- Store sharp tools like hoes and edgers with blades pointing up so you can see them immediately and aren't reaching blindly near cutting edges
- Label sections with waterproof chalk markers or a label maker — especially helpful if multiple people use the space
- Hang tools at a consistent height so nothing sticks out at head level when you're walking through
- Clean soil off handles before hanging tools — dried mud eats into wood over time and makes handles uncomfortable to grip
