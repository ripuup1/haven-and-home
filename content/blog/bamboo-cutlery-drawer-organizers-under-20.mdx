## What to Look for in a Bamboo Drawer Organizer

**The most important feature is expandability.** A fixed-width tray might not fit your drawer. Look for one that adjusts from at least 13 in. to 22 in. wide so it works across different drawer sizes without sliding around. Removable dividers are a bonus — they let you customize the compartment layout for your specific mix of utensils.

Most bamboo trays come in natural light wood tones, but some go darker with staining. Both look great; just pick what matches your kitchen best.

## Quick Tips for Organizing Your Cutlery Drawer

- Before buying, measure the inside width of your drawer (not just the front) — depth from front to back matters too, since some trays are longer than others
- Do a drawer purge first: take everything out, toss the duplicates and mystery tools, and only put back what you actually use regularly
- For a really tidy look, group items by category rather than by set — all forks together, all spoons together, regardless of which set they're from
- Bamboo is naturally antimicrobial, but wipe it down regularly and avoid soaking — prolonged moisture can cause warping over time
- If your drawer is unusually wide, two organizers side by side can work better than one wide expandable tray
