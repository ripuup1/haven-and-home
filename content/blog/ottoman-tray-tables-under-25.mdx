An ottoman tray is one of those small purchases that makes a living room feel significantly more pulled together. The problem with a large upholstered ottoman is that without a tray, it just looks like a place for everything to pile up — remotes, magazines, half-finished drinks, whatever was in someone's hands when they sat down. A tray gives the surface a boundary. It defines where the decorative items live and where they don't, and that simple framing is what separates a styled space from a cluttered one.

Every tray below is under $25 and designed to work on a standard-sized square or round ottoman. Most have handles, which serves two purposes: they make the tray easy to move when you actually want to use the ottoman as a footrest, and they make it look more intentional — like something you placed there deliberately rather than just something sitting on top of another thing.

## What's the Best Ottoman Tray for a Traditional Living Room?

**A rectangular wood tray with handles is the classic choice for an ottoman because it works in every decor style, from farmhouse to transitional to mid-century, and it's sturdy enough to hold a candle, a few books, and a small plant without flexing.**

The reason wood trays dominate this category is straightforward: wood reads as intentional and warm in a way that plastic or metal doesn't. The handles are both functional (you'll actually pick it up and move it) and decorative (they give the tray a finished, furniture-adjacent look). A natural or light stain works in most living rooms; a darker walnut stain leans more formal. At under $25, a well-made wood tray with handles is genuinely one of the better value items in home decor.

## What's the Best Option for a Round Ottoman?

**A round tray with handles is made specifically for circular ottomans — the proportions match, the shape echoes the ottoman itself, and the result looks designed rather than improvised.**

Round ottomans are having a moment right now, and round trays for them are correspondingly popular. The key is getting the diameter right: for a standard 20-24 inch round ottoman, a tray around 16-18 inches in diameter leaves a visible border of ottoman fabric, which looks better than a tray that goes all the way to the edge. Acacia or bamboo in a warm brown works beautifully with most velvet, linen, or woven ottoman fabrics.

**A white painted wood tray with cutout handles is the farmhouse version — it works with shiplap, neutrals, cream linen sofas, and the general aesthetic that never seems to go out of style regardless of what design magazines say.**

White trays read as fresh and clean, and they work especially well on darker ottomans where the contrast is visible. They also photograph beautifully, which matters if you care about how your living room looks on camera. The painted finish on quality versions is smooth enough that candle wax, condensation rings, and casual use wipe off cleanly. If you're building a classic neutral living room, a white tray with the right styling objects on it is the finishing touch.

Velvet trays exist in a slightly different aesthetic category than wood trays. They're more glamorous, more maximalist, and they work especially well in bedrooms, dressing rooms, and the kind of living rooms that have jewel-toned velvet sofas and gold accents everywhere. ## What About a Natural, Boho-Style Option?

**A woven rattan or seagrass tray with handles brings natural texture to a living room — it works with macrame, linen, jute, and all the other natural material trends that have been popular for the last several years.**

Rattan trays are lighter than wood trays and have a more casual, relaxed feel. They're ideal for boho, coastal, or eclectic living rooms where the goal is warmth and texture rather than sleek minimalism. The woven surface does make cleanup slightly harder than a smooth wood tray, so these work best when you're not planning to put wet glasses or anything that might drip on them directly. Use them for books, candles, plants, and dry decorative objects and they'll look great for years.

## Quick Tips for Styling an Ottoman Tray

- **The rule of three.** Put three items on your tray: something tall (a candle or small plant), something medium (a stack of books), and something small (a coaster, a small dish). This gives visual variety without clutter.
- **Keep the tray at about 60-70% of the ottoman width.** Too small and it looks lost. Too big and it looks shoved on. A visible border of ottoman fabric on all sides is the goal.
- **Use your tray to change seasonal decor easily.** Swap out a pumpkin in fall for pinecones in winter for a spring plant — the tray stays, only the items change.
- **Round trays on round ottomans, rectangular on square ones.** This isn't a hard rule, but matching shapes looks more intentional.
- **Prop a book or two face-up.** Books with beautiful covers or color-coordinated spines elevate a tray display from generic to styled.
