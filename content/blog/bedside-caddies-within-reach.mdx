Picture this: you're lying in bed, comfortable, almost asleep, and your phone buzzes on the other side of the room because that was the only place to charge it. Or you wake up in the middle of the night and your glasses are somewhere on the floor because there was no good place to put them. Or you're watching something in bed, the remote slips off the mattress, and suddenly you're doing a whole thing just to retrieve it.

A bedside caddy solves all of this. It's a hanging organizer that attaches to the side of your mattress or bed frame, keeping everything you actually reach for at night — phone, book, glasses, remote, chapstick, charger, water bottle — within arm's reach without requiring a separate piece of furniture. It's especially useful in smaller bedrooms where a nightstand just isn't practical, or in guest rooms where you want something functional without adding furniture.

Here are five bedside caddies worth knowing about, from compact single-pocket versions to multi-compartment organizers with cup holders and phone slots.

## Quick Tips for Using a Bedside Caddy

- **Measure your mattress depth before buying.** Most caddies fit mattresses up to 14 in. thick. If you have a pillow-top or extra-thick mattress, look specifically for caddies rated for 16 in. or deeper.
- **Choose based on what you actually keep in reach.** A cup holder is non-negotiable if you drink water at night. A tablet pocket is a must if you read on your iPad. Prioritize the features you'll actually use.
- **Don't overfill it.** A heavy, overstuffed caddy will slide. Keep it to items you actually reach for at night — phone, glasses, current book, chapstick, remote. That's it.
- **Place the caddy on the dominant-hand side.** Sounds obvious, but worth thinking through before you install it.
- **Check the mattress band periodically.** Some caddies use a flat panel that tucks under the mattress. If yours shifts, re-tuck the panel deeper between the mattress and box spring.
