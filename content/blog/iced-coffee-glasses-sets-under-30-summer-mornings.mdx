## Quick Tips

A few things I've learned after a year of taking iced coffee glasses way too seriously:

- **Pre-chill your glass.** Run it under cold water and toss it in the freezer for 5 minutes before you brew. Your ice lasts twice as long.
- **Big ice cubes melt slower.** A silicone tray that makes 2-inch cubes is $10 on Amazon and changes the entire experience. Less dilution, longer-lasting drink.
- **Don't put thin glass in the dishwasher's top rack.** It moves around during the cycle and chips. Bottom rack only or hand-wash for the delicate sets.
- **Glass straws beat plastic and stainless.** They don't change the taste, they're easy to clean with a brush, and they survive longer than plastic.
- **Match the glass size to your habit.** If you sip slow, go 12-15 oz. If you chug, get the 24 oz. Watered-down coffee at the end of a too-big glass is the worst feeling.
