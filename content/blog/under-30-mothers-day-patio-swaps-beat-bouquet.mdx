A bouquet costs $60 at the grocery store, dies in seven days, and ends up in the trash next to a Snickers wrapper. Mom deserves better. The good news is that for less than the price of those flowers, you can buy something that earns its place on her patio for the rest of the summer.

I've watched my own mother get bouquets every Mother's Day for thirty years. She loves them for about four days. Then she politely waters the brown stems for another week because she feels guilty throwing them out. This year, give her something that actually lives.

A ceramic outdoor planter pre-filled with starter herbs hits all three: it looks like a gift, it smells incredible when she pinches a basil leaf, and it gives her something to tend that talks back.

## What to Skip

Skip the engraved cutting board if she's never asked for one. Skip the personalized doormat if she lives in a condo. Skip the candle gift sets that come in tin boxes — she's been getting those for fifteen years and the boxes are in a closet somewhere.

The rule for Mother's Day patio gifts is simple. Buy something she'll actually use this summer, not something she'll feel obligated to display. A planter she'll fill, a kneeler that saves her knees, a lantern that lights her evenings, a feeder that brings birds. These outlast the bouquet by about ninety days.

Sign the card "I know you'd say not to spend money on you. Spend the summer outside instead." That's the part she's actually going to keep.
