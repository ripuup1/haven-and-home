If you're buying a new bed frame, let me save you some money right up front: you do not need a box spring with a platform bed. Platform frames have built-in wood slats that support your mattress directly, which means no extra $150–$200 purchase on top of the frame. This is one of the most commonly misunderstood things about bed shopping, and it's cost people a lot of unnecessary money.

The other thing that's changed in the last few years is quality. Under $200 used to mean wobbly metal tubes that squeaked every time you moved. Now you can get solid wood-and-metal hybrid frames with real headboards, clean lines, and center support legs that genuinely hold up to daily use. Here are six worth considering.

## What to Look for in a Platform Bed Frame

**The non-negotiables: center support legs (not just a center rail), slat spacing under 3 inches, and a weight capacity listed over 600 lbs for a queen.**

Center support legs are what keep the frame from sagging in the middle over time. If a listing only shows two legs at each end with a rail connecting them in the middle but no floor contact, skip it. Slat spacing matters for foam and memory foam mattresses — wider than 3 inches and your mattress can start to dip between slats.

## Quick Tips for Platform Bed Frames

- Always check weight capacity — for couples, look for 800+ lbs (frame plus two people plus mattress)
- Under-bed storage clearance varies hugely — if storage matters, look for 14–16 inches of clearance
- For memory foam mattresses, wood slats spaced 2–2.5 inches apart are ideal
- Assembly tip: loosely attach all bolts first, then tighten everything at once — prevents warped joints
- If you have a partner, 2-person assembly will cut your time in half and prevent alignment issues
