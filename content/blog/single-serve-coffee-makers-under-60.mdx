Counter space is precious. Anyone who has ever had to choose between a toaster and a coffee maker in a small kitchen knows exactly what I mean. The good news is that single-serve coffee makers have gotten genuinely compact in the last few years — we are talking machines that take up about the same footprint as a stack of two paperback books. And the best ones are under $60.

Whether you live alone and rarely brew more than one cup at a time, or you just want a dedicated machine that doesn't dominate your small kitchen, there is a perfect option here. These are the single-serve coffee makers worth your counter space and your money.

## Is the Keurig K-Mini Worth It for a Small Kitchen Counter?

**The Keurig K-Mini ($59.99, 4.6 stars, 45,000+ reviews) is the gold standard of compact single-serve coffee makers, and for good reason — it brews any K-Cup pod in 6 to 12 ounce sizes, takes up just 4.5 inches of counter width, and has built-in cord storage so your counter stays tidy.** It is the most convenient option if you already use K-Cups and want something that just works every single morning.

The K-Mini brews fresh from the tap, meaning you fill the water reservoir with exactly the amount you want to brew each time rather than maintaining a tank. Some people find this annoying, but if you drink one cup a day, it means the water is always fresh rather than sitting for days. The machine heats up in about two minutes, which is faster than most entry-level drip makers.

## How Does the Nespresso Essenza Mini Compare for Espresso-Style Coffee?

**The Nespresso Essenza Mini ($99 retail, but regularly found for $49 to $59 during sales, 4.7 stars, 22,000+ reviews) is a fundamentally different machine than the Keurig — it makes genuine espresso and lungo shots from Nespresso capsules using 19 bars of pressure, which creates that distinctive rich, crema-topped espresso you cannot replicate with drip brewing.** At just 3.3 inches wide and 8 inches tall, it is astonishingly small for an espresso machine.

If your morning coffee is a latte, a cappuccino, or an Americano rather than a classic drip cup, this is the machine for you. The capsules come in a huge range of intensity levels and roasts, and Nespresso's subscription service keeps you stocked automatically. The machine itself heats up in about 25 seconds, which is faster than any drip maker in its class.

## Quick Tips for Choosing a Single-Serve Coffee Maker

- Decide first whether you want espresso-style coffee or regular drip. These are fundamentally different beverages and require different machines.
- Factor in the ongoing pod cost, not just the machine price. K-Cups average 50 to 75 cents per cup in bulk. Nespresso capsules run about 70 cents to $1.25. Ground coffee in a reusable filter drops the per-cup cost to under 30 cents.
- Measure your under-cabinet clearance before buying any machine. Most single-serve makers are 11 to 14 inches tall, and low-hanging cabinets can make loading the water reservoir difficult.
- Look for a removable drip tray or adjustable height if you use a tall travel mug. Not every machine accommodates mugs over 5.5 inches tall.
- If you drink more than one cup in the morning, a single-serve machine with fast heat-up time (under 2 minutes) is more practical than one that requires waiting between cups.
