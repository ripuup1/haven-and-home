## Quick Tips for Anti-Fatigue Kitchen Mats

- **Measure before you buy.** Kitchen mats come in standard sizes but your sink area may be wider or narrower than average. A mat that is too small defeats the purpose.
- **Check the foam density, not just the thickness.** A flimsy 0.5-inch foam mat gives less support than a dense 0.4-inch foam mat. Read reviews specifically mentioning whether the mat flattens over time.
- **Non-slip backing matters more on smooth tile.** If you have heavily textured or grippy flooring, almost any mat will stay put. On polished tile or smooth hardwood, rubber-backed is worth the investment.
- **Machine-washable sounds great but check the fine print.** Most foam anti-fatigue mats should be hand-washed or spot-cleaned — machine washing can damage the foam core. Wipe-clean surfaces are more practical for daily kitchen use.
- **Rotate your mat occasionally.** If you always stand in the same spot, the foam compresses unevenly. Flipping or rotating extends the life significantly.

Standing in the kitchen is unavoidable. Hurting while doing it is not. Any of these mats will make a real difference — your feet will notice within the first week.
