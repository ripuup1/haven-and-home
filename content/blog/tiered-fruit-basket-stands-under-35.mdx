A bowl of fruit sitting flat on the counter is fine — until you realize it's taking up a full square foot of prep space and the peaches at the bottom are getting squished. Tiered fruit stands solve both problems by going vertical instead of horizontal, stacking tiers of fruit above each other and using far less counter footprint.

The best ones also look good enough to leave out all the time, which is a real bonus in smaller kitchens where everything on the counter is decorative by default. And unlike fancy kitchen gadgets that live in a drawer, a tiered fruit basket earns its spot every single day.

All five picks below are under $35, work well on a small counter, and hold enough fruit for a household of two to four people without taking over the kitchen.

## Quick Tips for Getting the Most From a Tiered Fruit Basket

- **Put ripening fruit on higher tiers.** Heat rises slightly, and the upper tiers tend to be slightly warmer — good for pushing an avocado from firm to ready.
- **Keep citrus on the bottom.** Oranges, lemons, and limes are heavy and don't need to ripen. Bottom tier keeps weight low and the basket stable.
- **Don't refrigerate fruit you're keeping in the basket.** The whole point is room temperature ripening and easy access. If fruit is ready to eat and you want to preserve it, move it to the fridge and replace it from the grocery haul.
- **Use the basket for onions and garlic too.** A designated tier for onions and garlic keeps them accessible, lets them breathe, and frees up a whole cabinet.
- **Wipe the tiers weekly.** Fruit oils, juice drips, and the occasional overripe situation make regular wiping worth it — most wire baskets take 30 seconds to wipe down.
