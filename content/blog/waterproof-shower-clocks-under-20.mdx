A waterproof shower clock fixes this so elegantly — just stick it to the wall with a suction cup and you always know exactly where you stand on time. Whether you want a simple analog face, a digital display that shows temperature, or a timer built for kids who treat showering as a recreational activity, there is something on this list for you. All of these come in under $20 and mount without tools or drilling.

## What to Look for in a Shower Clock

**The most important specs are the waterproofing rating, the mounting method, and the display readability from across the shower.** A clock that fogs up or shows a blurry face from five feet away defeats the purpose entirely.

Most good shower clocks attach via suction cup to tile or glass, which means zero damage to your walls. Digital models add useful extras like temperature and timer functions. Analog versions are simpler, quieter (no tick), and easier to read at a glance. Both work well — it really comes down to personal preference.

## Quick Tips

- Before mounting, wipe the tile surface dry and clean — suction cups on dusty or damp tile will fall off, usually at the worst possible moment.
- Most waterproof shower clocks use AAA or AA batteries, not charging cables. Keep a spare set in your bathroom cabinet so a dead battery does not catch you off guard.
- If your shower has textured tile, a suction cup may not grip well. Look for models that also include a hook mount option as a backup.
