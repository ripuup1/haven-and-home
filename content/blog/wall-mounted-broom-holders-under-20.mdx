## Quick Tips for Installing and Using Broom Holders

- **Mount at the right height.** Your broom handles should hang so the head is 2 to 4 inches off the floor — low enough to be stable, high enough to not drag.
- **Group tools by type.** Brooms and mops on the holder, dustpan and smaller items on the hooks — keeping similar tools together means you always know where to look.
- **Mark studs before drilling.** A $10 stud finder will save you from remounting when your holder falls off drywall. Most utility spaces have studs on 16-inch centers.
- **Use in pantries too.** Broom holders aren't just for garages and closets — a small mounted holder on the inside of a pantry door organizes dusters, bottle brushes, and small cleaning tools elegantly.
- **Clean the wall surface first.** For adhesive-mounted holders, a thorough wipe with isopropyl alcohol before sticking ensures the adhesive bonds properly. Skipping this step is the main reason adhesive holders fail prematurely.
