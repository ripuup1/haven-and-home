Have you ever come home from Costco with 48 cans of soup and zero idea where they're going? If your current strategy is stacking them in a wobbly pyramid or cramming them in a drawer, you're not alone — and you're definitely not getting the most out of your pantry space. That's where can dispensers come in.

Can dispensers (also called can racks or can organizers) slide a new can to the front every time you pull one from the bottom, so you're always using the oldest can first and never losing track of what you have. The problem is that the quality range is massive. Some hold 12 cans fine and fall apart at 24. Others are designed for standard 15 oz cans but can't fit a 28 oz tomato can without forcing it.

Here's what to actually look for before you buy, plus the specific racks that have earned their shelf space.

## What to Look For

**Can capacity vs. your shopping habits.** A rack that holds 18 cans is fine for weekly shopping. If you do big warehouse runs, look for racks with 30+ can capacity per unit — or buy two smaller ones and stack them.

**Can size compatibility.** Standard racks fit 15 oz soup cans. Verify the width if you stock larger cans (28 oz diced tomatoes, large chili cans). Some racks have adjustable rails that accommodate a range of sizes.

**Chrome vs. plastic.** Chrome racks are more durable and look cleaner, but they're heavier and cost more. Plastic racks are lighter and cheaper, but the rolling mechanism tends to wear out faster in cheaper models.

**Stackability.** If your pantry has multiple shelves, stackable racks let you build vertically. Look for units that are specifically designed to stack and interlock rather than just balance on top of each other.

**Installation.** Most can racks are freestanding and just sit on a shelf. A few mount to cabinet walls or doors. Measure your shelf depth before buying — most standard racks are 15-17 inches deep.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is the best can organizer for a pantry?

The Sagler Stackable Can Rack is the best overall pantry can organizer for most households. It holds 36 standard cans, stacks cleanly with additional units, and the chrome construction is durable enough for daily loading and unloading. For small pantries, the Decobros rack has a slimmer footprint.

### Do pantry can racks work with large Costco cans?

Standard can racks are sized for 15 oz cans, which fits most soups and vegetables. Large cans (28 oz and up) may not fit the standard rail width. Check the product dimensions before buying if you stock oversized cans. Some racks have adjustable rails that accommodate a wider range of sizes.

### Can you stack multiple can racks on top of each other?

Yes, most modern can racks are designed to stack. Look for racks that include interlocking tabs or notches that connect units securely. Simply stacking racks without interlocking them is not stable for full loads of cans. The Sagler, Decobros, and Simplehouseware racks all have stackable designs.

### How many cans can a standard pantry can rack hold?

Most single-unit pantry can racks hold between 18 and 36 standard 15 oz cans. If you need more capacity, buy two or three units and stack them. A stacked set of three Sagler racks holds up to 108 cans and fits on most standard pantry shelves.
