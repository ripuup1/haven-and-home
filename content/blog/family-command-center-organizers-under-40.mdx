## What to Put in Each Zone of Your Command Center

Once your command center is on the wall, here's how to make it actually work:

**Mail slots:** Designate one slot per family member, or use a system like: action needed / to file / reading material. The key is picking a system and sticking to it.

**Key hooks:** Put your most-used keys on the hooks closest to the door, and secondary keys (spares, guest keys) on hooks further in. Label with a small label maker for clarity.

**Chalkboard surface:** Use it for the week's schedule, grocery list additions, or reminders. Chalk markers last longer and look cleaner than regular chalk.

**Corkboard or pinning area:** If your organizer has one, pin items with a deadline — birthday invitations, permission slips, event tickets — so they're visible and don't get buried.

## Quick Tips for Setting Up a Family Command Center

- **Mount it at eye level:** About 60-65 in. from the floor is the right height for most adults, and ensures kids can reach the bottom hooks.
- **Put it where you actually enter:** The garage door entry, mudroom, or main front door — wherever your family realistically walks in. The back door counts too.
- **Pair it with a small basket:** A small basket or tray on the surface below the organizer catches items that don't fit on hooks, like sunglasses and dog leashes.
- **Do a weekly reset:** Schedule 10 minutes on Sunday to clear out the mail slots, update the chalkboard, and start the week clean.
- **Label everything:** Even if you think you'll remember the system, small labels make it easy for everyone in the house to follow it without being reminded.
