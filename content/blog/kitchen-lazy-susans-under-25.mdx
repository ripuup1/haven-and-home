There is a specific kind of frustration that comes from a corner cabinet. You know there are three bottles of olive oil back there somewhere because you keep buying olive oil. You just cannot reach them without removing everything in front. A lazy susan solves this so completely that it is almost annoying it took this long to buy one.

The good news is that lazy susans are one of the cheapest cabinet upgrades you can make. The ones below are all under $25, and most are under $20. They work in deep corner cabinets, under the sink, in the pantry, and even on the countertop. The trick is matching the right size and material to where you are actually putting it.

Bamboo and wood options look great on countertops and open shelving. Clear acrylic and plastic are better for inside cabinets because you can see what is on them from the front. Two-tier versions double your storage in the same footprint, which is useful when you are working with limited vertical space.

## Quick Tips for Lazy Susan Organization

- **Match size to purpose**: Use 9-10 inches for fridge and small cabinets, 12-14 inches for pantry and countertop, 15+ inches for deep corner base cabinets.
- **Group by use frequency**: Keep daily-use items like olive oil and salt on the countertop turntable. Weekly-use spices go in the cabinet. Store bulk extras on lower pantry shelves.
- **Clear for inside cabinets, wood for visible areas**: You want to see labels through the cabinet door, but you want the countertop to look nice.
- **Do not overfill**: A turntable that you have to unload to spin defeats the whole purpose. Leave room to rotate freely.
- **Wipe down monthly**: Lazy susans catch drips and crumbs. A quick wipe with a damp cloth keeps them looking clean and keeps rotation smooth.
