Eighty percent of the clothes in a typical closet sit in a small stack that's one wrong tug away from collapse. I learned that statistic the hard way last winter, when a single grab for a turtleneck sent five sweaters, two scarves, and one suspiciously dusty handbag tumbling onto my head from the top shelf.

The fix isn't more shelves or a Pinterest-perfect overhaul. It's stackable bins, sized right for the zone they're sitting in. Once you contain the piles, the avalanches stop. Here's how I'd build it out, zone by zone, all under $25 a piece.

This is the avalanche zone. The top shelf is where you stash the things you reach for least, which means they get buried, forgotten, and then suddenly toppled when you finally need them. Clear stackable bins with handles solve the whole problem in one move.

The actual key to a closet that stops avalanching isn't any specific bin, it's matching the bin to the zone. Top shelf gets clear bins with handles. Hanging bar gets pull-out drawers. Floor gets locking lids. Under-bed gets clear-window fabric. Once each zone has the right tool, the whole thing finally holds together.
