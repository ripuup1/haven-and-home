## Quick Tips for Hanging Your Gallery Wall

- **Use the paper template method even if your kit doesn't include one.** Trace each frame on brown kraft paper, cut out the shapes, tape them to the wall with painter's tape, and live with the arrangement for a day before committing to nails.
- **Start from the center and work outward.** Hang your anchor frame (usually the largest) first and build around it rather than starting from a corner.
- **Mix horizontal and vertical orientations.** An all-horizontal or all-vertical gallery wall looks rigid. Rotating a third of your frames creates movement.
- **Leave 2-3 in. between frames** for a gallery wall that reads as intentional rather than crammed. More than 4 in. starts to look scattered.
- **Don't feel locked in to photos.** Art prints, dried botanicals in frames, fabric swatches, and even decorative paper behind glass all look stunning in gallery arrangements.

Gallery walls are one of those projects that looks intimidating but becomes surprisingly satisfying the moment you step back and see it all together. Start with one of these kits, commit to the template approach, and you'll have something you love enough to keep for years.
