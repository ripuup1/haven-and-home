If you have been curious about bidets but do not want to spend hundreds on a standalone unit or rip out your existing toilet, a bidet attachment is the easiest upgrade you can make in your bathroom this year. These clip onto your existing toilet seat, connect to the water supply line that is already behind your toilet, and take about 15 minutes to install with zero plumbing experience.

The non-electric models are the sweet spot for most people. No outlet needed, no batteries, no complicated wiring — just clean, reliable water pressure controlled by a simple dial. Most of these use the same cold water supply your toilet already runs on, and honestly, the temperature is a non-issue for the vast majority of users. You adjust quickly.

All five picks below are under $40, fit standard two-piece toilets, and have thousands of verified reviews from real buyers. Here is what is actually worth your money.

## Quick Tips

- Turn off the water supply valve behind your toilet before installation — it is the small knob on the wall
- Run the bidet on low pressure for the first use so you can gauge the water temperature and strength
- Keep a small towel or a set of dedicated bidet towels nearby for patting dry — you will use dramatically less toilet paper either way
- If your toilet has a one-piece design (tank and bowl are fused), double-check compatibility before ordering
