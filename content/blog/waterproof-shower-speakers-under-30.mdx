Showering in silence is fine, but showering with your favorite podcast, playlist, or audiobook is better. The problem is that most Bluetooth speakers aren't built for a bathroom environment. Steam, water splashes, and humidity will kill a regular speaker within weeks. What you need is a speaker with a real waterproof rating, not just a marketing claim, and ideally one that mounts to your shower wall so it's not sitting in a puddle on the shelf.

I've compared six waterproof Bluetooth speakers under $30 that are specifically designed for shower use. The key specs to care about are the IPX rating (IPX7 means fully submersible, which is what you want), battery life (at least 8 hours so you're not charging daily), and mounting method (suction cup, clip, or carabiner). Everything on this list delivers on all three.

## Quick Tips

- Always check the IPX rating. IPX7 means fully submersible in 3 feet of water for 30 minutes. IPX4 only handles splashes and is not enough for a shower.
- Charge your speaker outside the bathroom. Plugging in a USB cable with wet hands near steam is a bad idea, even with waterproof speakers.
- Clean the suction cup with rubbing alcohol monthly. Soap residue and mineral buildup from hard water reduce suction strength over time.
- If your shower speaker keeps falling, try sticking the suction cup to the glass shower door instead of tile. Glass provides a smoother, more consistent seal.
