The wall above your bed is the largest blank canvas in the bedroom, and leaving it empty makes the whole room feel unfinished. But the options are overwhelming. Gallery wall? Mirror? Shelf? Tapestry? The best approach is matching the decor style to your headboard and bedroom vibe, then committing to one focal point instead of trying to do too much.

Here are the best above-the-bed decor options, organized by style, so you can find the right one for your room without second-guessing yourself.

## What Looks Best Above a Bed?

**The most popular over-the-bed decor is a set of 3 framed prints ($28 for a set). Three prints in matching frames create visual balance, fill the wall without overwhelming it, and are easy to swap out when you want a change. Botanical and abstract prints are the safest choices because they're timeless.**

A set of three is better than a single large print because it fills the width of a queen or king headboard naturally. Center the middle print above the headboard and space them 2-3 inches apart. This creates a coordinated headboard wall art display without the complexity of a full gallery wall.

## Is a Woven Wall Hanging a Good Choice?

A large woven wall hanging adds texture and warmth that framed prints can't replicate. It's perfect for boho, coastal, and earthy bedroom styles. A woven piece in cream or natural cotton softens the wall and creates a focal point that feels handmade and cozy.

Hang it centered above the headboard, about 6-8 inches above the top edge. The bottom of the hanging should have enough clearance that it doesn't get crushed when you lean against the headboard. Look for one that's at least 24 inches wide so it doesn't look lost above a queen bed.

## Should You Put a Mirror Above the Bed?

A round mirror above the bed creates an elegant focal point and makes the bedroom feel larger by reflecting light. It works especially well in rooms with limited natural light. Choose a round mirror with a thin frame or no frame for a modern look, or a thick rattan or wood frame for a boho or coastal vibe.

One rule: the mirror should be at least 24 inches in diameter to look proportional above a queen bed. Anything smaller looks like an afterthought. A 30-36 inch round mirror is the sweet spot for most bedrooms, similar to what works well with [floating shelves](/blog/floating-shelves-hold-weight) in living spaces.

## Can You Put Floating Shelves Above the Bed?

Floating shelves above the bed give you a spot for both decor and functional items. Lean a few small framed prints, add a trailing pothos plant, and set a candle or small vase on the shelf. The benefit over hanging art directly on the wall is flexibility. You can rearrange what's on the shelf whenever you want without putting new holes in the wall.

Use a set of two shelves, staggered at slightly different heights, for visual interest. Make sure they're securely mounted into studs because anything above a bed needs to be earthquake-safe (even if you don't live in earthquake country).

## How to Choose the Right Size

The general rule is that your above-bed decor should be about two-thirds the width of your headboard. For a queen bed with a standard headboard (about 62 inches wide), aim for decor that spans roughly 40-42 inches total. For a king, aim for 50-55 inches. This creates visual balance without the decor looking either too small or too wide for the bed.

Hang everything 6-10 inches above the top of the headboard. Too high and it disconnects from the bed. Too low and it feels cramped. Use painter's tape to map out placement before putting any holes in the wall, and you can also use [styled bookshelf techniques](/blog/style-bookshelf-looks-put-together) for arranging items on floating shelves.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is the best wall decor to put above a bed?

A set of 3 framed botanical or abstract prints ($28) is the most versatile choice for above the bed. They fill the wall proportionally, match most bedroom styles, and are easy to swap out. For a more textured look, a woven wall hanging ($32) adds warmth and works especially well in boho or coastal bedrooms.

### How high should you hang art above a bed?

Hang art 6-10 inches above the top of the headboard. The decor should feel connected to the bed, not floating high on the wall. If you don't have a headboard, hang art so the bottom edge is about 4-6 inches above the top of your pillows when they're propped up.

### Should wall decor above the bed be wider than the headboard?

No. Above-bed decor should be about two-thirds the width of your headboard for proper visual balance. For a queen bed, that means about 40-42 inches of total decor width. Going wider than the headboard makes the arrangement look top-heavy and unbalanced.
