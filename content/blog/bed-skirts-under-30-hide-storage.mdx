Under-bed storage is one of the most practical uses of bedroom space — but exposed storage bins, bare bed frames, and dusty under-bed gaps are not exactly the aesthetic most of us are going for. A bed skirt solves this problem without requiring you to sacrifice the storage, and it simultaneously makes your entire bed look more intentional and dressed.

The challenge with bed skirts has historically been installation — the old-fashioned ones required lifting the mattress and tucking the platform piece under, which is genuinely annoying. The modern wrap-around and elastic-band versions have solved this completely. You can change, wash, and reinstall in minutes. Here are six that look custom for well under $30.

## What Drop Length Do You Actually Need?

**Measure from the top of your box spring or platform to the floor before ordering.** Most standard beds need a 14 to 16 in. drop, but platform beds and storage beds can vary significantly. The products below note their drop lengths — getting this right is the difference between a bed skirt that grazes the floor elegantly and one that bunches or exposes a gap.

Wrap-around designs with adjustable elastic are the most forgiving for measuring variations. If you are not sure, opt for a 16 or 18 in. drop and let it brush the floor slightly — that slightly pooled look is more forgiving than one that is too short.

## Quick Tips for Bed Skirts and Under-Bed Storage

- Use flat storage containers under your bed rather than bags — boxes and bins with lids keep items dust-free and slide in and out cleanly
- A 16 in. drop covers most standard under-bed storage bins; measure from box spring to floor for a custom fit
- Wash your bed skirt every 2 to 3 months — they collect dust from under the bed faster than you expect
- Pair a white or neutral bed skirt with colored storage bins for a pop of organization-as-design energy
- Steam is more effective than ironing for getting wrinkles out of microfiber bed skirts after washing

A good bed skirt is the finishing touch that makes a bed look like someone actually styled it. Pin your favorites before your next bedroom refresh and check back when you are ready to tackle the storage situation under your bed.
