Opening a drawer and being greeted by a faint, lovely scent is one of those small pleasures that's completely easy to create and completely easy to forget about until you experience it somewhere else — like at a nice hotel or a friend's house — and immediately wonder why your own closet smells like, well, nothing particularly nice. Scented drawer sachets are the solution, and they're about as low-effort as home upgrades get. Tuck one in a drawer, forget about it, enjoy the results for 3-6 months.

The range of scents and styles available under $15 is actually impressive. Lavender is the classic for good reason, but cedar adds genuine moth-repelling properties, linen scents feel clean and neutral, and floral options like rose are perfect for delicate clothing drawers. This list covers the best options in each category so you can find what fits your space.

## Lavender: The Classic That Earns Its Reputation

**Lavender sachets smell genuinely beautiful, have mild calming properties that make them perfect for bedroom drawers and pillowcases, and the scent lasts.**

Real dried lavender sachets from quality brands hold their scent for 4-6 months without any refreshing. The key is buying ones with actual dried lavender flowers or high-quality essential oil-infused filling — not synthetic fragrance that fades in a month. Tuck them in clothing drawers, under pillows, or in linen closets.

## Cedar: The Functional Choice That Also Smells Amazing

**Cedar sachets do something lavender can't — they naturally repel moths, silverfish, and other fabric pests while keeping your closet smelling fresh.**

Cedar is especially valuable in closets where you store wool, cashmere, or seasonal items that sit for months without being touched. The oils in cedar wood are a natural insect repellent, and unlike mothballs, cedar smells genuinely pleasant rather than chemical. Cedar blocks or sachets last 1-2 years and can be refreshed by lightly sanding the surface.

## Quick Tips for Scented Sachets

- Place one sachet per drawer rather than multiple — the scent builds up in an enclosed space
- Avoid placing directly on delicate fabrics; lay them at the front or back of the drawer
- Cedar is the only scent that provides actual pest protection — lavender and florals are fragrance only
- Refresh dried lavender sachets by squeezing the bag gently to release more oil
- Store extras in a sealed plastic bag until ready to use to preserve the scent
