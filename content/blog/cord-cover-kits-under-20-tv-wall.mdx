You spent money on a wall-mounted TV to get a clean, modern look in your living room. And then the cords happened. That cascade of HDMI cables, power cords, and streaming device wires hanging down the wall can undo the whole effect in an instant.

Running cords through the wall is the cleanest option, but it requires cutting into drywall and potentially hiring an electrician. Cord cover kits are the practical middle ground: they sit on the surface of the wall, snap together over your cords, and when they are painted to match your wall color, they become almost invisible.

The ones on this list are all under $20, use simple peel-and-stick installation, and cover enough length to handle most standard TV setups.

## Quick Tips for Installing Cord Covers

- **Plan your route before installing anything.** Run all the cords loosely in the path you want them to take and confirm they reach. A cord that is six inches too short will undo the whole installation.
- **Paint the cover before sticking it to the wall.** Painting a flat piece of raceway on a table is much easier than painting it against a wall with a brush. Use the same interior paint as your wall, let it dry, then mount it.
- **Use a level for vertical runs.** A cord cover that runs slightly crooked down the wall is more noticeable than you would expect. Spend 30 seconds with a level and avoid the frustration.
- **Bundle cords inside the channel in a single layer.** Cords stacked on top of each other in a channel make it harder to snap closed and can cause the cover to bow out. Lay them flat and side by side instead.
- **Leave the ends accessible.** The end caps that come with most kits make the termination look clean, but make sure you position the ends in a place where you can still access the cords if you need to add or swap a device later.
