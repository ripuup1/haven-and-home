## Are Cotton Rope Baskets Good for Bathrooms?

Cotton rope baskets have a softer, more textured look than woven baskets. They're especially good for holding items you grab frequently because the soft material won't scratch anything. A large cotton rope basket is perfect for holding rolled bath towels on the floor next to the tub or on a bathroom stool. The coiled texture adds that "Pinterest bathroom" look instantly.

The only downside is that cotton rope can absorb moisture in a steamy bathroom. Place them where they won't get directly splashed, like on open shelves or on top of the toilet tank in your [organized linen closet](/blog/linen-closet-organization).

## How to Style Bathroom Baskets Like a Pro

The key to making bathroom baskets look decorative is the "rule of thirds." Fill each basket about two-thirds full so items peek out but don't overflow. Roll towels instead of folding them for a spa look. Mix basket materials and sizes for visual interest, but keep them in the same color family (all naturals, or all white, or all dark).

Place your largest basket on the floor or lowest shelf and work up to smaller baskets on higher shelves. This creates visual balance and puts heavier items where they're easier to access.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is the best type of basket for bathroom storage?

Woven seagrass baskets (set of 3, $28) are the best overall choice for bathroom storage. They're sturdy, naturally moisture-resistant, and add texture that makes any shelf look styled. For under-sink storage, stackable bins with pull-out fronts ($24) are more practical because they work around plumbing.

### How do you make bathroom baskets look decorative?

Fill baskets about two-thirds full so items are visible but not overflowing. Roll towels instead of folding for a spa look. Keep all baskets in the same color family and mix sizes for visual interest. Place larger baskets on lower shelves and smaller ones higher up.

### Can you use woven baskets in a humid bathroom?

Seagrass and woven baskets handle bathroom humidity well because they're naturally moisture-resistant. Cotton rope baskets absorb more moisture, so place them away from direct splashes. All woven baskets benefit from being wiped down occasionally and allowed to air out if they get damp.
