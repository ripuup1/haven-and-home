If you have been eyeing those gorgeous built-in niches on renovation Instagram but assumed they required a full tile job, think again. Whether you are doing a bathroom refresh or looking to add a practical upgrade without breaking anything, the options below cover everything from full recessed tile-ready inserts to no-drill stainless steel shelves that mount flush to your tile wall. The key is knowing what kind of niche you need before you shop. Tile-ready recessed niches get built into the wall during a renovation or major repair. Surface-mount stainless niches go on top of existing tile using adhesive or screws. And shower shelf organizer trays slide into an existing niche opening. Read on for the best picks in each style.

## Quick Tips for Choosing a Shower Niche Insert

- **Measure your stud bay first.** Standard US stud bays are 14.5 inches wide (16 inches on center). Most niches are designed for this, but measure your actual opening before ordering.
- **Check the depth.** A 3.5-inch deep niche holds standard shampoo bottles. If you have deep shelving or taller bottles, look for 4-inch or deeper options.
- **Stainless vs. ABS.** Stainless looks better long-term and does not yellow or crack. ABS plastic is lighter and fine for tray inserts, but avoid thin pieces for a permanent installation.
- **Match your finish.** Brushed nickel niches work with brushed nickel fixtures. Matte black coordinates with matte black. It sounds obvious, but it is easy to miss when shopping online.
- **Caulk every seam.** Whether it is a tray insert or a full niche, run a bead of silicone caulk around the perimeter to keep water from getting behind the wall.
