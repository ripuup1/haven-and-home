Nightstands are one of the biggest space wasters in a small bedroom. A standard nightstand takes up about 18 by 18 inches of floor space, leaving a tight walking path around the bed. Floating bedside shelves solve this without sacrificing the functionality — you still get a surface for your water glass, phone, and book, but the floor under it is completely open.

The right floating shelf for a bedside table needs to be drillable into a stud (or use appropriate wall anchors), deep enough to hold a phone and lamp without being awkward, and mounted at a height that works from your bed. About 24 to 28 inches from the floor works for most platform beds; 26 to 30 inches is better for taller beds with box springs. All seven options here stay under $30 and can pull that job off.

Some of these have drawers, some have open storage, and some have USB charging built in. The right choice depends on whether you want to stash things out of sight or just need a surface. Read on for the breakdown.

## Quick Tips for Installing Floating Bedside Shelves

- Use a stud finder before drilling — one screw into a stud holds far more weight than two screws into drywall anchors
- Mount at mattress-top height plus 2 to 4 inches so items are easy to reach without sitting up fully
- For shared beds, mount on opposite sides at the same height — use a laser level or measure from the ceiling rather than the floor since floors are rarely perfectly level
- Keep total load under 20 lbs per shelf regardless of the weight rating — this gives you a safety margin and prevents gradual pull-out over time
- If you have plaster walls, use toggle bolts instead of standard drywall anchors
