For ten years I kept every spice jar I owned in a single cabinet above my microwave. Every single time I needed cumin, I knocked over oregano. Every single time I grabbed oregano, paprika came down on my head. I tried drawer inserts (the drawer was too shallow), magnetic strips (the jars were too big), and a tiered lazy Susan (I couldn't read the labels on the back row). I gave up and just dealt with it.

Then I measured a random 4-inch gap between my fridge and my pantry cabinet, ordered a $24 pull-out spice rack on a Sunday, and by Wednesday my spice problem was over. I'm not exaggerating — it's the single best $24 I've spent on my kitchen. What follows is the one I bought, plus the five other narrow-cabinet options I tested along the way so you don't have to.

I didn't want another thing on my counter. I didn't want to drill. I wanted spices that came out, stayed in order, and disappeared when I was done. A slim pull-out rack with rubber bumpers that wedges into an unused gap between appliances or cabinets did all three.

## What I'd Do Differently

If I started over, I'd measure every gap in my kitchen before buying anything. The 4-inch space between my fridge and cabinet was wasted for a decade because I assumed nothing fit. Something did. Something almost always does.

The other thing: buy one narrow pull-out first and see if you actually use it. I bought one, used it daily for three weeks, and then bought a second for cleaning supplies next to the stove. Now there are two. Both earn their spot.

## Quick Tips

- Measure in three places: width, height, depth. Write them down before shopping.
- Check wheel diameter on pull-out racks. Smaller wheels snag on tile grout lines.
- Buy identical jars once the rack is in. Mismatched jars waste the organization.
- Put the spices you use daily in the front row. Rotate seasonal ones to the back.
