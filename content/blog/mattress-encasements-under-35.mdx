Allergy season hits different when you realize your mattress might be part of the problem. The average mattress collects dust mites, dead skin cells, pet dander, and moisture over time — and if you're waking up stuffy or sneezy, a mattress encasement is one of the most effective and affordable fixes you can make. We're not talking about those crinkly plastic covers from the '90s. Today's encasements are soft, breathable, and completely undetectable under your sheets.

A good encasement fully zippers around your mattress (unlike a fitted protector that only covers the top), creating a barrier against allergens, bed bugs, and liquid spills on all six sides. For under $35, you can protect a mattress that cost you hundreds — or thousands — and extend its life by years. Here are five that sleep comfortably and actually do their job.

## Quick Tips for Mattress Encasements

- Always wash your encasement before first use to soften the fabric and activate the waterproof layer
- Wash every 1-2 months on gentle cycle with cold water — hot water can degrade the waterproof membrane over time
- If you have a mattress deeper than 12 inches, double-check the pocket depth before ordering — most standard encasements max out at 12"
- Put your encasement on BEFORE your fitted sheet — it's a base layer, not a replacement for sheets
