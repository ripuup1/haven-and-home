A bar cart is one of those pieces that looks effortlessly cool in other people's homes and somehow ends up looking like a cluttered shelf of random bottles in yours. The secret is editing. A great bar cart isn't about having every spirit known to humankind. It's about having a few beautiful bottles, the right tools, and enough styling pieces to make it look intentional. Everything here is from Amazon, and the whole setup (minus the alcohol) runs about $175.

The cart itself matters more than anything on it. This gold two-tier cart has a tempered glass top and a metal frame that's sturdy enough to hold bottles without wobbling. The wheels lock, which is important because a bar cart that rolls away when you're making a drink is a disaster waiting to happen. Gold is the classic choice and works with virtually any decor style. If gold isn't your thing, this comes in black too. Assembly takes about 15 minutes and doesn't require any tools beyond what's included.

Stemless wine glasses are the bar cart workhorse. They're versatile enough for wine, cocktails, sparkling water, or just looking pretty grouped on a shelf. This set of six has a good weight to it (not too thin, not too thick) and they're dishwasher safe, which matters when you're using them regularly. Stemless is the way to go for a bar cart because they don't tip over as easily and they stack more compactly. Plus, they look more modern than traditional wine glasses.

Marble coasters on a bar cart are both practical and decorative. They protect the glass shelf from condensation rings (which is a real problem with cold drinks on glass surfaces) and they add a luxurious texture that photographs beautifully. Stack a few on the lower shelf when not in use. They double as display pieces. These have a cork backing so they don't scratch the glass. The natural marble veining means no two coasters look exactly the same, which adds character.

Guests help themselves and you don't have to play bartender all night. This one-gallon dispenser has a leak-proof spigot and a wide mouth for easy filling and cleaning. When you're not entertaining, it looks decorative just sitting empty on the cart. The glass is thick enough to feel substantial without being too heavy.

One last styling tip: keep the top shelf for daily-use items (your best bottles, the shaker, a pair of glasses) and the bottom shelf for backup stock, the dispenser, extra napkins, and a small plant or candle. The top shelf is what people see first, so make it count.
