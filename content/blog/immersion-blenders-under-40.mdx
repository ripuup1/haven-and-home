The average countertop blender takes up 144 square inches of counter space. That's a 12-inch by 12-inch footprint dedicated to a machine most people use twice a week for smoothies and soup. An immersion blender fits in a drawer, cleans in 30 seconds under the tap, and handles 80% of what a countertop blender does — without requiring you to transfer hot liquid into a container before blending, which is both a hassle and a burn risk.

The case for switching is strong. Immersion blenders (also called hand blenders or stick blenders) blend directly in the pot, bowl, or measuring cup. They don't require disassembly for cleaning. And with motors now reaching 500W to 1100W at the under-$40 price point, they handle soup, smoothies, baby food, vinaigrettes, whipped cream, and hummus without breaking a sweat.

Here are seven that are actually worth owning, organized by what makes each one worth the purchase.

## Quick Tips

- Always blend with the shaft fully submerged before turning on — starting with the blade above the liquid creates splatter
- When blending hot soups, tilt the pot to one side and blend in the deepest corner to keep the blade submerged
- Don't press the blade tip against the pot bottom — hover it slightly above for best results
- Clean immediately after use while wet; dried-on food is harder to remove from the blade housing
- If your blender stalls on tough ingredients, pulse instead of running continuously — this prevents motor strain

The Mueller Ultra-Stick is the one most people should buy. The KOIOS 4-in-1 is the right call if you want more versatility. And if you've burned through cheaper models before, the KitchenAid or Breville represent meaningful step-ups that will last.
