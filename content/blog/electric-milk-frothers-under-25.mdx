## Quick Tips for Getting Better Foam at Home

- **Warm your milk first.** Heat it to about 140 degrees Fahrenheit before frothing. This is the sweet spot for microfoam.
- **Keep the wand just below the surface.** Too deep and you miss the air incorporation. Too shallow and you get giant bubbles that collapse quickly.
- **Oat milk froths differently than dairy.** It actually foams beautifully but needs a slightly longer froth time. Barista-edition oat milks foam the most reliably.
- **Tap and swirl after frothing.** Tap the cup on the counter a few times and swirl the milk gently. This breaks up any large bubbles and tightens the texture.
- **Clean it immediately.** Froth a small amount of water right after use. Fifteen seconds of clean water through the whisk keeps it like new for years.
