## Quick Tips for Getting the Most Out of Your Hammock Stand

- **Check hammock length before buying a stand.** Most hammocks are 9 to 13 feet including the hanging hardware. A 9-foot stand works for most, but longer hammocks need a longer or adjustable stand.
- **Use the hanging hardware that comes with the stand**, not just your own rope. Stand-specific hardware is already rated to the right weight capacity.
- **For best comfort, the hammock should hang with about a 30-degree angle from the attachment point** — not too tight (that is uncomfortable) and not so loose it sags to the ground.
- **Stabilize on uneven ground** by placing something under one leg rather than trying to force the frame flat. Most stands have some flex to accommodate this.
- **Store the stand at the end of the season** if you live somewhere with harsh winters. Even weather-resistant steel lasts significantly longer when it is not sitting in snow for months.
