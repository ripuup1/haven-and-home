A good toothbrush holder solves this in two ways: it keeps toothbrushes upright and organized, and it prevents that gross water puddle that forms at the base when wet brushes drip onto the countertop.

## The Water Puddle Fix

The number one complaint about toothbrush holders is the water puddle. Here's how to prevent it: choose a holder with drainage holes or a removable drip tray. Shake excess water off your toothbrush before placing it in the holder. And empty/wipe the drip tray once a week as part of your regular bathroom cleaning routine.

If you're using a wall-mounted holder, the drip problem largely solves itself because gravity pulls the water down and away from the counter entirely.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is the best toothbrush holder for a bathroom counter?

A ceramic toothbrush holder with drainage holes ($14) is the best choice for most bathroom counters. The drainage prevents water pooling and mold, the ceramic is easy to clean, and it holds 4 brushes plus toothpaste. For electric toothbrushes, get a dedicated electric toothbrush stand ($18) instead.

### How do you keep a toothbrush holder from getting gross?

Choose a holder with drainage holes or a removable drip tray and clean it weekly. Shake excess water off your toothbrush before placing it in the holder. For deeper cleaning, soak the holder in warm soapy water monthly. Wall-mounted holders stay cleaner because water drips away from the counter naturally.

### Should I wall mount or countertop my toothbrush holder?

Wall-mount if you have limited counter space or a small bathroom. Countertop if you prefer easy access and don't mind using a few inches of counter. Wall-mounted adhesive holders ($12) work in rentals without drilling. Countertop ceramic holders ($14) are easier to clean and move around.
