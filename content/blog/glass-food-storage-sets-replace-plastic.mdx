## What Should You Look for in Glass Food Storage Containers?

**The most important criteria are borosilicate glass (stronger and more thermal-shock resistant than standard glass), airtight lids that actually seal, and BPA-free lid materials.** Bonus points for microwave safety, dishwasher safety, and oven safety — not all glass sets include all three, so check the specs for your specific cooking habits.

Capacity variety matters more than you might expect. A set with all large containers is useless for storing half an avocado or a cup of leftover sauce. Look for sets that include at least two or three different sizes, ideally ranging from under 2 cups to at least 6 cups. That range covers every practical kitchen storage scenario.

## Quick Tips for Switching to Glass Storage

- Transition one area at a time: start with meal prep containers, then move to pantry items, then leftovers
- Label lids with masking tape and a marker for grab-and-go organization — glass containers are identical and you cannot see in from the top
- Tempered glass is strong but not indestructible — avoid dropping on hard tile floors or placing cold glass on a hot burner
- Bamboo and wooden lids should be hand-washed to prevent warping, even if the glass container is dishwasher safe
