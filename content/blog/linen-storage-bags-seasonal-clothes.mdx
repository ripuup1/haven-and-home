There comes a point every spring and fall where you're standing in front of your closet, staring at a pile of bulky sweaters or sundresses that have no business taking up prime real estate for the next six months. You know you need to pack them away, but shoving everything into garbage bags feels wrong, and those giant plastic bins take up way too much space.

Linen and canvas storage bags are the sweet spot. They're breathable (so your clothes don't come out smelling musty), they fold flat when you're not using them, and the good ones have clear windows so you're not playing a guessing game when you need to find something. Most of them fit neatly under a bed, on a closet shelf, or in a storage bench.

Here are six that actually hold up and keep your off-season wardrobe in good shape.

## Quick Tips

- Always wash and fully dry clothes before storing them for the season -- even a little moisture can lead to mildew in a sealed bag.
- Toss a lavender sachet or cedar block inside each bag for extra moth protection and a fresh scent when you unpack next season.
- Store bags in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight. Attics get too hot in summer and can damage delicate fabrics.

Seasonal clothing swaps don't have to be a dreaded chore. With the right storage bags, it takes maybe an hour twice a year, and your closet stays manageable the rest of the time. Found something you love? Pin this for later so you don't lose it!
