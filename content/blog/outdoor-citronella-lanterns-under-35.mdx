Nothing ends a perfect patio evening faster than mosquitoes. You light the grill, pour the drinks, set out the cushions — and then spend the entire night swatting. Citronella candles help, but those basic tin bucket candles from the hardware store aren't exactly the vibe you're going for when you've spent all spring making your outdoor space look nice.

The good news is there are citronella lanterns that actually look like decor pieces while doing double duty as bug repellent. These six options all come in under $35, and they range from rustic metal lanterns to sleek glass tabletop designs. Your patio can smell like a garden instead of a chemical plant, and you won't have to sacrifice aesthetics for function.

## What's the Best Metal Citronella Lantern for Patio Tables?

**A classic metal lantern with a citronella candle insert is the most popular option, and for good reason. At around $22, you get something that looks like a decorative lantern first and a bug deterrent second.**

These lanterns typically come in black, bronze, or antique brass finishes that blend seamlessly with most patio furniture. The enclosed design protects the flame from wind, which means they actually stay lit — a real advantage over open bucket candles. Most come with a citronella candle included, and you can buy refill candles for a few dollars each. The handle on top makes it easy to move them around or hang them from a shepherd's hook.

## Do Hanging Citronella Lanterns Actually Work Better?

**Hanging citronella lanterns can be more effective than tabletop ones because they distribute the scent from a higher point, covering a wider area. A hanging lantern with chain runs about $25.**

Mosquitoes tend to fly at various heights, so having citronella at table level and at head level creates a better perimeter of protection. These hanging lanterns clip onto pergola beams, patio umbrella ribs, or shepherd's hooks and create beautiful ambient lighting while they work. The swinging motion in a breeze also helps spread the citronella scent more effectively. Just make sure the chain or hook is included — some listings are lantern-only.

## Quick Tips

- Place citronella sources every 5-6 feet around your seating area for the best coverage
- Light them 15-20 minutes before guests arrive so the scent has time to build
- Store candles with lids on between uses to preserve the citronella potency
- Combine citronella with a fan — moving air makes it harder for mosquitoes to land and spreads the scent further
