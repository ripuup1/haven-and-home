## What Should You Look for in a Stainless Steel Colander?

**The most important features are mesh fineness, handle comfort, and whether the base lets the colander sit stably in a sink or on a counter.** A colander that tips over when you're draining boiling water is a safety hazard, not just an annoyance.

For everyday use, a 4- to 5-quart colander handles a full box of pasta comfortably. Fine mesh is great for quinoa and small grains; pierced or punched designs drain larger foods faster. Nesting sets are smart for small kitchens — three sizes stack inside each other so you're not hunting down the right size mid-cook.

## The Classic Choice for Everyday Cooks

**If you want a simple, reliable colander that handles everyday kitchen tasks without overthinking it, a classic pierced stainless design in the 4-to-5-quart range is your best bet.**

Pierced colanders (with punched holes rather than mesh) tend to be sturdier and clean up faster. They're ideal if you primarily drain pasta and blanched vegetables. Mesh is better for grains and smaller produce. Both work well — it comes down to what you cook most often.

## Quick Tips for Colander Care

- Rinse your colander immediately after use to prevent staining from pasta starch or berry juice
- Most stainless steel colanders are dishwasher safe, but hand-drying prevents water spots
- If you cook grains regularly, a fine-mesh colander or liner will save you from chasing tiny lentils down the drain
- Nesting sets are worth it for small kitchens — they take up no more space than a single large colander
