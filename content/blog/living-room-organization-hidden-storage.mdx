The living room is where everyone gathers, which means it's also where everything gathers. Blankets, remotes, chargers, magazines, toys, random stuff that doesn't have a home anywhere else. The key to a living room that looks clean without constant effort is hidden storage. Pieces that look like regular furniture but secretly hold all the mess.

You don't need to buy a whole new living room set. Adding even one or two storage pieces changes the dynamic completely. Here's what works best.

A storage ottoman is the hardest-working piece of furniture in any living room. Use it as a footrest during movie night, pull it out for extra seating when guests come over, and stuff all your [throw blankets](/blog/best-throw-blankets-amazon) inside when you're tidying up. This one has a padded top that's comfortable to sit on and a deep interior that holds more than you'd expect. The linen fabric comes in neutral tones that work with any decor style. At $40, it's one of the cheapest ways to add meaningful storage to your room.

## What Is the Best Way to Hide Cables in the Living Room?

**A cable management box ($20) hides your power strip and all those tangled cords behind your TV stand or desk. It's the smallest change on this list but makes the biggest difference in how clean your space feels.**

Cables are the silent mess-maker in every living room. Even a beautifully organized space looks chaotic when there's a tangle of cords behind the TV or under the desk. A cable management box hides your power strip and all the cords inside a clean, ventilated box. This one has openings on both ends for cords to pass through and ventilation slits so nothing overheats. Place it behind your media console or under your desk. Pair it with some velcro cable ties (usually included) and your cord situation goes from embarrassing to invisible. The whole setup with a [wall art arrangement](/blog/affordable-wall-art-under-30) above a clean media console looks polished and intentional.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is the best hidden storage furniture for a living room?

The best hidden storage pieces for a living room are a storage ottoman ($40) for blankets and remotes, a lift-top coffee table ($130) for magazines and board games, and woven baskets ($35 for 3) for open shelving. Together they give you enough hidden storage to keep the entire room clutter-free.

### How do you organize a living room without it looking cluttered?

The key is closed storage. Use furniture with doors or lids instead of open shelving, and add baskets to any open shelves you do have. A cable management box ($20) hides cord clutter, decorative boxes ($27 for 3) corral small items on surfaces, and a storage ottoman doubles as furniture and hidden storage.

### How do you hide toys in the living room?

A storage ottoman with a flip-top lid is the fastest way to hide toys in the living room. Kids can toss everything in at cleanup time and the lid closes to look like a regular footrest. Woven baskets on lower shelves also work well because kids can reach them and the baskets are sturdy enough for heavy toys.
