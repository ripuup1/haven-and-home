Linen curtains do something no other fabric can — they filter light in a way that makes a room feel warm and lived-in rather than dark and closed off. The texture catches light differently throughout the day, creating soft shadows in the morning and a golden glow in the afternoon. It is the kind of subtle detail that makes a bedroom feel like a retreat rather than just a place you sleep.

The catch with real linen curtains is the price. Pure linen panels from brands like Pottery Barn or Restoration Hardware run $100-$200 per panel. But Amazon has made linen-look and linen-blend curtains available at a fraction of that cost, and many of them are genuinely difficult to distinguish from the expensive versions once they are hanging.

## Quick Tips

- Hang curtain rods 4-6 inches above the window frame and 3-6 inches wider on each side for a more spacious look
- Steam linen curtains after hanging rather than ironing — the natural wrinkle adds character
- If you need blackout but love the sheer linen look, use a double curtain rod with sheer linen in front and blackout panels behind
- Machine wash linen curtains on gentle cycle with cold water to prevent shrinkage
