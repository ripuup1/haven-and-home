Sheet sets have a way of separating themselves the moment you fold them. It is deeply irritating.

The fix is embarrassingly simple: bed sheet organizer bags. You fold your sheet set, stuff it inside the matching pillowcase, slide that into an organizer bag labeled with the bed size, and suddenly your linen closet looks like a Container Store display. These things cost between $10 and $20 for a multi-pack, and they completely change how your closet functions.

## Quick Tips for Organizing Your Linen Closet With Sheet Sets

- **Always store a sheet set inside one of its own pillowcases** before putting it in an organizer. It keeps everything together and takes up less space than folded flat sheets.
- **Label by bed AND set.** Queen-Gray means the gray sheets go on the queen bed. A little specificity goes a long way in a dark closet.
- **Store sets vertically when possible.** Standing organizers on their edges rather than stacking horizontally makes grabbing a specific set infinitely easier.
- **Keep a spare fitted sheet loose.** Fitted sheets are harder to fold neatly, so one extra loose in the closet is a reasonable exception to the organized-set rule.
- **Rotate your sets front-to-back.** When you wash sheets, put the clean ones at the back and bring the oldest ones to the front. Even wear extends the life of your sheets significantly.
