## What Makes a Velvet Jewelry Box Worth Buying

**The most important things are the interior layout, the quality of the velvet lining, and whether the lid closes securely.** A box with too few compartments forces you to layer pieces on top of each other — which defeats the whole point and leads to tangles.

Look for separate ring rolls, earring slots, and at least one compartment long enough for a necklace or bracelet. A mirrored lid is not essential but is a convenient bonus. Price does not always predict quality at this level — some of the most popular boxes here cost well under $15.

## Quick Tips

- Store necklaces in a single layer to prevent tangling. If your box only has one necklace section, hang longer chains on a small wall-mounted hook instead of forcing them into a compartment.
- Velvet naturally attracts lint over time. A quick pass with a lint roller every few months keeps boxes looking fresh.
- A jewelry box on the dresser works best when it holds only your most-worn pieces. Store seasonal or special-occasion pieces in a separate spot so you are not sorting through them daily.
