## Quick Tips for Decorating With Wood and Ceramic Kitchen Accessories

- Cluster your wood and ceramic pieces in one area of the counter rather than spacing them out. A grouped vignette always looks more intentional than scattered items.
- Stick to one or two material tones — warm acacia wood pairs beautifully with white or cream ceramic. Mixing too many finishes makes a counter feel busy.
- Use your decorative bowls. A gorgeous acacia wood bowl sitting empty on the counter should hold fruit, garlic bulbs, or onions — lived-in warmth beats pristine emptiness every time.
- Hand wash wood pieces every time, no exceptions. Even "dishwasher-safe" wood will eventually crack and warp if you run it through regularly.
- If a wood piece loses its luster, a quick rub with food-grade mineral oil (about $6 at most grocery stores) brings it right back. It takes about two minutes and the results are dramatic.
