Spring entertaining has a particular energy — the first dinner party on the patio after months indoors, the casual Sunday brunch where the mimosas outnumber the guests. The last thing you want is to haul dishes and drinks outside one at a time, making five trips between the kitchen and the patio table while everything gets cold. That's where a good outdoor serving tray earns its place.

The best outdoor serving trays are melamine (shatterproof, lightweight, and dishwasher safe), have actual handles so you can carry them with a full load, and look good enough to leave on the table as part of your presentation. And the good news is that every great option on Amazon comes in well under $25 — most under $20.

If you've been using a random baking sheet to carry things outside, this is your sign to upgrade.

## What Should You Look for in an Outdoor Serving Tray?

**Melamine is the material of choice for outdoor serving trays: it looks like ceramic, weighs almost nothing, won't shatter when it inevitably gets bumped off a table, and goes straight in the dishwasher.**

Handles are the other non-negotiable. A tray without handles is just a flat surface — you can technically carry it, but balancing drinks and snacks while holding the edges of a tray is a recipe for a dropped platter. Handles mean you can carry a tray confidently with both hands, even when loaded.

Size matters too. For a full charcuterie or app spread, go for at least 18 inches long. For drinks only, 12-15 inches works fine.

## Quick Tips for Outdoor Serving

- Use a non-slip mat or shelf liner under your tray on smooth surfaces — melamine on glass or ceramic tile can slide
- For drinks-heavy hosting, freeze a zip bag of water as a long-lasting ice pack and tuck it under a small towel on the tray to keep drinks cold
- Spring flowers from your garden placed in a small jar on the tray elevate any serving setup instantly
- Wide trays handle appetizer spreads; narrow trays handle drinks — invest in both if you entertain regularly
- Melamine can go in the dishwasher but keep it out of the microwave
