If you've never used a white noise machine, it's hard to explain how much difference it makes. You go from hearing every car door, dog bark, and 3 AM garbage truck to sleeping through all of it. White noise works by creating a consistent sound blanket that masks sudden noises — the ones that jolt you awake aren't louder than the ambient sound, so your brain ignores them.

The big decision is real fan vs. digital sounds. Real fan machines produce a richer, more natural sound but have fewer options. Digital machines offer dozens of sounds (rain, ocean, brown noise) but some people find them less natural. Here are the best options across both categories, tested and reviewed by thousands of actual sleepers.

## What's the Best White Noise Machine Overall?

**The Yogasleep Dohm Classic ($45) is the original white noise machine and still the one most sleep experts recommend. It uses a real internal fan to produce adjustable white noise — no loops, no digital artifacts, just smooth, continuous sound.**

The Dohm has been around for over 50 years, and there's a reason it's still the top seller. The real fan inside creates a sound that digital machines can't quite replicate — it's fuller, warmer, and completely non-looping. But if you just want reliable, natural white noise, the Dohm is the gold standard.

## Quick Tips for Using White Noise Machines

- **Place it between you and the noise source** — between your bed and the window, or between you and the bedroom door
- **Start at a lower volume** and increase gradually; you need less than you think
- **Use the timer** if you don't want it running all night — most people fall asleep within 20-30 minutes
- **Brown noise for deep sleepers**, white noise for light sleepers, and pink noise is the middle ground
- **Keep it consistent** — using the same sound every night creates a Pavlovian sleep association that helps you fall asleep faster over time
