Nonstick pans have gotten a bad reputation for dying fast, and honestly, most of the cheap ones deserve it. The thin PTFE coatings on bargain pans start flaking within months of regular use. ## Quick Tips for Making Nonstick Pans Last

- Never use metal utensils — stick to wood, silicone, or nylon
- Let pans cool completely before washing — thermal shock warps the base
- Hand wash when possible even if the pan is labeled dishwasher safe
- Don't spray aerosol cooking spray directly on nonstick — it builds up a sticky residue over time; use a brush with oil instead
- Store pans with a cloth or paper towel between them if stacking to prevent scratches
