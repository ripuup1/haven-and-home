## What Makes a Reed Diffuser Actually Work Well?

**Two things determine whether a reed diffuser is worth buying: the quality of the fragrance oil and the number of reeds.** More reeds = stronger scent throw. Most quality diffusers include 8-12 rattan reed sticks. For a small bathroom (which most are), even 6 reeds in a well-made oil will fill the space nicely.

Flip the reeds every week or two for a refreshed burst of scent. If the scent gets too strong, remove a few reeds. It's a completely customizable system.

## Quick Tips for Getting the Most Out of Your Reed Diffuser

- Place your diffuser in a spot with gentle air circulation (near a door or HVAC vent) to help distribute the scent — but not in direct sunlight, which can degrade the fragrance oil
- Flip the reeds every 5-7 days for a scent refresh — you'll notice a burst of fragrance for the next hour or so after flipping
- If the scent feels too strong, remove 2-3 reeds; if it feels too faint, add more or flip more frequently
- Clean the bottle with hot soapy water before switching to a new fragrance oil to prevent scent mixing
- For a bathroom specifically, keep the diffuser away from the shower splash zone — humidity can affect the reeds and reduce their lifespan
