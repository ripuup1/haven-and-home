## What to Look for in a Glass Pitcher

**Heat resistance, a drip-free spout, and a comfortable handle are the three features that separate a pitcher you love from one that sits in a cabinet.**

Borosilicate glass is the gold standard — it handles both hot and cold without cracking. Standard glass pitchers work fine for cold beverages but should not be used with hot tea or coffee fresh off the stove. A proper spout with a slight lip controls pours without dripping, and a handle that distributes weight evenly makes a full pitcher comfortable to carry across the table. Lids are a bonus, keeping out bugs and keeping drinks cold longer.

## Quick Tips for Glass Pitcher Entertaining

- Fill pitchers no more than three-quarters full when moving — the last thing you want is a spill on the way to the table
- A pitcher of water with cucumber and mint on the counter is the easiest effortless-looking detail you can add to any gathering
- Wide-mouth carafes are easier to add ice and fruit slices to without a funnel
- Store pitchers with their lids off to prevent any musty odors from developing
