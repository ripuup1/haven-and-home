Have you ever bought a bunch of fresh cilantro, used two tablespoons for one recipe, and watched the rest turn into a slimy pile by Wednesday? Because same. For years I thought this was just the nature of fresh herbs — you buy them, you use a little, the rest dies, you throw it out and feel guilty. Turns out the problem isn't the herbs. It's how we store them.

A good herb keeper container works by keeping the stems in a small amount of water (like a vase) while protecting the leaves from ethylene gas and temperature swings. It sounds simple, and it is — but it makes a surprisingly big difference. Fresh herbs stored properly can last two to three weeks instead of three to five days. That means fewer grocery trips, less food waste, and a lot less sad, brown cilantro.

Here are the herb keeper containers that actually work, all under $25.

## Quick Tips

- Change the water every 3-4 days for maximum herb freshness — think of it like flowers in a vase
- Trim the stems at an angle before putting herbs in the keeper, just like you would with cut flowers
- Basil does better on the counter at room temperature than in the fridge — a mason jar with an inch of water works great
- Hardy herbs (rosemary, thyme, oregano) last longer wrapped in a damp paper towel in the fridge than in a water-based keeper
- Keep your herb keeper in the door, not the back of the fridge — the temperature swings in the back can actually damage delicate herbs
