## How Do You Style a Fireplace Mantel?

**The classic mantel rule is odd numbers — three or five objects at varying heights, anchored by something tall in the center or at one end. A clock, a vase grouping, a framed piece, or a garland runner can all anchor the look, with smaller accent pieces filling in the sides.**

Beyond the rule of odds, the most important thing is cohesion. Mixing a modern metal piece with a distressed farmhouse tray reads as random; mixing a distressed wood clock with linen-toned ceramic vases reads as curated. All the sets below are designed with that in mind.

## Quick Tips for Styling Your Mantel

- Start with one anchor piece (clock, large vase, or garland) before adding smaller accents
- Vary heights — short, medium, and tall objects are more interesting than a flat row of similar-sized pieces
- Odd numbers (3 or 5 items) look more curated than even groupings
- Seasonal styling is easy: swap a garland for a wreath, or swap fall tones for spring whites, without replacing the anchor pieces

A styled mantel takes about 20 minutes and lasts for months. With these coordinated sets, the hardest part is choosing one. Found the right look? Pin this so you can come back to it when you're ready to redecorate!
