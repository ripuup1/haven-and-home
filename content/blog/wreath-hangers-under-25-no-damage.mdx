You found the perfect spring wreath — full of faux tulips, fresh greenery, or maybe a cheerful lemon motif — and now comes the part nobody thinks about until it's too late: how to hang it without leaving a scratch, dent, or strip of missing paint on your front door. A wreath hanger that digs into the door frame, pinches the door's edge, or uses adhesive that pulls off the finish is enough to ruin your whole decorating moment.

The good news is that the over-the-door wreath hanger design has been perfected down to a science. These hooks slip over the door from the top, work without any hardware, and the best ones have protective padding that keeps your door completely unmarked. They're also adjustable, which matters more than you'd think — door thicknesses vary, and a hook that's too short will let the door wobble. Here are the best options, all under $25.

## What to Look for in a Wreath Hanger

**The most important feature is the protective coating on the part of the hook that touches your door.** Bare metal hooks will scratch paint and wood, especially if the door moves and the hook shifts. Look for hooks with rubber, silicone, foam, or plastic bumpers on both the door-facing side and the frame-facing side.

Adjustable length is the second thing to prioritize. A hook that's exactly the right depth keeps your wreath centered on the door rather than riding too high or too low. Most doors are 1.375 to 1.75 inches thick, so a hook with an adjustable span of 1 to 2 inches covers the vast majority of front doors. Weight capacity matters for heavier wreaths — look for at least 5 to 10 pounds, which covers most standard seasonal wreaths.

## Quick Tips

- Measure your door thickness before buying — most are between 1.375 and 1.75 in., but storm doors can differ
- For heavier wreaths (over 10 lbs.), use a hook rated for at least 15 to 20 lbs. to avoid slippage
- Clear plastic hooks are the most invisible but can crack in extreme cold — store them inside in winter
- Place a folded felt pad between the hook and door edge for an extra layer of protection if your door has a delicate finish
- If your wreath keeps tilting, use a small piece of ribbon tied from the back of the wreath to the hook to keep it centered
