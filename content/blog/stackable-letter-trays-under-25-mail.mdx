Mail is insidious. It arrives daily, it piles invisibly, and then one afternoon you realize there's a six-inch stack of paper on your counter that you've been shoving aside for three weeks. The fix isn't a complex filing system — it's a landing zone. A place where mail and paper go the moment they come through the door, sorted by whether they need action or can wait.

Stackable letter trays are the most direct solution: one tier for incoming mail, one for bills to pay, one for things to file. At under $25 for most of these, the return on investment is enormous. Here are 6 worth organizing around.

## What Makes a Good Stackable Letter Tray?

**Two things: stability when stacked and easy access from the front. A tray that tips when you pull a paper out defeats the purpose. And a tray with sides that are too tall makes it hard to grab letters from the middle of the stack without emptying the whole thing first.**

Material matters for matching your space. Wire mesh trays suit home offices and workspaces; bamboo and acrylic trays suit countertops where aesthetics matter as much as function. The six picks below cover all the main materials and styles.

## Quick Tips for Taming Mail Clutter

- Label each tier so everyone in the household uses them correctly: Incoming, To Pay, To File
- Process mail the same day it arrives — spend 2 minutes sorting it into trays instead of making a pile
- Once a week, empty the To File tray into your actual filing system so it doesn't become a permanent holding zone
- If paper keeps escaping the trays, add a fourth tier: Recycle or Shred, so you have a destination for junk mail immediately

The difference between a cluttered counter and a clear one is almost always just a landing zone for paper.
