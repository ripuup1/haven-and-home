## How Much Weight Can a Door Handle?

**Most interior cabinet doors are hollow-core, which means they're lighter than they look and have limits.** The door itself can handle the weight of a well-designed organizer, but the hinges are the real constraint. For a single-door pantry or cabinet with two hinges, a fully loaded spice rack with 20 to 30 small bottles is generally fine. Start to worry when you're adding heavy cans or glass bottles — those add up quickly. Lighter items like foil boxes, bags, and small packages are always safe.

If your cabinet door has a tendency to swing shut on its own, the organizer will add to the door weight and may accelerate that tendency — a simple bumper pad on the cabinet frame can fix this.

## Quick Tips for Cabinet Door Organization

- **Measure your door clearance first** — the most common issue is the organizer bumping into the shelf inside the cabinet when the door closes; measure from the inside of the door to the closest shelf edge before buying.
- **Load heaviest items at the bottom** — putting heavy bottles on lower tiers lowers the center of gravity and reduces stress on the door hinges.
- **Group by use** — spices for cooking near the stove, baking supplies near the mixer, cleaning supplies under the sink rather than mixing categories.
- **Add a non-slip mat on each tier** — a strip of non-slip liner cut to fit each basket prevents small bottles from rattling and tipping when the door swings.
- **Check hinge tightness** after a few weeks of heavy use — door hinges can work loose under added weight; a quick tighten with a screwdriver keeps the door hanging straight.
