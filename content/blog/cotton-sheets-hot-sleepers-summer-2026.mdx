Ever notice how the second the temperature outside hits 75 degrees, your bed turns into a sauna and you wake up at 3 a.m. peeling the comforter off your legs? You're not imagining it. The same sheets that felt fine in February are working against you in June, and the difference often isn't your bedroom's air conditioning. It's the fabric.

Most "all-season" cotton sheets are actually winter sheets that you can also use in spring. Real summer sheets, the kind that genuinely help hot sleepers, have a specific weave (percale, not sateen), a specific thread count range (200-400, not 600+), and a specific cotton type (long-staple, not generic). I've been buying and testing summer sheets for the last three summers and these are the eight under-$80 sets that genuinely cool down hot-sleeper bedrooms.

## Quick Tips

How to actually keep your bed cool through a hot summer:

- **Wash new sheets before first use.** Cotton percale gets noticeably cooler after the first wash because the residual finishing comes off. Don't sleep on sheets straight out of the package.
- **Skip the dryer sheet on summer sheets.** Dryer sheets coat the fibers and reduce breathability. Use a wool dryer ball instead.
- **Two sets, rotated weekly.** Sheets stay cleaner and cooler when they get a full air-out between uses. Two sets means you're never sleeping on a fully-built-up set.
- **Cotton pillowcases matter as much as cotton sheets.** Most of your direct skin contact is with the pillowcase. A satin or polyester case undoes the cooling effect of percale sheets.
- **Wash on cold and line-dry when possible.** High heat breaks down cotton fibers faster. A lower heat setting and removing them from the dryer slightly damp keeps them cooler longer.
