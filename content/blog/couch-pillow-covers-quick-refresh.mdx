If your couch looks tired but a new sofa isn't in the budget, pillow covers are the move. Seriously — swapping out pillow covers is one of the fastest, cheapest ways to change the whole feel of a room. New pillows with inserts run $30-60 each. A set of covers for the same inserts you already own? Often under $15 for two.

The trick is knowing which ones are worth it. Cheap pillow covers range from "looks exactly like the photo" to "arrived and immediately went into the donate pile." I've been buying, testing, and gifting pillow covers for years, and the ones below have proven themselves in real living rooms — not just styled photography sets.

Most of these come in packs of two or four, which means you can mix textures and colors without breaking the bank. Here's what I'd actually put on my own couch.

## Quick Tips for Couch Pillow Refresh

- **Stick to odd numbers.** Three or five pillows on a standard sofa looks more natural than two or four — odd numbers feel intentional.
- **Mix textures, not necessarily patterns.** Velvet + linen + corduroy in the same neutral palette looks sophisticated without being overwhelming.
- **Scale matters.** Use 20x20 covers as the base and 18x18 as accents, or mix in a 12x20 lumbar for variety.
- **Buy inserts once, covers forever.** Invest in decent inserts (Ikea, Amazon Basics) and rotate covers seasonally for an ever-fresh look.
- **Lighter covers for dark couches, warmer tones for light couches.** Contrast and warmth are your friends.
