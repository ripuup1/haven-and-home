Empty corners are the most awkward problem in a living room. A floor lamp works, but you can only have so many. Furniture doesn't always fit. And most corners end up becoming a dead zone that you just stop looking at. A tall floor vase solves the problem in a way that feels intentional and actually adds visual warmth to the room.

The secret is that floor vases don't need anything in them to look good. A tall vase in a corner with some pampas grass or dried eucalyptus branches creates the kind of effortless, organic look that takes a living room from "fine" to "intentionally styled." Pampas grass in particular is incredibly cheap, lasts for years with zero maintenance, and looks beautiful in basically any style of vase.

Here are seven floor vases that cost under $50 and work in real living rooms — not just staged photo shoots.

## Quick Tips for Styling Floor Vases

- **Pampas grass is your best friend** — It's affordable, lasts for years, and looks beautiful in any vase from narrow-necked to wide-mouthed.
- **Odd numbers work better** — If you're grouping vases, three of different heights always looks more natural than two.
- **The corner trick** — Placing a tall vase in a corner pushes your eye upward and makes the ceiling feel higher.
- **Mix textures** — Combine a smooth ceramic vase with rough dried grasses or branches for contrast that feels intentional.
- **No water required** — Dried botanicals and pampas grass need no maintenance whatsoever. Just dust occasionally.
