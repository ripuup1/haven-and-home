## Quick Tips for Toilet Brush Holders

- **Drip-proof base is not optional.** A holder without a drip-proof reservoir means water from the brush runs onto your floor after every cleaning. Check specifically that any holder you consider has a sealed or reservoir-style base.
- **Covered versus open top depends on aesthetics.** Open-top holders show the brush handle above the rim, which is more visible but often easier to grab. Covered holders are more discreet. Both work; pick based on how much visibility you want.
- **Match your finish to your existing hardware.** If you have chrome faucets, chrome holder. Brushed nickel, brushed nickel. Matte black fixtures, black holder. The toilet brush holder is a bathroom accessory and should be treated as one.
- **Silicone bristles clean better but cost slightly more.** If you clean your toilet regularly, silicone bristles are worth the modest price premium because they actually stay hygienic between uses.
- **Replace the brush annually at minimum.** The most stylish holder does not help if the brush inside is degraded. Keep track of when you replaced it last — a good holder makes it easy to do a quick swap.

The toilet brush is not going anywhere. It might as well not be an eyesore.
