Paper cupcake liners are one of those small recurring expenses that adds up without you noticing. A box of 100 liners costs $4-6, and if you bake weekly, you're going through several boxes a year. Silicone baking cups eliminate that cost entirely. You buy them once, toss them in the dishwasher, and they're ready for the next batch.

Beyond the savings, silicone cups are genuinely better. Cupcakes and muffins release cleanly without sticking or tearing. They don't absorb grease the way paper does, so your baked goods stay moist. And they work in the oven, microwave, and freezer, which makes them useful for far more than just muffins. Here are six sets under $15 that are worth switching to.

## Quick Tips for Silicone Baking Cups

- No need to grease silicone cups, but a light spray helps with very sticky batters like banana bread
- Let cups cool for 5 minutes before removing muffins — the silicone holds heat and peels cleaner when slightly cooled
- Store cups nested inside each other or in a jar to keep them organized
- Silicone cups work great for freezing individual portions of soup, sauce, and baby food
