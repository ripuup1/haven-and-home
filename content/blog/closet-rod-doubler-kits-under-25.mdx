If your closet is packed to the point where you are shoving clothes in sideways and everything comes out wrinkled, the problem is probably not that you own too much. It is that a single closet rod wastes half the vertical space in your closet. Most standard closets have one rod at about 66 inches high, leaving three feet of dead air below your hanging shirts and blouses.

A closet rod doubler adds a second rod below your existing one, instantly doubling your hanging space without any renovation, demolition, or trip to the hardware store. It is the simplest closet upgrade that exists, and it takes about five minutes to set up.

Every option here is under $25, requires no tools or drilling, and works with standard closet rods. If your bedroom closet has been a source of daily frustration, this is the fix.

## Quick Tips for Maximizing Your Closet Doubler

- Hang shorter items like shirts, blouses, and folded pants on the lower rod — save the upper rod for longer items or items you grab less frequently
- Group similar garment lengths together to avoid the lower rod interfering with longer pieces above
- Use slim velvet hangers instead of bulky plastic ones to fit more garments per rod
- Check your closet rod's weight capacity before adding a doubler — the total weight of both rods hangs from the original
- Leave at least 2 inches of clearance between the bottom of upper garments and the top of lower garments to prevent wrinkling
