If you haven't made the switch to velvet hangers yet, I genuinely don't know what you're waiting for. And beyond the space savings, the velvet surface means nothing slides off anymore. No more finding your silky blouses crumpled on the closet floor. No more tank top straps dangling off the edges. Everything stays exactly where you hung it.

## Quick Tips for Switching to Velvet Hangers

- **Do it all at once.** Switching half your closet to velvet and leaving the other half on plastic creates visual chaos. Commit to the full swap -- it takes about 30 minutes and the difference is dramatic.
- **Recycle your old hangers.** Most dry cleaners will take wire hangers back. Plastic hangers can go in your recycling bin in many areas.
- **Match the hook color to your closet hardware.** Most velvet hangers come with chrome hooks, but some offer rose gold or matte black hooks for a more coordinated look.
- **Use the notched shoulders** for tank tops and strappy dresses. That's what they're designed for, and they work perfectly.
- **Don't hang heavy wet items on velvet hangers.** The moisture can damage the flocking over time. Let sweaters and heavy items air dry first, then hang them.

If your closet rod is 48 inches (standard single-rod closet), you can fit about 48 items on standard plastic hangers. Switch to velvet and you can fit 70-80 items on the same rod.
