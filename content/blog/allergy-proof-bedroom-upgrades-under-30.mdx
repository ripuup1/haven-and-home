If you wake up congested every morning and you're not sick, the problem is almost certainly your bedroom. Dust mites, pet dander, mold spores, and pollen all concentrate in the one room you spend a third of your life in. The good news: you don't need to gut your bedroom or buy a $400 air purifier to make a significant difference.

These six upgrades are all under $30, and they address the biggest allergen sources one by one. Start with whatever's causing the most obvious symptoms — the mattress and pillow encasements make the fastest difference for most people.

## What Causes Bedroom Allergies?

**Dust mites are responsible for most year-round bedroom allergy symptoms.** They live in mattresses, pillows, and bedding — feeding on skin cells and thriving in humidity. Encasing your mattress and pillows in allergen-proof covers cuts off their habitat without requiring any chemicals.

Beyond dust mites, pet dander and outdoor pollen track in on clothes and pets. A HEPA air purifier handles airborne particles that encasements can't. Together, these two approaches cover the vast majority of bedroom allergen sources.

## Quick Tips for an Allergy-Proof Bedroom

- Wash your bedding in hot water (130 degrees or higher) every week — that temperature kills dust mites
- Keep bedroom humidity under 50% with a dehumidifier or air conditioner; dust mites can't thrive below that
- Remove carpeting in the bedroom if allergies are severe — hard floors hold far fewer allergens
- Vacuum mattresses every time you change the sheets, even with an encasement on
- Keep pets out of the bedroom entirely — this is the hardest change but the most effective for pet dander
- Run the air purifier 24 hours a day, not just at night, to keep particle levels consistently low
