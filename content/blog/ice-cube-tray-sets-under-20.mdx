Here's a small truth nobody tells you about ice: the shape and size genuinely changes your drink. A standard ice tray makes cubes that melt fast, dilute your cold brew or cocktail in minutes, and never quite fill a highball glass the way you want. Larger cubes and spheres melt slower — which means your bourbon stays at the right strength, your sparkling water stays cold longer, and your drinks just look better.

All of these trays are under $20, and most come with lids (which matters more than you'd think — open trays pick up every smell in your freezer). Here's what's worth buying.

## Why Ice Tray Size and Material Matter

**Silicone is the only material worth buying for ice trays in 2025.** Plastic trays crack, stick, and absorb freezer odors over time. Silicone releases cubes cleanly by twisting or flexing the tray — no more running it under hot water and hoping for the best. BPA-free food-grade silicone is odor-neutral and dishwasher safe, and it lasts years with normal use.**

Sphere vs. cube: spheres have the smallest surface area for their volume, so they melt the most slowly. Large 2 in. cubes are nearly as good and fit more glasses. Standard 1 in. cubes are fine for everyday use. If you drink whiskey, bourbon, or anything you'd pour over ice in a nice glass, spheres or large cubes are a genuine upgrade.

## Quick Tips for Better Ice at Home

- Fill trays 90% full instead of to the brim — water expands when it freezes and overfilled trays spill
- Place filled trays on a flat shelf, not a tilted freezer drawer, for more even-shaped cubes
- Once frozen, transfer cubes to a zip bag to free up the tray and keep ice fresh longer
- Boiling water first and letting it cool slightly can help reduce cloudiness in standard trays
- Silicone trays with lids are the single best upgrade for freezer-odor-free ice
