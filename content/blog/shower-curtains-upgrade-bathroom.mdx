There's a reason every bathroom makeover blog starts with "swap out your shower curtain." It's the largest visual element in most bathrooms, and replacing a dingy plastic curtain with a proper fabric one instantly makes the whole room feel more intentional. The spa-bathroom look you see on Pinterest? Half of that is just a really good shower curtain.

The shift toward fabric shower curtains, especially waffle weave and linen-look textures, has made it easier than ever to get that high-end hotel bathroom feel without renovating anything. You just need the right curtain and a decent liner underneath. Here are the best options across different styles and budgets, with honest notes about water resistance, washing, and whether they actually stay looking good.

## Quick Tips for Shower Curtains

- Always use a liner behind fabric shower curtains, even if they claim to be water-repellent. It extends the curtain's life dramatically.
- Wash fabric shower curtains monthly on a gentle cycle with a small amount of detergent to prevent mildew and keep them looking fresh.
- Replace your liner every 6-12 months, or sooner if you see pink or black mildew spots that won't wash out.
- Leave the curtain and liner spread out (not bunched to one side) after showering to allow airflow and prevent mildew.
- Standard shower curtain size is 72" x 72", but measure your setup before ordering if you have a curved rod or wider shower.
