Outdoor throw pillows are the fastest way to refresh a patio without replacing any furniture. Swap in a set of spring colors — dusty rose, sage green, soft blue, a cheerful stripe — and a patio that looked tired all winter suddenly looks intentional and welcoming. The challenge under $30 is finding pillows or pillow covers that actually survive the season instead of fading, growing mildew, or falling apart at the zipper by June.

The best finds in this category are covers rather than filled pillows, which gives you two advantages: you can bring the inserts inside at night or during heavy rain, and when the covers eventually wear out (as all outdoor fabric does), you can replace just the covers for a fraction of the cost of full pillows. Most 18x18 covers fit standard throw pillow inserts, which means you probably already have some you can repurpose.

## What Makes a Good Outdoor Pillow Cover Under $30?

**The key specs to check on outdoor pillow covers are waterproof rating, UV resistance, and zipper quality. A cover that repels water rather than absorbing it means the insert stays dry even if the cover gets rained on. UV resistance is what keeps the color from fading in three weeks. A heavy-duty zipper that runs the full length of the back makes swapping inserts easy and keeps the cover from blowing off in the wind.**

Outdoor fabric grades vary significantly even within affordable price ranges. Polyester fabrics with a waterproof coating outperform standard polyester for moisture resistance, but the coating can crack over time — particularly in extreme heat. Olefin and solution-dyed acrylic fabrics resist fading much better than printed or dyed polyester, and they do not rely on a coating to be waterproof. At under $30, you will mostly find coated polyester, but the better-reviewed options use thicker material with a more durable coating.

## Quick Tips for Outdoor Throw Pillows

- Buy covers rather than filled pillows — you can bring inserts inside in heavy rain while leaving the covers on, extending the life of your inserts
- Standard 18x18 inserts are widely available and fit most 18x18 covers, but measure your existing inserts before ordering
- Mixing patterns works well outdoors: combine one floral, one stripe, and one solid in the same color family for a curated look
- Wash covers at the start of each season to remove any mildew spores that developed over winter storage
- Store covers in a mesh bag rather than sealed plastic during the off-season — they need to breathe to prevent mustiness
