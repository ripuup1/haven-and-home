A good towel bar solves all of it. Mount it on the wall, hang it over a cabinet door, or stick it to the side of your fridge, and suddenly your towels are actually where you need them without claiming any counter real estate.

The good news is that functional kitchen towel bars are genuinely affordable. We found nine options under $20, covering wall-mounted bars, over-cabinet-door styles, and magnetic options for fridges. ## Where Should You Put a Kitchen Towel Bar?

**The best place for a kitchen towel bar is wherever you actually reach for a towel most — usually near the sink, the stove, or the prep area.** A towel bar mounted in a spot that's inconvenient will never be used, and you'll be back to draping towels over the oven handle within a week.

Wall-mounted bars work well if you have a tile backsplash or a solid wall near the sink. Over-cabinet-door hooks are ideal for renters who can't drill holes, and they work especially well on the cabinet under the sink. Magnetic bars attach to the side of the refrigerator without any installation at all.

## Quick Tips for Kitchen Towel Bar Setup

- Clean the surface with rubbing alcohol before attaching any adhesive or suction mount — it dramatically improves staying power
- For renters using command strips or adhesive, always check the weight rating before hanging anything heavier than a single towel
- If you keep a dish towel and a hand towel, mounting two separate bars side by side in the same spot keeps things tidy
- Replace kitchen towels every two to three days; a towel bar is only as hygienic as the towel on it
