## Best Rattan Basket with Handles

**A Rattan Storage Basket with Handles ($35, 4.4 stars, 7,200+ reviews) brings a slightly more structured, vintage feel than seagrass. Rattan is a stronger, more rigid natural material that holds its shape permanently -- these baskets look exactly the same after years of daily use. The arched handles add a beautiful design detail.**

Rattan and seagrass get confused a lot, but they're quite different. Rattan is a solid vine (think: the material used in classic furniture), while seagrass is a grass. This means rattan is significantly sturdier and more rigid -- a rattan basket won't flex or compress when you push against it. It holds its shape like furniture, not like a bag.

The arched handles on this particular basket are both functional and decorative. They're woven from the same rattan as the body, creating a seamless look that feels handcrafted. They're tall enough to grip comfortably even when the basket is full, and strong enough to carry it loaded with books or toys.

Rattan does tend to look a bit more "tropical" or "vintage" than seagrass, which leans more neutral. If your living room has any rattan or wicker furniture already (a chair, a side table, a lamp base), matching baskets tie the whole look together beautifully.

## Quick Tips for Styling Storage Baskets

- **The one-third rule:** fill baskets about one-third full for styled photos and displays, but in real life, fill them however full they need to be -- that's what they're for
- **Roll blankets instead of folding them** before placing in baskets -- rolls look tidier and let you fit more in the same space
- **Group baskets in odd numbers** (1 or 3) for a more visually appealing arrangement than pairs of 2 or 4
- **Mix materials but keep tones similar** -- a seagrass basket next to a rattan basket looks intentional if they're both in the brown-to-honey range
- **Use basket liners** (even a pillowcase works) if you're storing small items that might poke through the weave
- **Place your largest basket on the floor** and smaller ones on shelves -- this creates visual balance and prevents shelves from looking top-heavy
