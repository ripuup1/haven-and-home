## Quick Tips for Getting the Best Blackout Results

- Hang your rod as high as possible — ideally within 1 to 2 inches of the ceiling. This eliminates the gap at the top where most light enters.
- Extend the rod 4 to 6 inches past each side of the window frame so the panels cover the gap where light sneaks around the edges when panels are closed.
- For windows that still let in light at the edges, use a tension rod inside the window frame with a small blackout panel as a light-blocking liner behind the main curtain.
- If you have two windows side by side, one long rod spanning both with multiple panels gives better coverage than two separate rods with separate panels.
