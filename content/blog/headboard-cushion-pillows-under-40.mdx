Sitting up in bed to read, watch something on a laptop, or work should not end in a sore lower back. A regular pillow propped behind you sounds like a solution until it collapses sideways every three minutes. A headboard cushion pillow — the wedge-shaped backrest style — is a different category entirely. It holds its shape, supports the lumbar curve, and often has the aesthetic of a proper bolster pillow rather than a shapeless foam block.

The tricky part is that headboard pillows come in three distinct designs that serve different purposes. True wedge shapes (triangular cross-section) give the most consistent back support. Bolster-style cushions look more decorative but often do not have the lumbar support of a true wedge. And padded headboard panels mount to the wall or sit against the headboard to cushion it. This roundup focuses on the standalone bolster and wedge types you can use on any bed.

One thing to look for: removable, washable covers. The fabric that is closest to your back when you sit up picks up body oils and needs washing. Any headboard pillow worth buying at this price should have a zippered cover that comes off.

## Quick Tips for Using Headboard Cushion Pillows

- Position the wedge so the thick end is at the bottom — you want the support under your lower back, not your shoulders
- If the pillow slides down during use, tuck the bottom edge under your mattress topper for a few inches to anchor it
- Wash the removable cover monthly if you use the backrest daily — it picks up body oils quickly
- For couples with different reading postures, a single king-width bolster spanning the whole bed is easier than two separate units
- A headboard cushion also works great on a couch, reading chair, or the floor for movie night floor seating

Reading in bed should be genuinely comfortable, not a negotiation between your lower back and a stack of pillows that keeps collapsing. Any of these options will change that experience for under $40.
