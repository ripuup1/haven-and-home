## Quick Tips for String Light Success

- **Use cup hooks, not nails.** Removable adhesive cup hooks on balcony rails and walls let you run string lights without drilling anything. Renter-friendly and repositionable.
- **Drape over a taut line, not a sagging one.** A taught paracord or clothes line run at the height you want creates a clean anchor for the lights. Sagging lines make the whole setup look cheap.
- **Warm white (2700K-3000K) for everyday; daylight only for task areas.** Cool white string lights look harsh at night and feel sterile. Stay in the warm range for any space where you're relaxing.
- **One primary strand, one accent layer.** Edison bulbs or G40s as the main ceiling source, copper fairy lights or solar accents as secondary. The layered effect looks more expensive than either does alone.
- **Smart plug + timer.** A $10 smart plug on any of the non-solar sets lets you set a schedule from your phone and never think about it again.

A balcony with warm globe lights, a stripe rug, and two comfortable chairs is a genuinely nice place to be at 9pm. String lights are the part of that equation that cost $25 and do the most work.
