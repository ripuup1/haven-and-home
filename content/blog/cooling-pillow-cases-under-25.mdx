## What Material Actually Keeps You Coolest?

**Bamboo-derived rayon (also labeled bamboo viscose) consistently performs best in real-world use for hot sleepers. It's breathable, moisture-wicking, and has a naturally smooth texture that feels cool against skin without the synthetic slipperiness of some microfiber options.**

Tencel (lyocell) is a close second — it's also plant-derived, extremely soft, and excellent at temperature regulation. Cotton percale is cooler than sateen cotton but isn't in the same league as bamboo or Tencel for truly hot sleepers. If you're waking up sweating, upgrade to bamboo or Tencel — it makes a real difference.

## Quick Tips for Cooler Sleep on Hot Nights

- Layer your bed with breathable linen or cotton percale sheets alongside your cooling pillowcases — the combination is more effective than any single cooling product
- Keep a small fan positioned to blow across your pillow rather than at your face directly — this maximizes airflow at the pillow surface
- Wash cooling pillowcases every 5-7 days in summer months, since buildup of body oils reduces the moisture-wicking effectiveness
- If you can only buy one cooling item for your bed, start with pillowcases — your head and neck produce the most heat and are the first to feel it
- Cooling pillowcases paired with a cool-water face wash right before bed is a surprisingly effective combo for hot sleepers

Good sleep is worth investing in, even just a little. Pin your favorite cooling pillowcase for when it's time to upgrade your bedding — your future well-rested self will absolutely thank you.
