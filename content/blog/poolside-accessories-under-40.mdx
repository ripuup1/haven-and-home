## Quick Tips for a Tidier Pool Area

- **Make it easy to put things away.** The closer storage is to the water, the more likely kids will actually use it. Place organizers within arm's reach of the pool steps.
- **Label bins by type.** Noodles in one bin, floats in another, water guns and small toys in a third. Labels mean everyone knows where things go without asking.
- **Rinse before you store.** A quick rinse of floats and toys before putting them away prevents algae, chlorine smell, and mildew from building up in the storage container.
- **Use hooks for swimsuits and goggles.** A simple outdoor hook rack near the pool entrance gives each family member a spot for their gear that isn't a pile on the concrete.
- **Cover the pool float storage overnight.** Dew and overnight humidity can leave stored floats damp. A breathable cover or bringing bins under a covered area extends the life of everything.

Summer pool days should feel effortless and fun — not like a cleanup project. A few smart storage pieces pay for themselves the first weekend in how much simpler the whole experience becomes.
