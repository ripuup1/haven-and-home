## What Makes a Good Curtain Tieback?

**The best tiebacks hold their shape, do not stretch out over time, and look deliberate rather than improvised.**

A lot of cheap tiebacks feel flimsy the moment you hold them, or they fray after a few months of daily use. The ones worth buying have some weight to them, use decent hardware, and come with everything you need to install them — including the wall hooks. Here are eight sets that actually deliver.

## Quick Tips for Curtain Tiebacks

- Hang tiebacks at about one-third of the way down from the top of the curtain for the most flattering look
- For a more dramatic pooled look, hang tiebacks lower — about halfway down the panel
- If you want maximum light, pull curtains back as far as the window frame will allow before securing the tieback
- Magnetic tiebacks work best on curtain panels that are not too thick — test with blackout curtains before committing
- Mix textures: rope tiebacks can add warmth to curtains that otherwise feel flat or corporate
