You know the drill. It's 2am, you're half-asleep, and you're trying to navigate to the bathroom without waking your partner or stubbing your toe on the doorframe. Turning on the overhead light is basically torture on your eyes — and then you can't fall back asleep. There's a better way.

Motion-sensor night lights are one of those small home upgrades that sounds minor until you actually have one. They plug directly into any standard outlet, sense when you walk by, and cast just enough light to see where you're going — no switches, no fumbling, no eye-searing brightness. And the best part? You can grab a solid one for well under $15. Here are six worth putting in your cart right now.

## Do Motion-Sensor Night Lights Actually Save on Electricity?

**Yes — dramatically. Most LED motion night lights use 0.5 to 1.5 watts, compared to 60+ watts for a standard overhead bulb.** Because they only turn on when triggered and shut off automatically after 30-90 seconds, your monthly impact is basically nothing. Some cost less than $0.50/year to run.

The real magic is the dual sensor most of them use: a motion sensor (PIR) plus a light sensor (dusk-to-dawn). That means they stay completely off during the day and only activate when it's dark and someone walks by. Set it, forget it, and let it do its thing.

## Quick Tips for Night Light Placement

- Put one at the start of each route you walk at night: bedroom door, bathroom entrance, hallway junctions
- Plug into the outlet closest to the floor for the most useful coverage — lights near the ground illuminate your path without hitting your eyes
- If your outlet is behind a door that swings open, use a rechargeable stick-on option instead so the sensor isn't blocked
- For bathrooms, place it near the entrance rather than inside the shower area — the steam can affect the sensor over time
