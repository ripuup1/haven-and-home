## Quick Tips for Adult Easter Egg Decorating

- **Bring eggs to room temperature before dyeing.** Cold eggs straight from the fridge can cause condensation that interferes with dye absorption — let them sit out for 30 minutes first.
- **Use white eggs for the most vivid color.** Brown eggs produce more muted, earthy tones, which is beautiful for natural dye kits but may disappoint with bright kits.
- **Dry eggs in an egg carton.** Set dyed eggs in an empty egg carton to dry so they do not roll and smear.
- **Add white vinegar to the dye bath.** Even if your kit does not call for it, a splash of white vinegar helps the dye bond more intensely to the shell.
- **Display immediately.** Dyed and decorated eggs look stunning in a clear glass bowl, on a tiered tray, or nestled in a basket with moss and spring greenery. Do not hide your work in the fridge.
