The fix is genuinely cheap, and once you put pads on, you stop thinking about it entirely.

The best felt pads cost less than a cup of coffee per chair. The tricky part is finding ones that actually stay on and don't flatten out to useless discs after two weeks. Here are 6 that deliver on both counts.

## What Makes a Felt Pad Actually Worth Buying?

**The most important thing isn't the felt — it's the adhesive. Cheap pads fall off within days, leaving sticky residue on your chair legs and zero protection for your floors. Quality pads use industrial-strength adhesive that grips the furniture leg and stays put for years.**

Thickness matters too. Thin pads (under 3mm) compress quickly and stop protecting. Look for pads at least 5mm thick — thick enough that there's still cushion left after a year of regular use. The picks below hit that mark.

## Quick Tips for Applying Felt Pads

- Clean furniture legs with rubbing alcohol before applying — oil and dust prevent adhesive from bonding properly
- Press firmly for 30 seconds after applying, then wait 24 hours before moving the furniture
- Replace pads every 1-2 years on high-use chairs; they compress and lose effectiveness over time
- For chair legs that splay outward, use round pads rather than square ones so the contact is even
