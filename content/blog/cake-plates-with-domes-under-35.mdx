Before picking, consider where this will live permanently. A glass dome on a white Shaker kitchen reads differently than a wood pedestal in a farmhouse kitchen with open shelving. Neither is wrong — you just want the stand to feel like it belongs.

## Quick Tips

- A cake dome on an empty stand still looks intentional — store a candle, small succulents, or a stack of macarons under it when you don't have cake
- Wipe the inside of a glass dome with a tiny bit of white vinegar to keep it streak-free and crystal clear
- Acacia and other porous wood stands should not go in the dishwasher — hand wash only, or the wood will crack
- If your dome doesn't fit snugly, a thin strip of rubber weatherstripping around the base of the dome creates a perfect seal
- The most versatile pick for most kitchens: a clear glass dome on a white ceramic base — works with everything and photographs cleanly

You don't need a $90 stand from a specialty shop. Any of these seven will hold a cake just as well, look just as good on the counter, and leave the rest of your budget for the actual baking.
