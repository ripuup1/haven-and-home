## What's the Best All-Purpose Cookie Cutter Set for Beginners?

**The best all-purpose set for beginners is a classic stainless steel nested circle and shape set. Clean cuts, easy release, dishwasher safe, and the shapes work for everything from simple rounds to holidays.**

The appeal of stainless steel over plastic is that the edges don't flex under pressure, which means you get a cleaner cut edge on your cookie. Plastic cutters warp over time and soften in hot water, which leads to blurry cuts and shapes that don't hold. Stainless sets cost a couple of dollars more upfront but last for years.

## Quick Tips for Getting Better Results with Cookie Cutters

**Chill the dough before cutting.** Room-temperature dough spreads when cut, blurring the edges. Refrigerate for at least 30 minutes before cutting, and work in batches returning the dough to the fridge between rounds.

**Flour or freeze your cutters.** Dip the cutter edge in flour before each press to prevent sticking, or run cold water over it. For very soft dough, a quick blast in the freezer for 10 minutes firms up the cutter and reduces sticking.

**Cut before you bake, not after.** Sounds obvious, but cut on the baking sheet when possible so you're not lifting and moving cut shapes, which distorts them.

**Offset spatulas are your friend.** Use a thin offset spatula to lift cut cookies from the work surface without stretching them. This is especially important for soft butter cookie dough.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What material is best for cookie cutters?

Stainless steel is the best material for cookie cutters. It holds its shape, cuts cleanly without flexing, doesn't warp in hot water, and lasts for years. Plastic cutters are cheaper but flex under pressure and warp over time.

### How do I prevent cookie cutters from rusting?

Dry stainless steel cookie cutters immediately after washing instead of letting them air dry. Moisture trapped in the folded edges causes rust. Most stainless cutters are dishwasher safe but hand washing and prompt drying extends their life.

### Can cookie cutters be used for fondant?

Yes. Cookie cutters work well for cutting fondant, gum paste, and modeling chocolate. Use the same dipping-in-cornstarch technique instead of flour to prevent sticking.
