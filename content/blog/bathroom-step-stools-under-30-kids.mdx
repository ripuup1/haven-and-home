Teaching kids to wash their hands independently sounds simple until you realize your bathroom sink is essentially at shoulder height for a three-year-old. A good step stool removes that barrier entirely — and once they can reach the sink on their own, they actually do it. Less chasing, less "did you wash your hands?!", more independence.

The trick is finding a step stool that's the right height (not too short, not wobbly), has serious non-slip feet and surface texture, and is sturdy enough that you're not going to have a kid tipping over mid-handwash. After going through the most-reviewed options on Amazon, here are six that actually deliver.

## The Height Question: One Step vs. Two Steps

**Most toddlers (18 months to 3 years) need about 7–9 inches of height** to comfortably reach the average bathroom sink. A single-step stool in that height range works fine. Once they're 4 and up and using the toilet independently, a two-step stool is more useful — gives them enough height for the sink AND they can use it as a toilet training step.

If you're buying for a kid right in the 2–3 year range, look for adjustable-height options. A few brands have figured out how to make step stools with removable steps so they grow with the kid.

## Quick Tips for Choosing the Right Step Stool

- Measure from the floor to the top of your sink — you need the stool to make up the difference
- Non-slip feet are non-negotiable; cheap stools slide on tile and that's how accidents happen
- Look for a textured stepping surface, not a smooth plastic platform
- If you have multiple kids, get two — one for each bathroom beats fighting over the same stool daily
