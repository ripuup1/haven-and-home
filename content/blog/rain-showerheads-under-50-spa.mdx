## Quick Tips for Installing a Rain Showerhead

- **Wrap the shower arm threads with Teflon tape** (3-4 wraps clockwise) before screwing on the new head to prevent leaks -- this is the step most people skip and then wonder why it drips
- **Hand-tighten first, then use a wrench** with a cloth over the connection to avoid scratching the finish -- you only need about a quarter turn past hand-tight
- **Clean silicone nozzles monthly** by wiping with your finger or a soft brush to prevent mineral buildup from reducing water flow
- **If your rain head hangs too low**, buy a shower arm extension (usually $10-15) -- it adds 6-10 inches of height and changes the angle to straight down
- **Soak the showerhead in white vinegar overnight** once every few months to dissolve hard water deposits -- put it in a plastic bag tied around the arm if you don't want to remove it
- **Test your water pressure before buying** by timing how long it takes to fill a 1-gallon bucket at full blast -- if it takes over 20 seconds, go with the high-pressure model
