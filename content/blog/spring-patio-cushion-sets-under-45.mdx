## What Should You Look for in an Outdoor Cushion Set?

**The three things that separate durable outdoor cushions from cheap ones are fabric type, fill density, and tie attachments. Olefin and polyester fabrics with UV treatment resist fading best; high-density polyester fill keeps its shape instead of going flat; and sewn-through ties hold cushions firmly on chairs without bunching or slipping off.**

Price matters less than material specs in this category. A $30 set with olefin fabric will outlast a $45 set made from untreated polyester by a full season or more. Check the product listing for mentions of UV resistance or fade resistance — those are the keywords that separate outdoor-rated fabric from basic fabric that just happens to be waterproof.

## Quick Tips for Patio Cushions

- Store cushions in a deck box or storage bag during extended rainy periods — even water-resistant cushions benefit from not sitting saturated for days at a time
- Bring cushions inside over winter if you are in a freeze-thaw climate — frozen wet fill breaks down faster than anything else
- Vacuum cushions periodically to remove pollen, debris, and dust before it works into the fabric
- If covers are removable, wash them once early in the season to remove any storage odors before guests see your patio
- Mix toss pillows in a complementary color to make a basic cushion set look more designed
