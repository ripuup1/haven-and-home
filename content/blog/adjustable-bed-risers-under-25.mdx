Under-bed storage is the most underutilized space in most bedrooms. Think about it: there's a whole rectangle of floor space under your mattress that most of us either ignore entirely or cram with random bins that don't fit properly. The problem is usually height — standard bed frames sit too low to slide anything meaningful underneath.

Bed risers solve this completely. A good set adds 3 to 8 inches of clearance, which is enough room for flat storage bins, seasonal clothing bags, extra bedding, or shoe organizers. The best part is that adjustable risers let you choose your height, so you're not locked into one option. And at under $25 for a full set? This is one of the best storage-per-dollar upgrades you can make in your bedroom.

## How Much Height Do You Actually Need?

**Three inches works for flat bins and vacuum bags. Five to six inches opens up most under-bed storage containers. Eight inches is ideal if you want to slide full-size storage boxes with lids underneath.**

Before buying, measure the clearance between your bed frame and floor — most frames already have 2 to 4 inches. Add your desired riser height to that for total clearance. Keep in mind that stackable risers let you start at a lower height and add more over time, which is a nice flexibility feature. Also check your bed frame type — most risers fit standard legs, but platform beds without legs won't work with risers at all.

## Quick Tips for Using Bed Risers

- Center each riser under the leg carefully — if the leg is even slightly off-center, the riser can shift under weight
- Add felt pads under the riser on hardwood floors to protect the finish, even if the riser claims to be non-slip
- Once your bed is lifted, measure the actual clearance before buying storage containers — don't assume
- Vacuum bags for bedding and seasonal clothing are the best use of the new space — they compress flat and store a surprising amount
