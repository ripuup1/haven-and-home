When's the last time you took a bath without balancing your phone on the edge of the tub? If the answer is "never," a bathtub caddy might be the single most useful bathroom upgrade you're not using. The right one holds your book flat, keeps your glass upright, and stops you from getting out of the water every ten minutes. The wrong one wobbles, slides, or collapses when you actually put weight on it.

Below are seven of the best bath caddies on the market right now, ranked by use case. Whether you're a book-in-the-tub person, a wine-and-candles person, or someone who just wants their phone within reach, there's a pick here for you.

## Quick Tips

- Measure your tub width before buying — most extendable caddies max out around 43 inches, which won't work on larger soaking tubs.
- Dry the caddy after each use if it's bamboo — especially the underside where it contacts the tub rim.
- Teak requires occasional oiling (every 3–6 months) to maintain its natural water resistance.
- If you want to use a phone, get one with a slot, not just a flat surface — vibration notifications will slowly walk it toward the water.
- Store candles on the caddy only if there's a raised edge — a flat surface is a fire risk as the wax gets soft.

A bath without a caddy is just soaking in hot water. With the right one, it's an hour that actually recharges you. Start with the bamboo extendable if you want the safest, most versatile pick.
