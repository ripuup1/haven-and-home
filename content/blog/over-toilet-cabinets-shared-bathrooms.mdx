If two (or four) people share a bathroom, you already know the math: one medicine cabinet, zero counter space, and a toilet tank covered in everyone's stuff. The wall above the toilet is wasted square footage most people never claim.

An over-the-toilet cabinet fixes it in an afternoon. Each person gets their own shelf, the counter stays clear, and nobody has to dig through a shared basket for a toothbrush at 6 a.m. Here are the seven that actually hold up when a whole household uses them daily.

## The Best Over-the-Toilet Cabinets, Ranked

**For most shared bathrooms, a 3-tier freestanding metal unit with a mix of open shelves and closed doors wins — you get visible storage for everyday items and hidden storage for the stuff nobody needs to see.**

I weighted this list toward pieces that (a) assemble in under an hour, (b) don't require drilling into tile, and (c) have the footprint to fit a standard 16-in. tank without blocking the flush lever. Prices range from about $40 to $75.

## How to Choose

- **Shared by 3+ people?** Get the 7-tier drawer unit — enclosed storage matters more than you think
- **Renting?** Anything freestanding. Avoid the Forbena floating shelves unless you're staying a while
- **Tight layout?** Measure before buying. The Glotika and bamboo shelf have the smallest footprints
- **Want it to look like furniture?** Bamboo or ladder options read warmer than matte-black metal
- **Low ceiling or window above toilet?** Ladder shelf next to the toilet instead of over it

Once everyone has their own shelf, the morning rush stops being a contact sport. Found one you love? Pin this for later so you don't lose it!
