There's a specific failure mode for cheap beach bags. You haul everything down to the sand on Saturday, the bag does fine. By Sunday afternoon, the seams on the bottom are pulling apart, there's a permanent damp patch from a wet swimsuit, and sand is now living in your car forever. The bag did not survive the weekend.

A good beach bag isn't about looks — it's about whether it can take wet sandy chaos for two full days without falling apart. Below are eight bags under $40 that actually pass that test, plus a quick guide on what features matter most depending on how you use the beach. None of these are precious. All of them you can hose off and use again next weekend.

## What to Look For in a Beach Bag

Before the picks, here's the short list of features that separate a beach bag that lasts a season from one that lasts a weekend:

- **Drainage** — either mesh sides or grommets at the bottom so wet sand and seawater can escape. Without it, you're carrying a swamp home.
- **Mesh vs canvas** — mesh is best for wet stuff and rinses clean; canvas looks better and holds shape but stains and stays damp.
- **Size** — for a family weekend, look for at least 18 inches wide and 14 inches tall. Day trip alone? 14 by 12 is plenty.
- **Waterproof inner pocket** — for phone, keys, and wallet. Non-negotiable.
- **Reinforced handles** — bags fail at the handle stitching first. Look for double-stitched or riveted handles, especially if you'll load it with a cooler.

## How to Choose

Pick based on the trip you take most often, not the trip you take twice a summer. If you're a once-a-week solo beach reader, the budget canvas tote is plenty. If you're hauling family gear every weekend, spend the $32 on the seven-pocket family bag and never think about it again.

A few non-obvious tips:

- Light colors hide sand better than dark colors. A black bag shows every grain.
- Test the handle stitching by pulling firmly side-to-side before your first trip. Cheap stitching reveals itself in the showroom, not on the beach.
- Always keep one waterproof zip pouch inside whichever bag you pick — phone, keys, wallet. Even the best beach bag is not a dry bag.
- Hose the bag down at the end of every weekend. Salt water + sand + canvas = mildew within a week if you skip this step.

The bags that survive sandy weekends aren't the most expensive ones. They're the ones with mesh bottoms, reinforced handles, and a waterproof pocket — and there are eight of them under $40.
