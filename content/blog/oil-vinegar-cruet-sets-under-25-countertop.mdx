## Why a Dedicated Oil Bottle Beats the Original Bottle Every Time

**The main issue with storing oil in its original container is mess and oxidation.** Most commercial olive oil bottles have poor spouts that drip, and keeping the bottle on the counter exposes the oil to light, which degrades it faster. A proper cruet with a tight-fitting stopper or self-closing spout controls both problems at once.

A good cruet set also just makes cooking feel a little more intentional. Having oil and vinegar within easy reach — in containers that are easy to pour precisely — actually changes how you cook. You reach for them more, which means fewer bland meals and more finished-dish drizzles.

## Quick Tips for Oil and Vinegar Storage

- Store oil away from the stove — the heat and humidity from cooking will degrade it faster than anything else
- Choose a bottle size that you'll empty within 30-60 days; smaller is often better for freshness
- If your counter gets direct sunlight, go for a ceramic or dark-tinted bottle over clear glass
- Label your bottles if you keep multiple oils — it saves a lot of tasting and guessing mid-recipe
