## Simmer Pot Sachets

Simmer pot sachets are the old-school method that's making a comeback. Drop one in a pot of water on the stove, bring it to a low simmer, and your kitchen fills with the smell of citrus, cinnamon, rosemary, or whatever blend you picked. It's like making your own Yankee Candle but better. These sachets come pre-mixed so you don't have to buy individual ingredients. Each one lasts about 2 to 3 simmering sessions (just add water and reheat). The downside is you can't leave the stove unattended, so it's best for when you're already in the kitchen cooking or cleaning.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is the cheapest way to make your home smell good?

The cheapest option is a simmer pot sachet at $14 for a variety pack of 6 blends. Each sachet lasts 2-3 simmering sessions, giving you weeks of fragrance for about $2 per use. Linen spray at $12 is another affordable option that lasts about 2 months.

### How long do reed diffusers last?

A good reed diffuser lasts about 90 days (3 months). This glass reed diffuser set costs $18 and works continuously without electricity or flames. Flip the reeds once a week to refresh the scent throw, and replace just the reeds if the scent fades before the oil runs out.

### Are essential oil diffusers better than candles?

Essential oil diffusers are safer (no flame), more customizable, and more cost-effective long-term than candles. This ceramic diffuser ($24) runs for 6 hours per fill and uses only 3-4 drops of oil per session. One bottle of essential oil lasts months compared to a candle that burns away.

### What essential oils make your house smell like a hotel?

Eucalyptus, lavender, and lemongrass are the key scents for a hotel-like home. Use eucalyptus in bathrooms, lavender in bedrooms, and lemon or lemongrass in kitchens. This essential oil diffuser set ($24) and a variety of oils give you full control over the intensity and blend.

### How do you layer home scents without them clashing?

Use one scent family throughout your home -- either all clean/fresh or all warm/woodsy. Layer a reed diffuser ($18) for constant background scent, linen spray ($12) on fabrics, and a wax warmer ($20) or diffuser ($24) for on-demand fragrance. The total cost for all five products is under $90.
